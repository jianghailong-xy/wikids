In English we use three little words to say what something **is** or who someone **is**: **am**, **is**, and **are**. Let's learn them with our friend Lisa.

## Meet Lisa

Lisa says hello! Read what she tells you about herself:

- "My name **is** Lisa."
- "I **am** 22."
- "I **am** American. I **am** from Chicago."
- "I **am** a student."
- "My favourite colour **is** blue."
- "My favourite sports **are** football and swimming."
- "I **am** interested in art."
- "I **am not** interested in politics."

<Callout type="tip" title="Did you spot them?">
  Find every **am**, **is**, and **are** in Lisa's sentences. How many did you count?
</Callout>

## The big rule

Pick the right word for the person you are talking about:

- **I → am** &nbsp; (I **am** happy.)
- **he / she / it → is** &nbsp; (She **is** my friend.)
- **we / you / they → are** &nbsp; (They **are** at school.)

## Saying "no" — the negative

To say **no**, we add **not** after am / is / are.

- I **am not** late. → **I'm not** late.
- He **is not** here. → **He isn't** here. (or **He's not** here.)
- They **are not** tired. → **They aren't** tired. (or **They're not** tired.)

<Callout type="info" title="Two ways are okay">
  Both **isn't** and **'s not** mean the same thing. **Aren't** and **'re not** are the same too. Pick the one that feels easy to say!
</Callout>

## Three special little words

Look at these short forms — they are very common:

- **that's** = that is &nbsp;→&nbsp; "Thank you. **That's** very kind of you."
- **there's** = there is &nbsp;→&nbsp; "Look! **There's** Chris."
- **here's** = here is &nbsp;→&nbsp; "**Here's** your key."
