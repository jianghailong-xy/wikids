## The big rule

<Animated variant="fadeUp">

Pick the right word for the person you are talking about:

</Animated>

<Stagger delay={0.15} className="my-4 space-y-2 text-slate-800">
  <span>👤 <strong>I → am</strong> &nbsp; (I <strong>am</strong> happy.)</span>
  <span>👧 <strong>he / she / it → is</strong> &nbsp; (She <strong>is</strong> my friend.)</span>
  <span>👨‍👩‍👧 <strong>we / you / they → are</strong> &nbsp; (They <strong>are</strong> at school.)</span>
</Stagger>

## Saying "no" — the negative

<Animated variant="slideRight">

To say **no**, we add **not** after am / is / are.

</Animated>

<Stagger delay={0.12} className="my-4 space-y-2 text-slate-800">
  <span>I <strong>am not</strong> late. → <strong>I'm not</strong> late.</span>
  <span>He <strong>is not</strong> here. → <strong>He isn't</strong> here. (or <strong>He's not</strong> here.)</span>
  <span>They <strong>are not</strong> tired. → <strong>They aren't</strong> tired. (or <strong>They're not</strong> tired.)</span>
</Stagger>

<Animated variant="slideLeft">
  <Callout type="info" title="Two ways are okay">
    Both **isn't** and **'s not** mean the same thing. **Aren't** and **'re not** are the same too. Pick the one that feels easy to say!
  </Callout>
</Animated>
