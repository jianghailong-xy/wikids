In Unit 99 you met *if* for **possible** futures: *If we go by bus, it'll be cheaper.* Today's *if* is different — it's the **dream / wish / not-real** *if*:

> Dan likes fast cars, but he doesn't have one. <br />
> **If he had the money, he would buy a fast car.**

Notice two strange-looking things:

- The *if*-half uses **had** (a past form) — but the meaning is **now**, not yesterday.
- The other half uses **would / wouldn't / could / couldn't** — never *will*.

That's because the situation is **not real**: *he doesn't have the money*. We're imagining what would happen **if it were true**.

Dan can't afford a Ferrari, so he's sitting at his desk dreaming. Every line uses the **unreal** *if*.

<Callout type="tip" title="The two halves of the dream">
  ① **if-half**: past form (*had / lived / could / were …*) — but the meaning is **now**. <br />
  ② **main half**: **would / wouldn't / could / couldn't** + verb. <br />
  *If I had time, I'd call you* = I don't have time, so I'm not calling you.
</Callout>

## Part A — *if I had …, I would …*

The shape:

    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center">
      <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">if-half</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-sm text-amber-900"><strong>If</strong> I / you / it / they …</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-sm text-amber-900"><em>had</em> / <em>knew</em> / <em>lived</em> /<br />didn't have / didn't know /<br /><em>were</em> / <em>could</em> …</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center">
      <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">main half</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-sm text-amber-900">I / you / it / they …</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-sm text-amber-900"><strong>would</strong> … / <strong>wouldn't</strong> … <br /> <strong>could</strong> … / <strong>couldn't</strong> …</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

You can say:

- **If he had the money**, he would buy a car. *(but he doesn't)*
- He **would buy a car** if he had the money.

Two halves, either order. **'d** is the everyday short form of **would**:

| Real life *(now)* | The dream sentence |
|---|---|
| I don't know the answer. | If I **knew** the answer, I**'d** tell you. |
| It's raining. We're not going out. | We**'d get** wet if we **went** out. |
| Jane lives in a city. She likes cities. | She **wouldn't be** happy if she **lived** in the country. |
| You have a job. | If you **didn't have** a job, what **would** you **do**? |
| I'm sorry I can't help you. | I**'d help** you if I **could**. *(but I can't)* |
| We don't have a car, so we can't travel much. | If we **had** a car, we **could travel** more. |

## Part B — *if I was* or *if I were*?

In this unreal *if*, English allows **either** *was* **or** *were* — even with **I / he / she / it**:

- It's not a very nice place. I wouldn't go there **if I were you**. *(or … if I was you)*
- It would be nice **if the weather was** better. *(or … if the weather were better)*
- What would Tom do **if he were** here? *(or … if he was here)*

<Callout type="info" title="The *if I were you* tip">
  *If I were you, I'd …* is the friendliest way to **give advice** in English. <br />
  *If I were you, I'd take the early train.* &nbsp; *If I were you, I wouldn't say anything.* <br />
  *Were* sounds slightly more careful and traditional; *was* is fine in everyday speech.
</Callout>

## Part C — real *if* vs unreal *if*

This is the big compare. Same word *if*, two very different worlds:

    <p className="text-center text-sm text-slate-700">if + <strong>past form</strong>, … <strong>would</strong> + verb</p>
    <p className="mt-3 text-center text-base font-semibold text-rose-900">If I <strong>had</strong> time, I<strong>'d go</strong> today.</p>
    <p className="mt-1 text-center text-xs italic text-slate-600">= I don't have time, so I'm not going</p>
    <p className="mt-3 text-center text-base font-semibold text-rose-900">I<strong>'d buy</strong> it if it <strong>wasn't</strong> so expensive.</p>
    <p className="mt-1 text-center text-xs italic text-slate-600">= it IS expensive, so I'm not buying it</p>
    <p className="mt-3 text-center text-base font-semibold text-rose-900">I<strong>'d help</strong> you <strong>if I could</strong>, but I can't.</p>
    <p className="mt-1 text-center text-xs italic text-slate-600">= I can't, so I'm not helping</p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="The two big traps">
  ❌ *If I **would have** time, I'd go.* &nbsp; → ✅ *If I **had** time, I'd go.* (no *would* in the if-half) <br />
  ❌ *If he **was** rich, he buys a car.* &nbsp; → ✅ *If he **had** money, he **would buy** a car.* (use *would* in the main half)
</Callout>
