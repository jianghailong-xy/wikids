If English had a Swiss Army knife, it would be **get**. One tiny verb, four big jobs: it can mean *receive*, *become*, *arrive*, even *climb in/out of a car*. The trick is to look at the **word right after** *get* — a noun, an adjective, a place, or a little word like *in/out/on/off* — and the meaning falls into place.

## Four lives of one verb

Lia just walked through the door. Listen for **get** in her chat — and notice it never means quite the same thing twice.

## Part A — *get* + *noun* = receive / buy / find

When *get* sits next to a **noun**, it usually swaps in for *receive*, *buy*, or *find* — picture the moment you **don't have something → you have it**.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-3">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">get = receive</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">I <strong>got an email</strong> from Sam this morning.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">get = buy</p>
    <p className="text-base text-amber-900">I like your sweater. Where did you <strong>get</strong> it?</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">get = find</p>
    <p className="text-base text-violet-900">It's hard to <strong>get a job</strong> at the moment.</p>
  </div>
</div>

- 'Is Lisa here?' 'Yes, I'll **get** her for you.' *(= fetch / bring)*
- 'Did you walk here?' 'No, I **got the bus**.' *(= took the bus)*

<Callout type="info" title="Also: get a bus / a train / a taxi">
  *Get a bus* doesn't mean buying a bus — it means **taking** one. Same with *get a train, get a taxi*. A handy everyday shortcut.
</Callout>

## Part B — *get* + *adjective* = become

Stick an **adjective** after *get* and it means **become** — moving from one state into another.

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-rose-50">
  <div className="bg-rose-200 px-4 py-2 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-900">not X → get X → X</div>
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-rose-200 sm:grid-cols-3 lg:grid-cols-5">
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-rose-900">get hungry</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-rose-900">get cold</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-rose-900">get tired</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-rose-900">get wet</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-rose-900">get better</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

- If you don't eat, you **get hungry**.
- Drink your coffee. It's **getting cold**.
- I'm sorry your mother is ill. I hope she **gets better** soon.
- It was raining hard. We didn't have an umbrella, so we **got very wet**.

### Three special phrases — *get married / get dressed / get lost*

These three are everyday classics. They follow the same *get + adjective* pattern, but they each have a fixed meaning.

</div>

## Part C — *get to* + *place* = arrive

Use **get to** + a place to talk about **arriving**.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">✅ get to + place</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">I usually <strong>get to work</strong> before 8.30. <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(= arrive at work)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">We left London at 10 and <strong>got to Manchester</strong> at 12.45.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">✅ no <em>to</em> with home / here / there</p>
    <p className="text-base text-violet-900">What time did you <strong>get home</strong> last night?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">How did you <strong>get here</strong>? By bus?</p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="Drop the to">
  *Home, here, there* never take *to* — same trap as *go home* in lesson 55. Say <strong>get home</strong>, <strong>get here</strong>, <strong>get there</strong> — never *get to home*.
</Callout>

## Part D — *get in / out / on / off*

For vehicles, the little word after *get* tells you which kind of vehicle and which direction.

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-sky-200 bg-white shadow-sm">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-sky-200 sm:grid-cols-2 lg:grid-cols-4">
    <div className="bg-sky-50 p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">small vehicle ↗</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-base font-semibold text-sky-900">get <strong>in</strong></p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-xs text-slate-600">a car / a taxi</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-rose-50 p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">small vehicle ↙</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-base font-semibold text-rose-900">get <strong>out (of)</strong></p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-xs text-slate-600">a car / a taxi</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-amber-50 p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">big vehicle ↗</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-base font-semibold text-amber-900">get <strong>on</strong></p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-xs text-slate-600">a bus / a train / a plane</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-violet-50 p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">big vehicle ↙</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-base font-semibold text-violet-900">get <strong>off</strong></p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-xs text-slate-600">a bus / a train / a plane</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

- Kate **got in the car** and drove away. *(also: Kate got **into** the car and …)*
- A car stopped and a man **got out**. *(but: A man got out **of** the car.)*
- We **got on the bus** outside the hotel and **got off** in Church Street.

<Callout type="info" title="in vs into, out vs out of">
  When the vehicle is named, *get out* and *get in* often grow a little tail: *got out **of** the car*, *got **into** the car*. When the vehicle is left silent, the tail drops: *A man **got out**.*
</Callout>

## Now you try: a day in the life of *get*

Write **six** true sentences about today (or a recent day), using a different shape of *get* in each one.

1. **get + noun (receive/buy)** → *This morning I got …*
2. **get + adjective (become)** → *Later I got a bit …*
3. **get to + place** → *I got to … at …*
4. **get home / here / there** → *I got home at …*
5. **get in / get out (of)** → *I got in/out of …*
6. **get on / get off** → *I got on/off the …*
