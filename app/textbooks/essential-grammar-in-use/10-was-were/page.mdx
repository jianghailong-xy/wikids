So far, every sentence we've made has been about **right now**: *I **am** tired. She **is** at home. They **are** late.* But what about **yesterday**? What about **last night**? When we step into the past, **am / is / are** change shape — they become **was** and **were**.

## The big rule: am/is/are step back in time

You already know **am / is / are** for *now*. To talk about *then*, just swap each one for its past partner:

Watch the same sentence travel from **today** to **yesterday**:

| Now (present) | Then (past) |
|---|---|
| I **am** tired. | I **was** tired last night. |
| Where **is** Kate? | Where **was** Kate yesterday? |
| The weather **is** good today. | The weather **was** good last week. |
| You **are** late. | You **were** late yesterday. |
| They **aren't** here. | They **weren't** here last Sunday. |

## When does was/were live?

Anywhere **earlier than now** — even one minute ago counts.

- **Now Robert is at work.** &nbsp; <SayIt text="Now Robert is at work." iconOnly />
- **At midnight last night he wasn't at work — he was in bed, asleep.** &nbsp; <SayIt text="At midnight last night he wasn't at work. He was in bed, asleep." iconOnly />

<Callout type="info" title="One job, two times">
  **Be** is the same little verb in both sentences. It just dresses differently for present (**is**) and past (**was**).
</Callout>

## All seven persons in one place

The past has even *fewer* shapes than the present — only **two**: **was** and **were**. Match each pronoun once and you're done forever.

| Subject | positive | negative | question |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I** | was | was not (**wasn't**) | **was** I? |
| **he / she / it** | was | was not (**wasn't**) | **was** he? |
| **we / you / they** | were | were not (**weren't**) | **were** they? |

A few real examples to put it together:

- Last year Rachel **was** 22, so she **is** 23 now.
- When I **was** a child, I **was** scared of dogs.
- We **were** hungry after the journey, but we **weren't** tired.
- The hotel **was** comfortable, but it **wasn't** expensive.

<SayIt text="When I was a child, I was scared of dogs." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="The hotel was comfortable, but it wasn't expensive." />

## Saying "no" — wasn't and weren't

You already know the **n't** trick from Lesson 1: stick **n't** on the verb to say no. Same trick here — **was not** shrinks to **wasn't**, and **were not** shrinks to **weren't**.

<Callout type="warning" title="No I'was, no they're-was">
  Unlike **am / is / are**, the past forms **don't shrink with the subject**. <br />
  ✅ I **was** late. &nbsp; ❌ I'**was** late. <br />
  ✅ They **were** here. &nbsp; ❌ They'**re** here. *(that's present, not past!)*<br />
  Only the **negative** shrinks: **wasn't** and **weren't**.
</Callout>

## Short answers

A polite **yes** or **no** in English brings the verb along — same trick as Lesson 2, just in the past:

| Question | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| **Were** you late? | **Yes, I was.** | **No, I wasn't.** |
| **Was** Tom at work yesterday? | **Yes, he was.** | **No, he wasn't.** |
| **Were** Sue and Steve at the party? | **Yes, they were.** | **No, they weren't.** |

<Callout type="tip" title="Mirror, mirror">
  The pronoun in the answer **mirrors** the subject of the answer, not the question. *"Were **you** late?"* → *"No, **I** wasn't."* (you turns into I!)
</Callout>

<SayIt text="Were you late? No, I wasn't." />
