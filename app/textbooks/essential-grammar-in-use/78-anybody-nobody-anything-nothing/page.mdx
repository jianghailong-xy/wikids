You met *anybody / something* in lesson 76. Today the **zero** members of the family arrive: **nobody / no-one / nothing**. The rule: **negative verb + anybody/anything**, **positive verb + nobody/nothing**. Same meaning — different shape. Plus the golden rule of English negatives: **never two at once** (*He doesn't know nothing* ❌).

<Callout type="tip" title="Two ways, one zero">
  Negative verb + **any-** (didn't see anybody / isn't anything). Positive verb + **no-** (saw nobody / there is nothing). Pick one and stop.
</Callout>

- There **isn't anybody** in the room. = There **is nobody** in the room.
- There **isn't anything** in the bag. = There **is nothing** in the bag.
- A: Who is in the room? B: **Nobody. / No-one**.
- A: What's in the bag? B: **Nothing**.

<Callout type="info" title="-body and -one are the same">
  *anybody = anyone, nobody = no-one*. Pick whichever you like — they mean exactly the same.
</Callout>

## Part B — Translating between the two shapes

- I don't know **anybody** here. = I know **nobody** here.
- I'm lonely. I have **nobody** to talk to. = I don't have **anybody** to talk to.
- The house is empty. There is **no-one** in it. = There **isn't anyone** in it.
- I can't remember **anything**. = I remember **nothing**.
- She said **nothing**. = She **didn't say anything**.
- There's **nothing** to eat. = There **isn't anything** to eat.

## Part C — *nobody / no-one / nothing* alone or to start a sentence

You can drop them in alone (as an answer) or put them at the **start** of a sentence:

- The house is empty. **Nobody** lives there.
- 'Who did you speak to?' '**No-one**.'
- **Nothing** happened.
- 'What did you say?' '**Nothing**.'

## Now you try

Write **six** zero-sentences. Use each shape at least once.

1. *I didn't see ___ at the party.* (anybody)
2. *___ knows my secret.* (Nobody)
3. *There's ___ to eat.* (nothing)
4. *I don't have ___ to do tonight.* (anything)
5. *___ told me about the meeting.* (Nobody)
6. *I'm not telling ___ .* (anybody)

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — proofread for double negatives">
  Read this paragraph and find each double negative: <br /><br />
  *Yesterday I went to the market, but I didn't buy nothing because there wasn't no fresh bread, and nobody didn't help me find anything.* <br /><br />
  Now rewrite it with **one negative per clause**: *I didn't buy anything … there wasn't any fresh bread … nobody helped me …*.
