Five quantity words on one sliding scale: **all** (100%), **most** (a big majority), **some** (less than half), **any** (an unknown amount, often in questions and negatives), and **no / none** (0%). The trick is the little word **of**: drop it before a plain noun (*most children*), but keep it before *the / this / my* (*most of the children*).

  Plain noun (*children, money, books*) → **no of**: *most children, some money, any books*. Specific noun with *the / this / my* → **add of**: *most **of** the children, some **of** this money, any **of** my books*.
</Callout>

## Part B — *most / some* etc. + noun (no *of*)

In front of a **plain noun** (in general), use *all / most / some / any / no* with **no of**:

- **Most children** like playing. *(not Most of children)*
- I don't want **any money**.
- **Some books** are better than others.
- He has **no friends**.
- **All cities** have the same problems.

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch the of trap">
  ❌ *Most of people drive too fast.* &nbsp; ✅ **Most people** drive too fast. <br /> ❌ *Some of birds can't fly.* &nbsp; ✅ **Some birds** can't fly.
</Callout>

## Part C — *most of / some of* etc. + the/this/my

When the noun has **the / this / that / these / those / my / your / Paul's** in front of it, slide an **of** in:

- **Most of the children** at this school are under 11.
- I don't want **any of this money**.
- **Some of these books** are very old.
- **None of my friends** live near me.

### *all the* or *all of the*?

After **all** the *of* is **optional** (with or without):

- **All the students** in our class passed the exam. *(or All of the students)*
- Amy has lived in London **all her life**. *(or all of her life)*

## Part D — *all of it / most of them / none of us*

With pronouns (*it, them, us, you*) you **must** use *of*:

    <div className="bg-white p-3"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">word</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-sky-900">all / most / some / any / none</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">+ of + pronoun</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-sky-900">it / them / us / you</p></div>
  </div>

- You can have **some of this cake**, but not **all of it**.
- A: Do you know those people? B: **Most of them**, but not **all of them**.
- **Some of us** are going out tonight. Why don't you come with us?
- I have a lot of books, but I haven't read **any of them**.
- 'How many of these books have you read?' '**None of them**.'

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences about your life. Use each shape at least once.

1. *Most of my friends ___ .*
2. *Some ___ in my country are very ___ .*
3. *None of my ___ live near me.*
4. *All cities ___ .*
5. *I have a lot of ___ , but I haven't read any of them.*
6. *Most ___ have ___ these days.*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — the of switch">
  Write the same sentence twice — once general, once specific. Example: *Most children like playing.* (in general) → *Most of the children at this school like playing.* (a specific group). Try it with: *teachers, books, money, restaurants, films*. The word **the** (or *this / my*) is what flips the switch.
</Callout>
