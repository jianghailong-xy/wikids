A small family of *middle words* — **always, usually, often, sometimes, ever, never, rarely, seldom, also, just, already, still, all, both** — share **one simple slot rule**. They go: **(1) before the main verb** (*I always drink coffee*), **(2) after am/is/are/was/were** (*I am always tired*), and **(3) between two verbs* (*I will always remember you*, *Have you ever been to Egypt?*). Same rule for all of them — once you've got it, fourteen words click into place at once.

## Part B — *before the main verb*

With one ordinary verb (*go, play, have, drink, eat*…), the middle word sits **right in front** of it:

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">always / often / never + verb</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">I <strong>always drink</strong> coffee in the morning. <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(not I drink always coffee)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Helen <strong>often goes</strong> to London. <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(not Helen goes often)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">You <strong>sometimes look</strong> unhappy.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">They <strong>usually have</strong> dinner at 7 o'clock.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">We <strong>rarely watch</strong> TV. *or* We <strong>seldom watch</strong> TV.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">also / all + verb</p>
    <p className="text-base text-violet-900">Richard is a good footballer. He <strong>also plays</strong> tennis and volleyball. <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(not He plays also tennis)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">I've got three sisters. They <strong>all live</strong> in London.</p>

## Part C — *after* am / is / are / was / were

With **be** (am, is, are, was, were), the middle word jumps to the **other side** — *after* the verb:

- I**'m always tired**. *(not I always am tired)*
- They **are never** at home during the day.
- It **is usually** very cold here in winter.
- When I was a child, I **was often** late for school.
- A: Where's Laura? B: She**'s still** in bed.
- I've got two brothers. They**'re both** doctors.

<Callout type="warning" title="Be is special">
  With ordinary verbs → **before** (*I always drink*). With *be* → **after** (*I am always tired*). Same word, opposite side.
</Callout>

## Part D — *between two verbs* (helper + main)

When there are **two verbs** (modal + verb, or *have* + past participle), the middle word sits **between them**:

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-amber-200 sm:grid-cols-3">
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">verb 1 (helper)</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-amber-900">will / can / do / have / has</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">always / often / never …</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-amber-900">middle slot</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">verb 2 (main)</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-amber-900">go / find / been / gone / remember</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

- I **will always remember** you.
- It **doesn't often rain** here.
- **Do you usually go** to work by car?
- I **can never find** my keys.
- **Have you ever been** to Egypt?
- A: Where's Laura? B: She**'s just gone** out. *(she's = she has)*
- My friends **have all gone** to the cinema.

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences using a middle word.

1. *I always ___ in the morning.*
2. *I am never ___ .*
3. *My friend often ___ .*
4. *I have just ___ .*
5. *Have you ever ___ ?*
6. *My brother and I both ___ .*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — three-step slot test">
  Before you place a middle word, ask:<br />
  ① **Is the verb am / is / are / was / were?** → put the middle word **after** it.<br />
  ② **Are there two verbs (will/can/do/have + main)?** → put it **between** them.<br />
  ③ **Otherwise — one ordinary verb?** → put it **before** the verb.<br />
  Three questions, fourteen words slot themselves into place.
</Callout>
