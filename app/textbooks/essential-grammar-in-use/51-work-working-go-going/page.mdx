You've now met every verb form English has — *work, to work, working*. They look small and harmless, but **picking the right one** is what trips beginners up over and over. Today is a **map**: three forms, three jobs, and the trigger word that pulls each one out of the toolbox. Once you can spot the trigger, the form falls into place automatically.

## The three forms, side by side

Take any verb — say *work* — and English will reach for one of these three shapes:

The trick is to **read the word in front** of the gap. That word is the trigger.

## Part A — plain infinitive: *work, go, be, do*

Use the **plain** form (no -s, no -ing, no to) after these triggers.

### After modal verbs

The modals (*will, shall, might, may, can, could, must, should, would*) all glue straight onto the plain verb. No *to*, no *-s*.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">modal + plain verb</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">Anna <strong>will be</strong> here soon.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Shall I <strong>open</strong> the window?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">I might <strong>phone</strong> you later.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">May I <strong>sit</strong> here?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">I can't <strong>meet</strong> you tomorrow.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Could you <strong>pass</strong> the salt?</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">It's late. I must <strong>go</strong> now.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">You shouldn't <strong>work</strong> so hard.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Would you like some coffee?</p>
  </div>
</div>

### After do / does / did

When *do/does/did* shows up (questions, negatives), the main verb stays **plain** — no -s, no -ed.

- **Do** you **work**? &nbsp; They don't **work** very hard. &nbsp; Helen doesn't **know** many people.
- How much **does** it **cost**?
- What time **did** the train **leave**? &nbsp; We didn't **sleep** well.

Some words **demand** a *to* before the verb. Memorise this trigger list — it gets you most of the way.

## Part C — *-ing* form: *working, going, doing*

Use the *-ing* form for present continuous (*am/is/are + -ing*) and past continuous (*was/were + -ing*).

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">am/is/are + -ing (now)</p>
    <p className="text-base text-violet-900">Please be quiet. I'<strong>m working</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">Tom <strong>isn't working</strong> today.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">What time <strong>are</strong> you <strong>going</strong> out?</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">was/were + -ing (then)</p>
    <p className="text-base text-violet-900">It <strong>was raining</strong>, so we didn't go out.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">What <strong>were</strong> you <strong>doing</strong> at 11.30 yesterday?</p>
  </div>
</div>

## The trigger map — at a glance

When you see a gap, look at the word **right before it**. That word picks the form.

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-slate-200 bg-white shadow-sm">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-slate-200 sm:grid-cols-3">
    <div className="bg-emerald-50 p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">→ plain verb</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-sm text-slate-700">will, shall, might, may, can, could, must, should, would, do, does, did</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-amber-50 p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">→ to + verb</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-sm text-slate-700">going to, have to, want to, would like to, used to (+ many more)</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-violet-50 p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">→ -ing</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-sm text-slate-700">am, is, are, was, were (continuous tenses)</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="Two classic mix-ups">
  ❌ I will <span className="line-through">to work</span>. &nbsp;✅ I **will work**. *(modals never want to)*<br />
  ❌ I want <span className="line-through">work</span>. &nbsp;✅ I **want to work**. *(want always wants to)*<br />
  ❌ I'm <span className="line-through">work</span> now. &nbsp;✅ I'm **working** now. *(am/is/are want -ing)*
</Callout>

## Now you try: a sentence with each form

Write **one sentence about yourself** for each row below — and circle (mentally) the trigger word in each one.

1. **will + plain** → *I will …*
2. **can / can't + plain** → *I can / can't …*
3. **should + plain** → *I should …*
4. **going to + plain** → *I'm going to …*
5. **want to + plain** → *I want to …*
6. **have to + plain** → *I have to …*
7. **am/is/are + -ing** → *I'm … now.*
8. **was/were + -ing** → *Last night at 9 I was …*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — eight verbs in one paragraph">

In the evening I'**ll be cooking** dinner for my flatmate." <br /><br />
  Twelve verbs, three different shapes, one breezy plan. The trigger pulled the form every time.
