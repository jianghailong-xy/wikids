Many English verbs come with a tiny preposition stuck to them — and you have to **say it** for the sentence to feel complete.

> ✅ I'm **listening to** the radio. <br />
> ❌ ~~I'm listening the radio.~~

Today's lesson is the everyday list — plus the *look* family (*at / for / after*) and the *depend on* trap.

## Part A — verbs with a fixed preposition

| verb + prep | example |
|---|---|
| **ask** (somebody) **for** … | Don't **ask me for money**. I don't have any. |
| **belong to** … | This house doesn't **belong to me**. *(= it's not mine)* |
| **happen to** … | I can't find my phone. What's **happened to it**? |
| **listen to** … | **Listen to this music.** It's great. |
| **talk to** sb (**about** …) | Did you **talk to Paul about the problem**? |
| **speak to** sb (**about** …) | I'd like to **speak to the manager**, please. |
| **thank** sb **for** … | **Thank you very much for your help.** |
| **think about / of** … | He never **thinks about other people**. |
| **wait for** … | **Wait for me.** I'm nearly ready. |

<SayIt text="Listen to this music." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="Wait for me." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="Thank you for your help." />

<Callout type="warning" title="The no-preposition trap">
  These verbs take **NO preposition** — never *to* or *at*. <br />
  ✅ I have to **phone my parents**. *(not phone **to** my parents)* <br />
  ✅ Shall I **text you** or **email you**? <br />
  Same rule: *call somebody*, *answer a question*, *enter a room*, *meet a person*.
</Callout>

## Part C — *depend on*

The fixed shape: **depend on** + a noun.

- 'Do you like eating in restaurants?' 'Sometimes. **It depends on the restaurant**.' *(not depends **of**)*

When the next word is a question word (*what / where / how / when / who / why*), the **on** is **optional**:

- 'Do you want to come out with us?' 'It **depends where** you're going.'
- … *or* It **depends on where** you're going.

<Callout type="info" title="depend on, not depend of">
  Many languages use *of*; English uses **on**. *It depends on the weather, on the price, on the time.*
</Callout>

## Now you try

Make four true sentences about your own week.

1. *I'm waiting **for** ___ .*
2. *I need to talk **to** ___ **about** ___ .*
3. *Tonight I'd like to listen **to** ___ .*
4. *It depends **on** ___ .*

<Callout type="tip" title="One-sentence summary">
  Many verbs lock to a preposition: *listen **to**, wait **for**, ask **for**, belong **to**, happen **to**, talk / speak **to about**, thank somebody **for**, think **of / about**, depend **on***. <br />
  The **look** family splits by preposition: *look **at*** (eyes), *look **for*** (try to find), *look **after*** (take care of). <br />
  Trap: *phone / call / text / email / answer / meet* take **no** preposition.
</Callout>
