In Lesson 3 you learned the **live commentary** tense — *I'm doing, she's reading* — for things happening **right now**. In Lesson 10 you learned how **am/is/are** step back in time and become **was/were**. Today we put the two ideas together. Result: a tense that lets you say what was **in the middle of happening** at a moment in the past — even if it's already finished now.

## The big idea: in the middle of doing it

Look at Paul. It's 6 o'clock now and he's at home watching TV. But two hours ago, at 4 o'clock, he wasn't on the sofa — he was **in the middle of a swim**. He had jumped in at 3:30 and he didn't climb out until 4:15. So at 4:00, between those two moments, **he was swimming**.

## The pattern: was/were + verb-ing

If you can do present continuous, you can already do past continuous. Just swap **am/is/are** for their past partners:

> **was / were** + verb-**ing**

| Subject | + be (past) | + verb-ing |
|---|---|---|
| **I** | **was** | working |
| **He / She / It** | **was** | working |
| **We / You / They** | **were** | working |

Tap to hear the three forms:

<SayIt text="I was working at ten thirty last night." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="She was reading a book." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="They were playing football."/>

<Callout type="warning" title="Both halves, always">
  Like in Lesson 3, the **be** half and the **-ing** half travel as a pair. Drop one and the sentence breaks. <br />
  ✅ She **was reading**. &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ She reading. &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ She was read.
</Callout>

## Spelling: same -ing tricks as Lesson 3

The **-ing** form doesn't care whether the helper is *am* or *was* — the spelling rules are exactly the same as in Lesson 3. A quick refresher:

- **Drop the -e:** liv**e** → liv**ing**, mak**e** → mak**ing**, danc**e** → danc**ing**
- **Double the last letter:** ru**n** → ru**nning**, sw**i**m → swi**mming**, s**i**t → si**tting**
- **ie → y:** l**ie** → l**ying**, d**ie** → d**ying**

So: *I was lying on the sofa. They were running for the bus. She was making dinner.*

## Three real-life examples

Look at each example and notice how the past continuous paints the **scene** — the thing that was already in progress when something else happened or when you took a snapshot.

- It **was raining**, so we didn't go out. <SayIt text="It was raining, so we didn't go out." iconOnly />
- In 2009 we **were living** in Canada. <SayIt text="In 2009 we were living in Canada." iconOnly />
- I woke up early yesterday. It was a beautiful morning — the sun **was shining** and the birds **were singing**. <SayIt text="The sun was shining and the birds were singing." iconOnly />
- Today she's wearing a skirt, but yesterday she **was wearing** trousers. <SayIt text="Yesterday she was wearing trousers." iconOnly />

## Saying "no" — wasn't and weren't + -ing

To say something **wasn't happening**, use the negative forms you met in Lesson 10 — **wasn't** and **weren't** — and keep the **-ing** verb after them:

| Subject | negative | + verb-ing |
|---|---|---|
| **I / he / she / it** | **wasn't** | listening |
| **we / you / they** | **weren't** | listening |

- 'What did he say?' 'I don't know. I **wasn't listening**.' <SayIt text="I wasn't listening." iconOnly />
- We **weren't watching** TV when you came in. <SayIt text="We weren't watching TV." iconOnly />
- The shops **weren't open**, so we **weren't buying** anything. <SayIt text="The shops weren't open." iconOnly />

<Callout type="info" title="One small word, big difference">
  The only thing that changes between *yes* and *no* is **n't** sliding onto **was/were**. The **-ing** verb stays exactly the same. <br />
  ✅ He **was working**. &nbsp; ✅ He **wasn't working**.
</Callout>

## Asking questions: just flip!

Same flip trick from Lesson 10 — lift **was** or **were** to the front of the sentence. The **-ing** verb stays put.

<WordFlip

<Callout type="info" title="The flip rule (past continuous edition)">
  **Statement:** subject + **was/were** + verb-ing + ... &nbsp;&nbsp;→&nbsp;&nbsp; **Question:** **was/were** + subject + verb-ing + ... ?
</Callout>

And as always, **wh-** words sit out in front of the flip:

- **What were** you **doing** at 11:30 yesterday? <SayIt text="What were you doing at 11:30 yesterday?" iconOnly />
- **Where was** Sue **going** so fast? <SayIt text="Where was Sue going so fast?" iconOnly />
- **Why were** you **driving** so fast? <SayIt text="Why were you driving so fast?" iconOnly />

## Now or then? Pick the right helper

The **-ing** verb stays exactly the same. Only the helper word — *am/is/are* vs *was/were* — tells you whether you're talking about **now** or **then**.

    <ul className="mt-3 space-y-1.5 text-sm text-emerald-900">

      <li>• It <strong>isn't raining</strong> (now).</li>
      <li>• What <strong>are</strong> you <strong>doing</strong> (now)?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-indigo-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-indigo-50 to-purple-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-indigo-700">Past continuous</p>
    <p className="mt-1 text-2xl font-bold text-indigo-900">was / were + -ing</p>
    <ul className="mt-3 space-y-1.5 text-sm text-indigo-900">
      <li>• I <strong>was working</strong> at 10:30 last night.</li>
      <li>• It <strong>wasn't raining</strong> when we went out.</li>
      <li>• What <strong>were</strong> you <strong>doing</strong> at 3 o'clock?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="tip" title="Spot the time-clue">
  The little time word at the end is your radar. **Now / at the moment** → present. **Yesterday / last night / at 3 o'clock / when X happened** → past. Once you spot it, the helper picks itself.
</Callout>
