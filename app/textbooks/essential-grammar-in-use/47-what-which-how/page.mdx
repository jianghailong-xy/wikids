You've built every English question shape there is — flips, no-flips, prepositions on tails. Today's lesson is the **vocabulary upgrade**: three little wh-words that let you target *exactly* what you want to know. **What** opens a wide door (any colour, any kind, any name). **Which** narrows it to a small set (this one or that one?). **How** asks about manner and measurement — and pairs up with adjectives like *tall, far, often, much* to nail down a precise number.

## Part A — *What* + noun

Stick a noun straight after *What* to ask about that exact feature: *What colour? What size? What time? What kind?*

### *What* without a noun — open the door wide

Without a noun, *What* is the most general "tell me anything" question word.

- **What's** your favourite colour? <SayIt text="What's your favourite colour?" iconOnly />
- **What** do you want to do tonight? <SayIt text="What do you want to do tonight?" iconOnly />

## Part B — *Which* + noun (small set of options)

When there's a **small number of possible answers** sitting in front of you (this train or that train? these four umbrellas?), reach for **Which**.

- **Which train** did you catch — the 9.50 or the 10.30?
- **Which doctor** did you see — Doctor Ellis, Doctor Gray or Doctor Hill?

<SayIt text="Which train did you catch — the 9.50 or the 10.30?" />

### *Which* without a noun — for things only

You can drop the noun if it's obvious from context — but only for **things**, not people.

- There are four umbrellas here. **Which is yours?**
- **Which is bigger** — Canada or Australia?

### People with no noun → *Who*, not *Which*

This is the trap. When you're choosing between **people** without a noun, English uses **Who**, not Which.

<Callout type="info" title="Quick rule">
  *Which* + **noun** works for both things and people: *Which doctor? Which boy?*<br />
  *Which* alone (no noun) only works for **things**. For people alone, swap to **Who**.
</Callout>

## Part C — *What* or *Which*?

Both can ask about the same idea, but they paint different sized pictures.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">Which — small set (2, 3, 4 options in mind)</p>
    <p className="text-base text-violet-900"><strong>Which way</strong> shall we go? <em>(this way or that way)</em></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900"><strong>Which</strong> is yours? <em>(out of these four umbrellas)</em></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900"><strong>Which colour</strong> do you prefer, pink or yellow?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900"><strong>Which</strong> is the longest — Mississippi, Amazon or Nile?</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">What — wide open, no set in mind</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900"><strong>What's</strong> the capital of Argentina? <em>(any city)</em></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900"><strong>What sort</strong> of music do you like? <em>(any music)</em></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900"><strong>What colour</strong> are his eyes? <em>(any colour)</em></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900"><strong>What</strong> is the longest river in the world?</p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="The small-set test">
  Picture the options. Can you count them on one hand? → **Which**. <br />
  Could the answer be anything? → **What**.<br /><br />
  *What colour are his eyes?* — could be any colour. ✅ What.<br />
  *Which colour do you prefer, pink or yellow?* — only two options on the table. ✅ Which.
</Callout>

## Part D — *How*…?

*How* is the all-purpose **manner / quality** word. On its own it asks "good or bad? in what way?"

- **How** was the party last night? — It was great. <SayIt text="How was the party last night?" iconOnly />
- **How** do you usually go to work? — By bus. <SayIt text="How do you usually go to work?" iconOnly />

### *How* + adjective / adverb — pin down a number

Stick an adjective or adverb after *How* and you've targeted a precise measurement.

## Now you try: a curious-friend interview

Pick a friend (or imagine one). Ask **eight** questions, each with a different wh-shape from this lesson.

1. **What + noun** → *What kind of …?*
2. **What** alone → *What's your favourite …?*
3. **Which + noun** (small set) → *Which … — A or B?*
4. **Who** between two people → *Who is taller, … or …?*
5. **How** alone → *How was …?*
6. **How tall / old / big** → *How … are you?*
7. **How far / long** → *How far is it from …?*
8. **How often / much** → *How often do you …?*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — fill the form">
  Imagine you're filling out a form for a new friend. Write **ten** questions to gather everything you'd want to know about them. Use a **different** wh-shape from this lesson in each question. <br /><br />
  *Example:* "What's your name? What nationality are you? How old are you? Which language do you speak best — English or Spanish? Who's your favourite singer? How tall are you? What kind of food do you like? How often do you cook? How far is it from your home to here? Which is your favourite season — spring, summer, autumn or winter?"<br /><br />
  Ten wh-questions in a row — every shape from this lesson, in your pocket.
</Callout>
