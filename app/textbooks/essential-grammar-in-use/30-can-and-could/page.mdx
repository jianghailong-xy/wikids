Some verbs have a special little helper that lives next to them. **Can** is one of those — it doesn't change for *he/she/it*, it doesn't take *do/does* in questions, and it has a built-in past form: **could**. With just these two words, you can talk about **what you're able to do**, **what was possible back then**, and politely **ask people for things**.

## At the front door

Sara is carrying a tall pile of books. Her neighbour Ben sees her struggling and lends a hand. Tap any bubble to hear it — and listen for every **can**, **can't**, **could**, and **couldn't**.

## The shape of can

**Can** is a *modal verb* — a special helper that sits in front of the main verb. The main verb stays in its plain form (no *-s*, no *-ing*, no *to*).

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-teal-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-3 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">positive & negative</p>
    <p className="text-center text-sm text-slate-600">subject + <strong>can / can't</strong> + verb</p>
    <ul className="mt-3 space-y-1 text-center text-base font-semibold text-emerald-900">
      <li>I <strong>can</strong> swim.</li>
      <li>She <strong>can't</strong> swim.</li>
      <li>They <strong>can</strong> come.</li>
      <li>He <strong>can't</strong> come.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-indigo-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-indigo-50 to-purple-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-3 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-indigo-700">questions</p>
    <p className="text-center text-sm text-slate-600"><strong>Can</strong> + subject + verb<strong>?</strong></p>
    <ul className="mt-3 space-y-1 text-center text-base font-semibold text-indigo-900">
      <li><strong>Can</strong> you swim?</li>
      <li><strong>Can</strong> he come?</li>
      <li><strong>Can</strong> we sit here?</li>
      <li><strong>Can</strong> I help?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="Three things can won't do">
  ❌ She <strong>cans</strong> swim. *(no -s for he/she/it)* <br />
  ❌ <strong>Does</strong> she <strong>can</strong> swim? *(no do/does in questions)* <br />
  ❌ She can <strong>to swim</strong>. *(no "to" before the verb)* <br /><br />
  ✅ She **can swim**. ✅ **Can** she **swim**? ✅ She **can't swim**.
</Callout>

## Job 1 — what you're able to do

**I can do something** = *I know how to do it*, or *it's possible for me to do it*. **I can't** is the opposite — *I don't know how*, or *it isn't possible*.

- I **can play** the piano. My brother **can play** the piano too. <SayIt text="I can play the piano." iconOnly />
- Sarah **can speak** Italian, but she **can't speak** Spanish. <SayIt text="Sarah can speak Italian, but she can't speak Spanish." iconOnly />
- A: **Can you swim?** &nbsp; B: Yes, but I'm not a very good swimmer. <SayIt text="Can you swim?" iconOnly />
- A: **Can you change** twenty pounds? &nbsp; B: I'm sorry, I **can't**. <SayIt text="Can you change twenty pounds?" iconOnly />
- I'm having a party next week, but Paul and Rachel **can't come**. <SayIt text="Paul and Rachel can't come." iconOnly />

<Callout type="info" title="Two flavours of 'can'">
  *I can play the piano* could mean **skill** (*I learned how*) or **possibility** (*the piano is free, I'm allowed to use it*). Same shape — let the sentence around it tell you which.
</Callout>

## Job 2 — could / couldn't (the past of can)

Step back into yesterday, last week, when I was young. **Can** turns into **could**, and **can't** into **couldn't**. The shape stays the same — *subject + could/couldn't + verb*.

  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-teal-50 p-4 text-center shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">now / in general</p>
    <p className="my-2 text-2xl font-bold text-emerald-900">can / can't</p>
    <p className="text-sm italic text-slate-600">I <strong>can</strong> run fast.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-amber-50 to-orange-50 p-4 text-center shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">yesterday / last week / when I was young</p>
    <p className="my-2 text-2xl font-bold text-amber-900">could / couldn't</p>
    <p className="text-sm italic text-slate-600">I <strong>could</strong> run fast.</p>
  </div>
</div>

- When I was young, I **could run** very fast. <SayIt text="When I was young, I could run very fast." iconOnly />
- Before Maria came to Britain, she **couldn't understand** much English. Now she **can understand** everything. <SayIt text="Before Maria came to Britain, she couldn't understand much English." iconOnly />
- I was tired last night, but I **couldn't sleep**. <SayIt text="I was tired last night, but I couldn't sleep." iconOnly />
- I had a party last week, but Paul and Rachel **couldn't come**. <SayIt text="Paul and Rachel couldn't come." iconOnly />

<Callout type="tip" title="Listen for the time clue">
  *Yesterday, last night, last week, when I was young, before…* are all little flags shouting **past** — so reach for **could / couldn't**, never *can / can't*. <br />
  ✅ I **couldn't** sleep last night. &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ I **can't** sleep last night.
</Callout>

## Job 3 — asking people to do things

Need someone's help? Start your sentence with **Can you …?** or **Could you …?** Both work — **could** is just a touch more polite.

- **Can you** open the door, please? &nbsp; *or* &nbsp; **Could you** open the door, please? <SayIt text="Could you open the door, please?" iconOnly />
- **Can you** wait a moment, please? &nbsp; *or* &nbsp; **Could you** wait a moment, please? <SayIt text="Could you wait a moment, please?" iconOnly />
- **Could you** pass the salt? <SayIt text="Could you pass the salt?" iconOnly />
- **Can you** turn off the radio? <SayIt text="Can you turn off the radio?" iconOnly />

## Job 4 — asking *for* something, or asking permission

Two more polite recipes — both built from **can / could**, but pointed at *yourself* this time.

  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-rose-50 to-pink-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">asking <em>for</em> something</p>
    <p className="my-2 text-lg font-semibold text-rose-900"><strong>Can I have …?</strong> &nbsp; <strong>Can I get …?</strong></p>
    <ul className="space-y-1 text-sm text-slate-700">
      <li><strong>Can I have</strong> a glass of water, please?</li>
      <li><strong>Can I get</strong> a coffee to take away?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-violet-50 to-purple-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">asking <em>permission</em></p>
    <p className="my-2 text-lg font-semibold text-violet-900"><strong>Can I …?</strong> &nbsp; <strong>Could I …?</strong> &nbsp;<span className="text-xs text-violet-600">(= is it OK to … ?)</span></p>
    <ul className="space-y-1 text-sm text-slate-700">
      <li><strong>Can I</strong> sit here?</li>
      <li>Tom, <strong>could I</strong> borrow your umbrella?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<SayIt text="Can I have a glass of water, please?" /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="Could I borrow your umbrella?" />

<Callout type="info" title="Can vs Could — politeness dial">
  Both are friendly, but **could** sounds a little softer and more careful. Use **could** with strangers, in shops, with teachers; use **can** with friends and family. Either works — nobody will mind.
</Callout>

## Side-by-side: four jobs at a glance

The same little helper, in four very different situations:

| Job | Recipe | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Ability / possibility** | I / you / he … **can / can't** + verb | I **can swim**. She **can't speak** Spanish. |
| **Past ability** | I / you / he … **could / couldn't** + verb | When I was young, I **could run** fast. I **couldn't sleep** last night. |
| **Ask someone to do** | **Can you …?** &nbsp; **Could you …?** | **Could you** open the door, please? |
| **Ask for / Ask permission** | **Can I have …?** &nbsp; **Can I …?** &nbsp; **Could I …?** | **Can I have** a coffee? &nbsp; **Could I** borrow your pen? |

## Now you try: a tiny me-portrait

Say one true sentence about yourself for each prompt. Use **I can**, **I can't**, **I could**, or **I couldn't**.

1. Something **I can** do well — *e.g.* "I **can** ride a bike."
2. Something **I can't** do (yet) — *e.g.* "I **can't** speak French."
3. Something **I could** do when I was younger — *e.g.* "When I was 6, I **could** do a cartwheel."
4. Something **I couldn't** do yesterday — *e.g.* "Yesterday I **couldn't** find my phone for an hour."
5. A polite request you'd make to a friend — start with **Could you …?**
6. Something you'd ask for at a café — start with **Can I have …?**
