In Lesson 10 you learned how to talk about **yesterday** with **was** and **were**. But what about your **whole life so far** — every place you've visited, every job you've had, every silly thing you've done? English has a special tense for that. It's called the **present perfect**, and the magic words are **have** and **has**.

## The big idea: a time from the past until now

The present perfect is the tense for **a person's whole life so far** — from the day they were born up to **right now**. It doesn't matter *when* something happened; it only matters whether it has happened **at any point in your life**.

<div className="not-prose my-6 rounded-2xl border-2 border-indigo-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-indigo-50 to-purple-50 p-5 text-center shadow-sm">
  <div className="flex items-center justify-between text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-slate-500">
    <span className="text-indigo-700">past</span>
    <span>now</span>
  </div>
  <div className="my-3 h-1 w-full rounded-full bg-gradient-to-r from-indigo-200 via-indigo-400 to-emerald-500" />
  <p className="text-base font-semibold text-indigo-800">↑ "Have you ever been to Japan?" lives across this whole line</p>
  <p className="mt-1 text-xs text-slate-500">any time in your life · last year · ten years ago · last week · five minutes ago …</p>
</div>

So when Lisa asks **"Have you ever been to Japan?"**, she's really asking: *"In your whole life — from your very first day to today — has there been a moment when you were in Japan?"* Helen answers **"No, never"** — meaning *not once, ever*.

## How to build it: have/has + past partner

The recipe is just two ingredients:

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-rose-50 to-pink-50 p-5 text-center shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">Ingredient 1</p>
    <p className="my-2 text-3xl font-bold text-rose-900">have / has</p>
    <p className="text-sm text-slate-600">**has** for he / she / it<br />**have** for everyone else</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-teal-50 p-5 text-center shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">Ingredient 2</p>
    <p className="my-2 text-3xl font-bold text-emerald-900">past partner</p>
    <p className="text-sm text-slate-600">been, had, played,<br />seen, won, read …</p>
  </div>
</div>

Each verb has a special **past partner** (grammar books call it the *past participle*). Some you already know — for many verbs it's just the verb + **-ed** (*play → played*). For others you have to learn the new shape:

| Verb | Past partner | Example |
|---|---|---|
| be | **been** | I've **been** to Canada. |
| have | **had** | She's **had** many jobs. |
| play | **played** | Have you **played** golf? |
| live | **lived** | He's **lived** in three countries. |
| see | **seen** | I've **seen** that film twice. |
| read | **read** | I've **read** this book. |
| win | **won** | Brazil has **won** the World Cup. |
| break | **broken** | I've **broken** my leg. |
| fly | **flown** | She's **flown** in a helicopter. |
| do | **done** | He's **done** a lot of work. |
| write | **written** | She's **written** ten books. |
| meet | **met** | I've **met** a lot of people. |
| drive | **driven** | He's **driven** a bus. |
| lose | **lost** | I've **lost** my passport. |

<Callout type="tip" title="Don't panic about the list">
  You don't need to memorise these all at once. Meet them in sentences and they'll stick. The pattern of **have/has + past partner** is the only thing you really have to lock in today.
</Callout>

## ever and never — the two tiny power-words

Two little words turn the present perfect into a question about your *whole life*:

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-sky-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-sky-50 to-blue-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">ever</p>
    <p className="my-2 text-base text-slate-700">= **at any time in your life**</p>
    <p className="text-sm text-slate-600">Goes between **have/has** and the past partner.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-sm font-semibold text-sky-900">Have you **ever** been to Australia?</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-rose-50 to-pink-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">never</p>
    <p className="my-2 text-base text-slate-700">= **not once, in your whole life**</p>
    <p className="text-sm text-slate-600">Goes in the same spot — and replaces "not".</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-sm font-semibold text-rose-900">I've **never** been to Japan.</p>
  </div>
</div>

Some real examples — read them out loud:

- **Has** Ann **ever been** to Australia? — Yes, **once**. *(once = one time)*  <SayIt text="Has Ann ever been to Australia? Yes, once." iconOnly />
- **Have** you **ever played** golf? — Yes, I play a lot. <SayIt text="Have you ever played golf? Yes, I play a lot." iconOnly />
- My sister **has never travelled** by plane. <SayIt text="My sister has never travelled by plane." iconOnly />
- I've **never ridden** a horse. <SayIt text="I've never ridden a horse." iconOnly />
- "Who is that man?" — "I don't know. I've **never seen** him before." <SayIt text="Who is that man? I don't know. I've never seen him before." iconOnly />

<Callout type="warning" title="never is already negative">
  Don't add a second **not**. <br />
  ✅ I've **never** been to Japan. &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ I haven't never been to Japan.
</Callout>

### How many times? — once, twice, many times

Once you've done something, you can say *how often*:

| times | English |
|---|---|
| 1 | **once** |
| 2 | **twice** |
| 3+ | **three times**, **four times** … |
| lots | **many times**, **a lot**, **a few times** |
| 0 | **never** |

- "Have you read this book?" — "Yes, I've read it **twice**." <SayIt text="Have you read this book? Yes, I've read it twice." iconOnly />
- How many times **has** Brazil **won** the World Cup? <SayIt text="How many times has Brazil won the World Cup?" iconOnly />

## gone vs been — the famous mix-up

This is the trickiest pair in today's lesson. Both are past partners of "go", but they tell completely different stories.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-amber-50 to-orange-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">has gone to</p>
    <p className="my-3 text-center text-3xl">✈️ ➡️ 🇪🇸</p>
    <p className="text-center text-base font-semibold text-amber-900">Ben **has gone** to Spain.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-center text-sm text-slate-600">= he is **in Spain right now**.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-teal-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">has been to</p>
    <p className="my-3 text-center text-3xl">🇪🇸 ➡️ 🏠</p>
    <p className="text-center text-base font-semibold text-emerald-900">Ben **has been** to Spain.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-center text-sm text-slate-600">= he went, but **he's back now**.</p>
  </div>
</div>

A tiny story to feel the difference:

> Friend 1: "Where's Ben?" <br />
> Friend 2: "He's on holiday. He's **gone** to Spain." *(he's there now)*
>
> *Two weeks later — at the airport:* <br />
> Friend 1: "Hello, Ben!" <br />
> Ben: "Hi! I've **been** to Spain." *(he's back)*

<Callout type="info" title="A simple rule of thumb">
  Ask yourself: **is the person there right now, or back home?** <br />
  • Still away → **gone** <br />
  • Back home → **been**
</Callout>

Compare:

- I can't find Susan. Where **has** she **gone**? *(where is she now?)* <SayIt text="I can't find Susan. Where has she gone?" iconOnly />
- Oh, hello Susan! I was looking for you. Where **have** you **been**? *(she's right here, back from somewhere)* <SayIt text="Oh, hello Susan! I was looking for you. Where have you been?" iconOnly />
