In lesson 47 you met **how long** as part of the *how + adverb* family. Today the spotlight goes on its most useful trick — a fixed little machine for asking and answering about **time spent**: getting somewhere, doing something, finishing a task. The whole machine has just three slots: **how long** + (optionally) **who** + **the action**. Once you've memorised the shape, you can ask about anything from a bus ride to a degree.

## Part B — *How long does it take to do something?*

Now swap the *from-to* for an **action** with the word **to** + plain verb (*to cross, to fix, to learn*). Same machine — different filling.

      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-base font-semibold text-indigo-900">How long &nbsp; <span className="rounded bg-amber-100 px-2">does/did/will</span> &nbsp; it take &nbsp; <span className="rounded bg-emerald-100 px-2">to</span> + verb?</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-indigo-700">answer</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-base font-semibold text-indigo-900">It &nbsp; <span className="rounded bg-amber-100 px-2">takes/took/will take</span> &nbsp; <em>(a time)</em> &nbsp; <span className="rounded bg-emerald-100 px-2">to</span> + verb.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

### Three tenses, one shape

Notice that *does → did → will* changes with the time, but the rest of the machine stays still.

- **How long does it take to cross** the Atlantic by ship? <SayIt text="How long does it take to cross the Atlantic by ship?" iconOnly />
- 'I came by train.' 'Did you? **How long did it take to get** here?' <SayIt text="How long did it take to get here?" iconOnly />
- **How long will it take to get** from here to the hotel? <SayIt text="How long will it take to get from here to the hotel?" iconOnly />

<Callout type="info" title="Spot the 'to'">
  In Part A, *to* is the preposition pointing to a place: *to Manchester, to the hotel*. <br />
  In Part B, *to* is the **infinitive marker** before a verb: *to cross, to learn, to fix*. <br /><br />
  Both shapes use the same machine — just notice what comes after *to*. **Place** = journey. **Verb** = action.
</Callout>

## Part C — *How long does it take YOU to do something?*

You can slot a **person** into the machine, right after *it take*, to say *how long it takes that specific person*.

      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-base font-semibold text-violet-900">How long &nbsp; <span className="rounded bg-amber-100 px-2">does/did/will</span> &nbsp; it take &nbsp; <span className="rounded bg-rose-100 px-2">you/Tom/them</span> &nbsp; to + verb?</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">answer</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-base font-semibold text-violet-900">It &nbsp; <span className="rounded bg-amber-100 px-2">takes/took/will take</span> &nbsp; <span className="rounded bg-rose-100 px-2">me/Tom/them</span> &nbsp; <em>(a time)</em> &nbsp; to + verb.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

### Watch the pronoun flip

The question uses the **subject** form (*you, Tom, them*); the answer uses the **object** form (*me, Tom, them*).

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">

    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Did it take <strong>you</strong> a long time to find a job?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">How long did it take <strong>Tom</strong> to paint the bathroom?</p>

  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-sky-200 bg-sky-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">answers</p>
    <p className="text-base text-sky-900">It takes <strong>Tom</strong> 20 minutes to get to work.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-sky-900">It took <strong>us</strong> an hour to do the shopping.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-sky-900">It will take <strong>me</strong> an hour to cook dinner.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-sky-900">It took <strong>me</strong> three days to read the book.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<SayIt text="It took me three days to read the book." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="It takes Tom 20 minutes to get to work." />

<Callout type="warning" title="The slot order matters">
  ✅ It takes **me** an hour **to** cook dinner. <br />
  ❌ It takes an hour **me** to cook dinner.<br />
  ❌ It takes me to cook dinner an hour.<br /><br />
  The order is locked: **it + take + (person) + (time) + to + verb**. Keep the person right after *take*; the time right after the person; the action at the very end.
</Callout>

## Now you try: about your own life

For each prompt, write **one true sentence** about yourself starting with **It takes me / It took me / It will take me**.

1. fly from your city to London → *It takes me / It took me …*
2. fly from your city to New York → *…*
3. study to be a doctor in your country → *It takes / would take …*
4. walk from your home to the nearest shop → *…*
5. get from your home to the nearest airport → *…*
6. (about something you finished yesterday) → *It took me … to …*
7. (about something coming up tomorrow) → *It will take me … to …*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — the day-in-numbers">
  Pick a normal day in your life and describe it as **six** *It takes me …* sentences. Mix the tenses (present, past, future) and use a different action in each one.<br /><br />
  *Example:* "It takes me ten minutes to wake up properly. It takes me half an hour to get ready. It takes me 25 minutes to cycle to school. It takes me an hour to do my homework. Today it took me twenty minutes to make lunch. Tomorrow it will take me three hours to finish this project." <br /><br />
  Six little machines, all running off one fixed pattern. The day suddenly fits in six lines.
</Callout>
