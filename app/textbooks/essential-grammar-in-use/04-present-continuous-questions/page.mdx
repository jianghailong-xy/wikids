That's this lesson.

## The flip — same trick, longer verb

To turn a present continuous **statement** into a **question**, do exactly what you did in Lesson 2: lift **am / is / are** to the front. The **-ing** verb stays right where it is.

<WordFlip
  subject="You"
  verb="are"
  rest="watching it"
  caption="You are watching it. → Are you watching it?"
/>

<Callout type="info" title="The flip rule (now with -ing)">
  **Statement:** subject + **am/is/are** + **verb-ing** + ... &nbsp;&nbsp; → &nbsp;&nbsp; **Question:** **am/is/are** + subject + **verb-ing** + ... ?
</Callout>

Watch the same flip work for every subject — only **am / is / are** moves:

| Statement | Question |
|---|---|
| **I am** working today. | **Am I** working today? |
| **He is** reading. | **Is he** reading? |
| **She is** going home. | **Is she** going home? |
| **It is** raining. | **Is it** raining? |
| **We are** waiting. | **Are we** waiting? |
| **You are** watching TV. | **Are you** watching TV? |
| **They are** coming. | **Are they** coming? |

## Word order: only **is/are** moves

When the subject is more than one word — **Ben**, **the children**, **your friends**, **those people** — only **is / are** jumps to the front. The subject stays together and the **-ing** verb stays at the end.

<Callout type="warning" title="Don't break the sentence">
  ✅ **Is Ben working** today? &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ Is working Ben today? <br />
  ✅ **Where are those people going**? &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ Where are going those people? <br />
  ✅ **Why are you looking** at me? &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ Why you are looking at me?
</Callout>

Think of it like this: grab **am / is / are**, lift it to the front (or right after the wh-word), and **don't touch anything else**.

## Wh-questions: asking for details

Yes/no isn't always enough. To ask **what / where / why / who / how** someone is doing, just put the **wh-word** before the flip:

<Callout type="info" title="The pattern">
  **Wh-word** + **am/is/are** + **subject** + **verb-ing** + ... ?
</Callout>

- **What** are you **doing**? <SayIt text="What are you doing?" iconOnly />
- **Where** is she **going**? <SayIt text="Where is she going?" iconOnly />
- **Why** are you **wearing** a coat? <SayIt text="Why are you wearing a coat?" iconOnly />
- **What** is Paul **doing**? <SayIt text="What is Paul doing?" iconOnly />
- **Who** are you **waiting** for? <SayIt text="Who are you waiting for?" iconOnly />
- **What** are the children **doing**? <SayIt text="What are the children doing?" iconOnly />

Just like Lesson 2, **wh-word + is** loves to shrink. *What is* → **What's**, *where is* → **Where's**, *who is* → **Who's**:

- **What's** Paul doing? <SayIt text="What's Paul doing?" iconOnly />
- Look, there's Emily! **Where's** she going? <SayIt text="Where's she going?" iconOnly />
- **Who's** crying? <SayIt text="Who's crying?" iconOnly />

<Callout type="warning" title="Only with 'is'">
  Same warning as Lesson 2: we only shrink **wh-word + is**. With **are**, we keep them apart: ✅ *What are you doing?* &nbsp; ❌ *What're you doing?*
</Callout>

## Short answers

When someone asks a present-continuous yes/no question, don't just grunt "yes" or "no" — add the matching **am / is / are** so it sounds friendly and complete.

| Question | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| **Are you** going now? | **Yes, I am.** | **No, I'm not.** |
| **Is** Ben **working** today? | **Yes, he is.** | **No, he isn't.** |
| **Is** she **coming**? | **Yes, she is.** | **No, she isn't.** |
| **Is** it **raining**? | **Yes, it is.** | **No, it isn't.** |
| **Are we winning**? | **Yes, we are.** | **No, we aren't.** |
| **Are** your friends **staying** at a hotel? | **Yes, they are.** | **No, they aren't.** |

<Callout type="tip" title="The verb is enough">
  Notice the answer doesn't repeat the **-ing** verb. *"Is Ben working today?"* → *"Yes, he is."* (not *"Yes, he is working."*) The **is / are** alone carries the whole answer.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="Two ways to say 'no'">
  Same rule as Lesson 1 — both are correct: <br />
  • **No, he isn't.** / **No, she isn't.** / **No, they aren't.** <br />
  • **No, he's not.** / **No, she's not.** / **No, they're not.**
</Callout>

## Now you try: answer about yourself

Look up from your screen. Right this second, what's true? Answer each question out loud with a **short answer** — *Yes, I am.* / *No, I'm not.* — then say a full sentence about what you **are** doing instead.

1. Are you watching TV? &nbsp; → *No, I'm not. I'm using my phone.*
2. Are you wearing a watch?
3. Are you eating something?
4. Is it raining?
5. Are you sitting on the floor?
6. Are you feeling well?

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge">
  Look around you. Pick **three** people or things in the room (or out the window) and ask **three different wh-questions**: one with **What**, one with **Where**, one with **Why**. Say them out loud — that's how the flip becomes automatic. <br /><br />
  *Example:* What is the cat doing? &nbsp;·&nbsp; Where is my brother going? &nbsp;·&nbsp; Why is it raining again?
</Callout>
