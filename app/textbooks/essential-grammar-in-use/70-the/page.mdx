In lesson 69 you saw the basic split — *a* (one of many) vs *the* (the one we both know). Today we widen the *the* family. There's only **one** name of this street, **one** time *now*, **one** sun, **one** police force in your city — so they all take **the**. We also use it for *the same*, for positions (*the top, the middle, the left*), and for instruments (*the piano, the guitar*). And just as importantly: we **drop** the for *TV, breakfast/lunch/dinner, next/last + week*.

## A walk through the only-ones

Listen for **the** in places where there's only one of the thing in the world (or in our shared picture).

## Part A — The "only one" *the*

Use **the** when there's only one of the thing in our shared picture — a name, a time, a position in a building.

- What is **the name** of this street?
- Who is **the best player** in your team?
- Can you tell me **the time**, please? *(= the time now)*
- My office is on **the first floor**.

### Don't forget the:

- Do you live near **the city centre**? *(not near city centre)*
- Excuse me, where is **the nearest bank**? *(not where is nearest …)*

## Part B — *the same …*

A fixed phrase. Always use **the same**.

- We live in **the same street**. *(not in same street)*
- 'Are these two books different?' 'No, they're **the same**.' *(not they're same)*

## Part C — One-of-a-kind *the*

A short list of nouns that are **always** *the*-things: there's only one in the world (or one big shared one).

- **The sky** is blue and **the sun** is shining.
- Do you live in a town or in **the country**?
- My brother is a soldier. He's in **the army**.
- Write your name at **the top of** the page.
- The table is in **the middle of** the room.
- Paula is learning to play **the piano**.
- I listen to **the radio** a lot.
- What do you use **the internet** for?

## Now you try

Write **six** true sentences using *the* (or dropping it!).

1. *I usually have ___ at … o'clock.* (meal — no the)
2. *I'm going on holiday ___ summer.* (next/last — no the)
3. *Can you tell me ___ time, please?*
4. *I live in ___ centre / ___ country.*
5. *I (don't) play ___ guitar / ___ piano.*
6. *We live in ___ same street / age / colour.*
