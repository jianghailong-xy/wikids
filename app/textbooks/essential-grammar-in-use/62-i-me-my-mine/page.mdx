Three lessons in a row, three different shapes of the same pronoun: **I** (lesson 59), **me** (lesson 59), **my** (lesson 60), **mine** (lesson 61). Today we line them up side by side and learn to read **what comes next** in the sentence — a verb, a preposition, a noun, or nothing — to pick the right column. One quick scan, and the right form falls out.

## Four lives of one pronoun

A grandmother watches the news; a couple swap phone numbers. Listen for **all four columns of *I*** lighting up in turn.

<Callout type="tip" title="Read the next word, pick the column">
  *I* + verb. *me* + nothing (it's after the verb or after a preposition). *my* + noun. *mine* — alone. Same idea, four shapes, picked by what's right next to the gap.
</Callout>

### One row, four shapes — *I / me / my / mine*

- **I** know Tom. → **Tom knows me.** → It's **my** car. → It's **mine**.

### A bigger picture

- 'Do **you** know that man?' 'Yes, **I** know **him**, but I can't remember **his** name.'
- **She** was very pleased because **we** invited **her** to stay with **us** at **our** house.
- That's **my** pen. Can **you** give **it** to **me**, please?
- 'Is this **your** umbrella?' 'No, it's **yours**.'
- **He** didn't have an umbrella, so **she** gave **him hers**. *(= her umbrella to him)*
- I'm going out with a friend of **mine** this evening. *(not a friend of me)*

## Part B — A four-step decision

When you see a gap, ask **four quick questions** in order:

      <div className="col-span-4 bg-rose-50 px-4 py-3 text-base font-semibold text-rose-900">→ object (me, us, him…)</div>
    </div>
    <div className="grid bg-white sm:grid-cols-12 items-center">
      <div className="col-span-1 bg-violet-100 px-3 py-3 text-center font-bold text-violet-900">3</div>

      <div className="col-span-4 bg-violet-50 px-4 py-3 text-base font-semibold text-violet-900">→ + noun (my, our, his…)</div>
    </div>

      <div className="col-span-1 bg-pink-100 px-3 py-3 text-center font-bold text-pink-900">4</div>
      <div className="col-span-7 px-4 py-3 text-sm">Possessive but the noun is **missing** at the end?</div>
      <div className="col-span-4 bg-pink-50 px-4 py-3 text-base font-semibold text-pink-900">→ no noun (mine, ours, hers…)</div>
    </div>
  </div>
