You've met **the echo** (lesson 40) and the **reply question** (lesson 41). Today you learn the third trick the same eight little helpers can do — say **"me too" / "me neither"**. Two flavours: the everyday tail (*…too / …either*), and the snappy flip (*So am I / Neither do I*) that swaps the helper to the front. Same helpers; one tiny but slippery word-order rule.

<Callout type="tip" title="One tiny choice">
  *Too* and *either* both mean **"the same goes for me"** — they sit at the **end** of your reply. You only have to remember one thing: was the other person's sentence **positive** or **negative**?
</Callout>

## Trick 1 — too / either at the end

Here's the rule, in one tidy box. The opener picks the word for you.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-teal-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">positive opener → too</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">'I'm happy.' &nbsp; 'I'm happy <strong>too</strong>.'</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">'I enjoyed the film.' &nbsp; 'I enjoyed it <strong>too</strong>.'</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Jane is a doctor. Her husband is a doctor <strong>too</strong>.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-violet-50 to-purple-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">negative opener → either</p>
    <p className="text-base text-violet-900">'I'm not happy.' &nbsp; 'I'm not happy <strong>either</strong>.'</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">'I can't cook.' &nbsp; 'I can't <strong>either</strong>.'</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">Ben doesn't watch TV. He doesn't read newspapers <strong>either</strong>.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="Don't mix them up">
  ❌ I'm not happy too. <br />
  ✅ I'm not happy **either**. <br /><br />
  *Too* and a negative don't get along. The moment you see *not / no / never / can't / doesn't*, switch to **either**.
</Callout>

## Trick 2 — the snappy flip: So am I / Neither do I

Here's the cooler, shorter way to agree. Instead of repeating the whole sentence and tacking *too* on the end, you lead with **so** (for positive) or **neither** (for negative), then **flip** the helper to the front — like a question — and finish with the new subject.

<Callout type="info" title="Two ways to say the same thing">
  *So am I* = *I am too*. <br />
  *Neither have I* = *I haven't either*. <br />
  Same meaning — pick whichever one feels more natural in the moment. The flip version (*So am I*) sounds a touch more lively; the *…too / …either* version is calmer.
</Callout>

## The big trap — word order!

This is **the** mistake to dodge. After *so* or *neither*, you **flip** the helper to the front, just like a question. You **never** say *So I am* or *Neither I have*.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-rose-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">❌ broken (subject first)</p>
    <p className="text-base text-rose-900 line-through">So I am.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">Neither I have.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">So I do.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">Neither I can.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">✅ good (helper first)</p>
    <p className="text-base font-semibold text-emerald-900"><strong>So am I.</strong></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base font-semibold text-emerald-900"><strong>Neither have I.</strong></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base font-semibold text-emerald-900"><strong>So do I.</strong></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base font-semibold text-emerald-900"><strong>Neither can I.</strong></p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="Three slots, one rule">
  After *so* / *neither*, the order is always:<br />
  &nbsp;&nbsp;**so / neither &nbsp; → &nbsp; helper &nbsp; → &nbsp; subject**<br /><br />
  Think of it like a question shape (*Am I? Have I? Do I?*) — but with *so* or *neither* glued in front instead of a question mark on the end.
</Callout>

## Bonus — *Nor* is just a fancy *Neither*

In a few books and a few mouths, you'll meet **Nor** doing exactly the same job as **Neither**. They're interchangeable.

- 'I'm not married.' &nbsp; '**Nor am I.**' &nbsp;*or*&nbsp; '**Neither am I.**'
