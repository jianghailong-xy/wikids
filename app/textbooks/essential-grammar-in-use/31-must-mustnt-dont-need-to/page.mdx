Real life is full of little **rules** — *be on time, don't shout, don't worry about lunch*. English has three small words that turn each rule into a sentence: **must** (you really need to), **mustn't** (it would be wrong to), and **don't need to** (it's not necessary). Today we'll learn all three — plus what happens when the rule was *yesterday*, because **must** has no past form.

## Mia's class trip to the museum

It's Saturday morning. Mia's class is going to a big art museum, and Zoe is doing the last-minute checks before the bus. Listen for **must**, **mustn't**, **don't need to**, and one quick **had to** about something that happened *yesterday*. Tap each bubble to hear it.

  Every rule above followed the same simple pattern: **subject + must / mustn't / don't need to + verb** (no -s, no -ing, no -ed — just the bare verb). *You **must be**. You **mustn't touch**. You **don't need to bring**.* That's the whole grammar.

## must + verb — *I really need to do it*

**Must** is a tiny word that carries a big *need*. After **must**, the verb is in its plain dictionary form — **must do**, **must go**, **must see**, **must eat**. Same shape for everyone, no -s for *he/she/it*.

  <div className="grid grid-cols-[auto_auto_1fr] items-center gap-x-4 gap-y-1 p-5 text-center font-semibold">
    <div className="text-sm text-amber-900">I / we / you / they<br />he / she / it</div>
    <div className="rounded-lg bg-amber-200 px-3 py-2 text-amber-900">must</div>
    <div className="text-left text-amber-900">do<br />go<br />see<br />eat &nbsp;<span className="text-amber-600">etc.</span></div>
  </div>
</div>

**must** = *I need to do it. It's important.*

- I'm very hungry. I **must eat** something.
- It's a fantastic film. You **must see** it.
- The windows are very dirty. We **must clean** them.
- It's late. I **must go** now.

<SayIt text="I must eat something." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="You must see it." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="I must go now." />

<Callout type="info" title="must has no -s, no past, no future">
  Strange but true: **must** is the same for *I, you, he, she, we, they* — no -s on *he/she/it* (✅ *He **must go***, ❌ *He musts go*). And **must** has **no past form**. To talk about *yesterday / last week*, we use **had to** instead — meet it next.
</Callout>

## For yesterday: had to (*not* must)

**Must** lives in the present. As soon as the rule was *yesterday, last night, last week*, swap it for **had to**.

| now / today (must) | yesterday / last week (had to) |
|---|---|
| I'm very hungry. I **must eat** something. | I was very hungry. I **had to eat** something. |
| It's late. We **must walk** home. | There were no buses. We **had to walk** home. |
| I **must run** to catch the bus. | I was nearly late, so I **had to run**. |

<SayIt text="I had to eat something." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="We had to walk home last night." />

<Callout type="warning" title="The 'must yesterday' trap">
  As soon as you hear *yesterday, last night, last week, this morning (already over)*, switch to **had to**. <br />
  ✅ I **had to** walk home last night. &nbsp; ❌ I must walked home last night. &nbsp; ❌ I must walk home last night.
</Callout>

## mustn't + verb — *it would be wrong to do it*

**mustn't** is the short form of **must not**. It doesn't mean *you don't have to* — it means **don't do it, it's the wrong thing.**

> subject + **mustn't** + verb

- I **must go**. I **mustn't be** late.
- I **mustn't forget** to phone Chris. *(= I must remember)*
- Be happy! You **mustn't be** sad. *(= don't be sad)*
- You **mustn't touch** the pictures. *(= don't touch them)*

<SayIt text="I mustn't be late." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="You mustn't touch the pictures." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="I mustn't forget to phone Chris." />

<Callout type="tip" title="mustn't = don't!">
  An easy way to feel **mustn't**: replace it with **don't** in your head. *You **mustn't** shout* → *don't shout*. *I **mustn't forget*** → *don't forget*. If that swap makes sense, **mustn't** is right.
</Callout>

## don't need to + verb — *it's not necessary*

**don't need to** is the gentle one. It doesn't say *don't do it* — it says *you can if you want, but you don't have to*.

> subject + **don't / doesn't** + **need to** + verb

- I **don't need to go** yet. I can stay a little longer.
- You **don't need to shout**. I can hear you OK.
- She **doesn't need to bring** lunch — the school gives one.

You can also say **don't have to** with the same meaning:

- I **don't have to go** yet. I can stay a little longer.
- You **don't have to shout**. I can hear you fine.

<SayIt text="I don't need to go yet." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="You don't have to shout." />

## The big contrast: mustn't vs. don't need to

This pair trips up nearly every learner. They look almost the same, but they say **opposite** things. Read both versions of the same sentence:

    <p className="mb-2 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">mustn't</p>
    <p className="text-center text-sm text-slate-700">It would be <strong>wrong</strong> to do it. <br /><em>(= don't!)</em></p>
    <hr className="my-3 border-rose-200" />
    <p className="my-2 text-center text-base font-semibold text-rose-900">You <strong>mustn't go</strong>.</p>
    <p className="text-center text-sm text-slate-600">You must stay here.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-teal-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">don't need to</p>
    <p className="text-center text-sm text-slate-700">It's <strong>not necessary</strong>. <br /><em>(= but you can if you want)</em></p>
    <hr className="my-3 border-emerald-200" />
    <p className="my-2 text-center text-base font-semibold text-emerald-900">You <strong>don't need to go</strong>.</p>
    <p className="text-center text-sm text-slate-600">You can stay here if you want.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="One word, two opposite worlds">
  *You **mustn't** touch the cake.* → it's the wrong thing to do. **Don't!** <br />
  *You **don't need to** touch the cake.* → it's optional — leave it alone or have a slice, it's up to you.
</Callout>
