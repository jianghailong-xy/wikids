You already know the basic passive: somebody **paints** the door → the door **is painted**. The door is the *star*, and we don't care who's holding the brush. Now we'll add two more passive shapes that English speakers reach for every day: **is being done** (it's happening *right now*) and **has been done** (it's *finished*, and the result is still here).

## Mia comes home

Mia has been at her grandma's for a week. While she was away, her parents started a little house makeover. Some of it is **still going on**, some of it is **already finished**. Walk through the front hallway with her — and listen to the two new shapes appear.

  • **What's happening to it right now?** → *is/are being* + done. *The kitchen is being painted.* <br />
  • **What's already happened to it?** → *has/have been* + done. *The door has been painted.*
</Callout>

## Quick recap: what passive means

The passive flips the spotlight. The active sentence puts the *doer* first; the passive sentence puts the *thing* first — and uses **be + past participle**.

<div className="not-prose my-6 rounded-2xl border-2 border-slate-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-slate-50 to-white p-5 shadow-sm">
  <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-slate-500">the passive recipe</p>
  <p className="mt-3 text-center text-base text-slate-800">
    <strong>subject</strong>
    &nbsp;+&nbsp;
    <strong className="text-emerald-700">be</strong>
    &nbsp;+&nbsp;
    <strong className="text-indigo-700">past participle</strong>
    &nbsp;<em>(painted, broken, stolen, built, made, washed, …)</em>
  </p>
  <p className="mt-3 text-center text-sm text-slate-600">
    Change the form of <strong className="text-emerald-700">be</strong> and you change <em>when</em>. The past participle never moves.
  </p>
</div>

| be-form | shape | when |
|---|---|---|
| **is / are** | the door **is painted** | a general fact (every door in this style) |
| **was / were** | the door **was painted** | finished moment in the past *(Lesson 21)* |
| **is / are being** | the door **is being painted** | happening **right now** |
| **has / have been** | the door **has been painted** | finished — result is **still here** |

## A. is being done — happening right now

This is just the **present continuous** in passive clothes. We swap *am/is/are + verb-ing* for **am/is/are + being + past participle**. The action is in the middle of happening *as we speak*.

    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">active</p>
    <p className="mt-1 text-xl font-bold text-amber-900">Somebody is painting the door.</p>
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-amber-800">The doer (somebody) is the star.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-teal-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">passive</p>
    <p className="mt-1 text-xl font-bold text-emerald-900">The door is being painted.</p>
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-emerald-800">The door is the star — we don't say who's holding the brush.</p>
  </div>
</div>

- My car is at the garage. It **is being repaired**. *(= somebody is repairing it)* <SayIt text="My car is being repaired." iconOnly />
- Some new houses **are being built** opposite the park. *(= somebody is building them)* <SayIt text="Some new houses are being built." iconOnly />
- Don't sit on that bench — it **is being painted**. <SayIt text="Don't sit on that bench, it is being painted." iconOnly />

### *is being done* vs *is done* — don't mix them up

Both use *am/is/are*, but they mean very different things. The *-ing* in **being** is the giveaway: it's *happening this very minute*.

| present **simple** passive — *every day, in general* | present **continuous** passive — *right now* |
|---|---|
| The office **is cleaned** every day. | The office **is being cleaned** at the moment. |
| Football matches **are** often **played** at the weekend. | No matches **are being played** next weekend. |
| English **is spoken** all over the world. | A new agreement **is being signed** as we speak. |

<SayIt text="The office is cleaned every day." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="The office is being cleaned at the moment." />

<Callout type="info" title="The -ing trick">
  Spot the **being**. If you can see *being* between *is/are* and the past participle, the action is happening **now**. No *being*? It's a routine or a general fact.
</Callout>

## B. has been done — finished, with a result you can see

This one is the **present perfect** in passive clothes: **has/have + been + past participle**. Something happened earlier, but the result is still here, right now — the door is green, the key is missing, the shirt smells fresh.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-4 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-amber-50 to-orange-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">active</p>
    <p className="mt-1 text-xl font-bold text-amber-900">Somebody has painted the door.</p>
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-amber-800">We don't know — or care — who.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-teal-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">passive</p>
    <p className="mt-1 text-xl font-bold text-emerald-900">The door has been painted.</p>
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-emerald-800">Result: the door is now a different colour.</p>
  </div>
</div>

- My key **has been stolen**. *(= somebody has stolen it — and I still can't find it)* <SayIt text="My key has been stolen." iconOnly />
- My keys **have been stolen**. *(plural subject → have)* <SayIt text="My keys have been stolen." iconOnly />
- I'm not going to the party. I **haven't been invited**. *(= nobody has invited me)* <SayIt text="I haven't been invited." iconOnly />
- **Has** this shirt **been washed**? *(= has somebody washed it?)* <SayIt text="Has this shirt been washed?" iconOnly />

### *has been done* vs *was done* — same yesterday-trap as before

This is the same rule from Lesson 20, just dressed in passive. **Finished time word** (*yesterday, last week, in 2002*) → past simple passive. **Result-still-here** → present perfect passive.

| present **perfect** passive — result is still here | past **simple** passive — finished moment with a time tag |
|---|---|
| The room isn't dirty any more. It **has been cleaned**. | The room **was cleaned** yesterday. |
| I can't find my keys. I think they **'ve been stolen**. | My keys **were stolen** last week. |
| The bridge is closed — it **has been damaged**. | The bridge **was damaged** in the storm. |

<SayIt text="The room has been cleaned." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="The room was cleaned yesterday." />

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch the time word">
  As soon as you see a **finished time word** (*yesterday, last week, on Friday, in 2002*), use **was/were + done**, not *has/have been done*. <br />
  ✅ My sunglasses **were stolen** at the beach yesterday. &nbsp; ❌ My sunglasses *have been stolen* at the beach yesterday.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="One short test">
  Before you choose, ask: <br />
  • Is it happening **right now**? → *is/are being done*. <br />
  • Is the action **over** but the result is **still here**? → *has/have been done*. <br />
  • Is it tied to a **finished time word** (*yesterday, last week*)? → *was/were done*. <br />
  • Is it a **routine** (*every day, often, usually*)? → *is/are done*.
</Callout>

## Now you try: tour your own room

Look around the room you're in right now. Out loud, say one sentence with each shape — true if you can, made-up if you can't.

1. Something that **is being done** somewhere in the world right now → *"Right now, ___ is being ___ ."*
2. Something in your room that **has been done** recently → *"The ___ has been ___ ."*
3. Something that **is done** every day → *"The ___ is ___ every day."*
4. Something that **was done** yesterday → *"___ was ___ yesterday."*
