When you point at things in English, you have four words to pick from. **this** and **these** for things **near you** (right here, in your hand). **that** and **those** for things **further away** (over there). Singular: **this / that**. Plural: **these / those**. They each travel with a noun (*this picture*) or stand alone (*Who's that?*).

<Callout type="tip" title="Two questions">
  *Near or far?* Near → **this/these**. Far → **that/those**. *One or more than one?* One → **this/that**. More than one → **these/those**.
</Callout>

- Do you like **this picture**? *(= this picture here)*
- Do you like **that picture**? *(= that picture there)*
- **These flowers** are for you. *(= these flowers here)*
- Who are **those people**? *(= those people there)*

## Part C — *That* for what just happened or what someone just said

**That** has a special second job: pointing at the **last thing said or done**.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">that = something that just happened</p>
    <p className="text-base text-amber-900">'I'm sorry I forgot to phone you.' '<strong>That's</strong> all right.'</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-amber-900"><strong>That</strong> was a really nice meal. Thank you very much.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-pink-200 bg-pink-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-pink-700">that = what somebody just said</p>
    <p className="text-base text-pink-900">'You're a teacher, aren't you?' 'Yes, <strong>that's</strong> right.'</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-pink-900">'Martin has a new job.' 'Really? I didn't know <strong>that</strong>.'</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-pink-900">'I'm going on holiday next week.' 'Oh, <strong>that's</strong> nice.'</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences using one of *this/that/these/those* in each.

1. *I love ___ song.* (one playing right now)
2. *Look at ___ stars in the sky.* (far away, plural)
3. *___ is my friend Mai.* (introducing — this!)
4. *I can't believe ___ .* (something that just happened)
5. *Hi, ___ is …* (you on the phone)
6. *Is ___ yours?* (point at one near object)

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — pictures vs words">
  Cover the words and look around the room. Point at one nearby object → say *this is …*. Point at one far object → *that is …*. Pick up two objects close → *these are …*. Look at two far objects → *those are …*. Repeat five times until it feels automatic.
</Callout>
