In lesson 54 you saw *go to + verb* for purpose (*I went to the shop **to** get bread*). Today we zoom out: **go** is one of English's busiest verbs, and the tiny word right after it (*to, on, for*, or **nothing at all**) decides exactly what kind of going you mean. Five little patterns, all using the same verb. Once you know which preposition each activity wants, your *go*-sentences become invisible — they just sound right.

Everyone in the flat is going somewhere this weekend. Listen for the **little word after go** in each line — *to, on, for*, or no preposition at all. Tap any bubble to hear it.

## Part A — *go to* + place

Use **to** when you say **where** you're going — a place name, a building, an event.

## Part B — *go on* + planned activity

Use **on** for organised, packaged activities — things with a beginning and an end, often arranged in advance.

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50">
  <div className="bg-amber-200 px-4 py-2 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-900">go on + a planned event</div>
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-amber-200 sm:grid-cols-3 lg:grid-cols-5">
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-amber-900">go on holiday</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-amber-900">go on a trip</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-amber-900">go on a tour</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-amber-900">go on a cruise</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-amber-900">go on strike</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

- We're **going on holiday** next week.
- Children often **go on school trips**.
- Workers at the airport have **gone on strike**. *(= they are refusing to work)*

## Part C — *go for* + a quick activity (often noun)

Use **for** when you go somewhere **briefly** to do a small activity — usually with a noun like *a walk, a swim, a coffee*.

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50">
  <div className="bg-violet-200 px-4 py-2 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-900">go (somewhere) for + small activity</div>
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-violet-200 sm:grid-cols-3 lg:grid-cols-5">
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">go for a walk</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">go for a run</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">go for a swim</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">go for a drink / coffee</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">go for a meal</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

- 'Where's Emma?' 'She's **gone for a walk**.'
- Do you **go for a run** every morning?
- The water looks nice. I'm **going for a swim**.
- I met Chris in town, so we **went for coffee**.
- Shall we **go out for a meal**? I know a good restaurant.

## Part D — *go + -ing* (sports & shopping)

For most **sports** and the activity **shopping**, drop the preposition entirely. Just *go* + **-ing**.

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-sky-200 bg-sky-50">
  <div className="bg-sky-200 px-4 py-2 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-900">go + -ing — no preposition</div>
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-sky-200 sm:grid-cols-3 lg:grid-cols-6">
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">shopping</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">swimming</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">fishing</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">sailing</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">skiing</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">jogging / riding …</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

- Are you **going shopping** this afternoon?
- It's a nice day. Let's **go swimming**. *(or Let's **go for a swim**.)*
- Richard has a small boat and he often **goes sailing**.
- I **went jogging** before breakfast this morning.

<Callout type="info" title="swim vs go for a swim vs go swimming">
  All three exist! *I'm going to **swim*** (the verb), *I'm going **for a swim*** (a quick dip — focus on the activity), *I'm going **swimming*** (a planned outing — focus on the sport). The last two are interchangeable for everyday talk.
</Callout>

## Now you try: a week's worth of going

Write **six** true sentences about your own week, using a different *go* shape in each one.

1. **go to + place** → *On Monday I usually go to …*
2. **go home** → *Yesterday I went home …*
3. **go on + planned event** → *Next month I'm going on …*
4. **go for + quick activity** → *In the evenings I sometimes go for …*
5. **go + -ing (sport)** → *On weekends I love to go …*
6. **go + -ing (shopping)** → *I usually go … on Saturday morning.*
