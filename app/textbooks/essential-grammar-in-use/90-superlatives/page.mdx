When something beats *all* the others, English makes a **superlative**: *the oldest, the biggest, the most expensive*. Two recipes — same as comparatives, only the tags change. Short word → **the -est** (*old → the oldest, big → the biggest*). Long word → **the most …** (*expensive → the most expensive*). Plus the same three irregulars: *good → the best, bad → the worst, far → the furthest*. And one tiny word that's almost always glued in front: **the**.

<Callout type="tip" title="Comparative vs superlative">
  *bigger / more expensive* — comparing **two**. *the biggest / the most expensive* — top of **all of them**. Always *the* in front.
</Callout>

## Part A — comparative vs superlative

- Box A is **bigger than** Box B. *(comparing two)*
- Box A is **bigger than all the other boxes**. *(still a comparative)*
- Box A is **the biggest** box. *(superlative — top of the group)*

The same with the hotel:

- Europa is **more expensive than** the Grand.
- Europa is **more expensive than** all the other hotels.
- Europa is **the most expensive** hotel in the city.

## Part B — making the superlative

| recipe | adjective | superlative |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| short → the **-est** | old | **the oldest** |
| | cheap | **the cheapest** |
| | nice | **the nicest** |
| | big | **the biggest** *(double consonant)* |
| | hot | **the hottest** |
| -y → **-iest** | easy | **the easiest** |
| | heavy | **the heaviest** |
| | pretty | **the prettiest** |
| long → **the most** … | careful | **the most careful** |
| | expensive | **the most expensive** |
| | interesting | **the most interesting** |
| irregular | good | **the best** |
| | bad | **the worst** |
| | far | **the furthest** |

## Part C — *the …est / the most …*

Always with **the**:

- The church is very old. It's **the oldest building** in the town. *(= older than all the other buildings)*
- What is **the longest river** in the world?
- Money is important, but it isn't **the most important** thing in life.
- Excuse me, where is **the nearest bank**?

## Part D — superlative without a noun

You can drop the noun once it's clear:

- Luke is a good player, but he isn't **the best** in the team. *(the best = the best player)*
- Of all the dishes I tried, this one is **the cheapest**.

## Part E — superlative + *I've ever* / *you've ever*

A common pattern: superlative + present perfect with **ever** (= in your whole life so far):

- The film was very bad. I think it's **the worst film I've ever seen**.
- What is **the most unusual thing you've ever done**?
- It's **the most beautiful place** I've **ever visited**.

## Now you try

Write **six** superlative sentences about your country, your school, or your life.

1. *The ___ city in my country is ___ .*
2. *The ___ river in my country is ___ .*
3. *My ___ is the ___ in my family.*
4. *Today is the ___ day of the week.*
5. *It's the most ___ ___ I've ever ___ .*
6. *My favourite ___ is the ___ in the world.*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — three, not two">
  A superlative needs at least **three** things to compare (top of all the rest). Pick three of anything — three friends, three cities, three foods — and write a sentence that names the **best, biggest, oldest, or most something**. *Of my three best friends, Anna is the funniest.* Repeat with five new groups every day.
</Callout>
