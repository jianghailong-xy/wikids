In English, most things you can count come in two shapes: **one** of it (*a flower*) or **more than one** (*some flowers*). The simple rule is: stick **‑s** on the end. But a handful of nouns squeeze the spelling slightly (*bus → buses, baby → babies, knife → knives*), a smaller handful change shape entirely (*man → men, child → children, mouse → mice*), and a stubborn few are **always plural** (*jeans, scissors, glasses, people*). Today you sort out all three.

## A vase of singulars and plurals

A flower, a vase, some flowers — listen for the **‑s** that turns *one* into *more than one*.

<Callout type="tip" title="Two shapes, one rule (with edge cases)">
  Most nouns: just add **‑s**. A few need **‑es**, **‑ies**, or **‑ves**. A small group changes completely (*man → men*). And a stubborn handful stay singular but mean lots (*sheep, fish*) — or are always plural even though they look like one thing (*jeans, scissors*).
</Callout>

## Part A — The basic plural rule

Singular = one. Plural = two or more. Mostly: just add **‑s**.

## Part B — Always plural

Some clothing and tools are **two halves joined** — they're always plural in English. Use the verb **are**, never *is*. Count them with *a pair of*.

  <div className="bg-violet-200 px-4 py-2 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-900">always plural</div>
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-violet-200 sm:grid-cols-2 lg:grid-cols-4">

    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">trousers</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">jeans</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">shorts</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">tights</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">pyjamas</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

- Do you wear **glasses**?
- Where **are** the **scissors**? I need **them**.
- I need **a new pair of jeans**. *or* I need **some new jeans**. *(not a new jeans)*

## Part C — Irregular plurals (no ‑s)

A short list of nouns that change shape entirely. Memorise these — they show up everywhere.

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-pink-200 bg-pink-50">
  <div className="bg-pink-200 px-4 py-2 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-pink-900">singular → plural (no -s)</div>
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-pink-200 sm:grid-cols-2 lg:grid-cols-3">
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-pink-900">man → men</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-pink-900">woman → women</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-pink-900">child → children</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-pink-900">foot → feet</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-pink-900">tooth → teeth</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-pink-900">mouse → mice</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-pink-900">sheep → sheep</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-pink-900">fish → fish</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-pink-900">person → people</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

- one **foot** → two **feet**
- a **tooth** → all my **teeth**
- a **child** → many **children**
- a **mouse** → some **mice**
- that **sheep** → those **sheep**
- a **fish** → a lot of **fish**
- She's **a nice person**. *but* They're **nice people**. *(not nice persons)*

## Now you try

Write **six** true sentences using a plural noun in each.

1. *In my street there are a lot of ___ .* (people / cars / shops …)
2. *I have ___ in my family.* (brothers / sisters / children)
3. *I'm going to buy a new pair of ___ .* (jeans / shoes / glasses)
4. *Yesterday I cleaned my ___ .* (teeth / shoes / dishes)
5. *I'd like to visit some ___ .* (cities / countries / museums)
6. *Many people in my country ___ .* (verb in plural form)

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — five spelling tweaks">
  Take five everyday singular nouns: *bus, baby, knife, day, party*. Write the plural of each, then say each one out loud. Notice the four different patterns: ‑es, ‑ies, ‑ves, ‑s. Then write a single sentence that uses **all five plurals**. Hard? Try: *On Friday last week I saw two **buses** full of **babies**, three **knives** in the kitchen, six rainy **days** in a row, and two birthday **parties**.*
</Callout>
