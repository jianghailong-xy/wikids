In lesson 65 you met **a/an** — the article that says *one of many*. Today the second article walks in: **the**. The split is simple but huge. **a/an** = *one of many — you don't know which*. **the** = *the one we both know — there's only one in the picture, or we mentioned it already*. Once you can read which kind of "one" you mean, the right article falls out.

## Three windows or one?

A room with three windows — *a window* (which one?). A room with **one** window — *the window* (the only one). Same request, two articles.

### Compare side by side

- I bought **a jacket** and **a shirt**. **The jacket** was cheap, but **the shirt** was expensive.

The first time you mention something, it's *one of many* (**a**). The second time, you both know which one (**the**).

## Part B — *the* for things we share (rooms, houses, towns)

We say **the** when there's **only one** of these in the picture you're sharing.

    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3">
      <p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">of a town</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-sm font-semibold text-amber-900">the centre, the station, the airport, the town hall</p>

- 'Where's Tom?' 'In **the kitchen**.' *(= the kitchen of this house)*
- Turn off **the light** and close **the door**.
- Do you live far from **the centre**?
- I'd like to speak to **the manager**, please.

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences using *a/an* or *the* correctly.

1. *I've got ___ … (introduce something new)*
2. *___ … is/was very nice. (refer back to it)*
3. *Where's …? — In ___ kitchen / garden / bathroom.*
4. *___ … is the only one of its kind. (e.g., the capital, the youngest)*
5. *I'd like to speak to ___ manager.*
6. *I bought ___ … and ___ … . ___ … was cheap.*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — first mention vs second mention">
  Write a four-sentence story. In sentence 1, introduce **two new things** with *a/an*. In sentences 2–4, refer back to them with *the*. Example: <br /><br />
  *Yesterday I bought **a book** and **a pen**. **The book** is about Japan. **The pen** is red. **The book** was cheaper than **the pen**.*
</Callout>
