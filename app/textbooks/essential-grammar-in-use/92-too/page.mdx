**too** = *more than you want, more than is good*. The shoes are *too big*; the music is *too loud*; you put *too much sugar* in the coffee. Same word, three little patterns: **too + adjective/adverb** (*too big, too fast*), **too much / too many + noun** (*too much sugar, too many cars*), and the follow-ups *too … for somebody* and *too … to do something* (note: **to + verb**, never *for + verb*). And don't confuse *too* (= more than enough — bad) with *enough* (= the right amount — good) — they're opposites.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pick the shape">
  **too + adjective/adverb** (*too big, too hard*). **too much + uncountable** (*too much sugar*). **too many + plural** (*too many cars*). After *too …* use *for* + thing/person and *to* + verb.
</Callout>

## Part A — *too + adjective/adverb*

A bigger amount than you want:

- Can you turn the radio down? It's **too loud**. *(= louder than I want)*
- I can't work. I'm **too tired**.
- I think you work **too hard**.
- I'm **too tired** to go out tonight.

## Part B — *too much / too many*

For nouns. **too much** with uncountable, **too many** with plural:

## Part C — Compare *too* and *not enough*

Two opposite faces of *the wrong amount*:

    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">too … (= too much) — bad</p>
    <p className="text-base text-rose-900">The hat is <strong>too big</strong> for him.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900">The music is <strong>too loud</strong>. Can you turn it down?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900">There's <strong>too much sugar</strong> in my coffee.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900">I don't feel very well. I ate <strong>too much</strong>.</p>

  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">not enough (= less than is good) — also bad</p>

    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">The music <strong>isn't loud enough</strong>. Can you turn it up?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">There's <strong>not enough sugar</strong> in my coffee.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">You're very thin. You <strong>don't eat enough</strong>.</p>

## Part D — *too … for / too … to do*

Three follow-up shapes:

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-1">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">too … for somebody/something</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">These shoes are <strong>too big for me</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">It's a small house — <strong>too small for a large family</strong>.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">too … to do something</p>
    <p className="text-base text-violet-900">I'm <strong>too tired to go out</strong>. <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(not for go out)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">It's <strong>too cold to sit</strong> outside.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">too … for somebody to do something</p>
    <p className="text-base text-amber-900">She speaks <strong>too fast for me to understand</strong>.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="to + verb, never for + verb">
  ❌ *too tired for go out.* &nbsp; ✅ *too tired **to go** out.* &nbsp; *for* takes a noun (*for me*); *to* takes a verb (*to go*).
</Callout>

## Now you try

Write **six** *too*-sentences about your life.

1. *Today is too ___ to ___ .*
2. *I have too much ___ .*
3. *There are too many ___ in ___ .*
4. *These ___ are too ___ for me.*
5. *I'm too ___ to ___ now.*
6. *He / She speaks too ___ for me to ___ .*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — too vs enough flip">
  Pick five things in your day that aren't quite right. Write each one **twice** — once with *too* (= too much, bad) and once with *not enough* (= not enough, also bad). *The coffee is too cold.* / *The coffee isn't hot enough.* Same complaint, opposite shape. Once you can flip on demand, both patterns belong to you.
</Callout>
