Four little time-joiners. They split into two pairs.

> **before** the film &nbsp; **during** the film &nbsp; **after** the film <br />
> **before** we played &nbsp; **while** we were playing &nbsp; **after** we played

The first pair (*before / during / after*) takes a **noun** behind it. The second pair (*before / while / after*) takes a **verb**. Pick the one that fits what you've got.

## Part A — *before*, *during*, *after*

Three moments around an event:

  <div className="grid gap-px bg-sky-200 sm:grid-cols-3">

      <p className="text-2xl">🎟️</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">before the film</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-xs text-slate-600">buying tickets</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-2xl">🎬</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">during the film</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-xs text-slate-600">watching</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-2xl">🚪</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">after the film</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-xs text-slate-600">leaving</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

- Everybody feels nervous **before exams**.
- I fell asleep **during the film**.
- We were tired **after our visit** to the museum.

## Part B — *before*, *while*, *after*

Same three moments, but now connected by a verb:

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-emerald-200 sm:grid-cols-3">
    <div className="bg-white p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-2xl">🎾</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">before we played</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-2xl">🤾</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">while we were playing</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4 text-center">
      <p className="text-2xl">🥤</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">after we played</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

- Don't forget to close the window **before you go out**.
- I often fall asleep **while I'm reading**.
- They went home **after they did the shopping**.

## Part C — *during* + noun &nbsp; vs &nbsp; *while* + verb

A super common mix-up. The two words mean almost the same — but they take different shapes after them.

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-amber-200 sm:grid-cols-2">
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">during + noun</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-base font-semibold text-amber-900">We didn't speak <strong>during the meal</strong>.</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-center text-xs italic text-slate-600">during + thing</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">while + verb</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-base font-semibold text-amber-900">We didn't speak <strong>while we were eating</strong>.</p>
      <p className="mt-1 text-center text-xs italic text-slate-600">while + subject + verb</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="Never *during we were eating*">
  ❌ *We didn't speak **during** we were eating.* <br />
  ✅ *We didn't speak **while** we were eating.* <br />
  *during* takes a noun; *while* takes a clause (subject + verb).
</Callout>

## Part D — for a **length**, use *for*, not *during*

For a clean stretch of time (*two hours, a year, three days*), use **for** — not *during*.

| ❌ broken | ✅ fixed |
|---|---|
| We played tennis during two hours. | We played tennis **for two hours**. |
| I lived in London during a year. | I lived in London **for a year**. |
| Gary stayed during three days. | Gary stayed **for three days**. |

<SayIt text="We played tennis for two hours." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="I lived in London for a year." />

## Part E — *before / after* + **-ing**

When the same person does both actions, you can swap *before/after + I/we/they* for *before/after + -ing*. Same meaning, shorter shape.

| Long shape | Short shape |
|---|---|
| I always have breakfast **before I go** to work. | I always have breakfast **before going** to work. |
| **After they did** the shopping, they went home. | **After doing** the shopping, they went home. |
| **Before I eat** the apple, I wash it. | **Before eating** the apple, I wash it. |
| I started work **after I read** the newspaper. | I started work **after reading** the newspaper. |

<Callout type="warning" title="-ing, not *to + verb*">
  ❌ *before to go* / *after to do* <br />
  ✅ *before going* / *after doing* <br />
  Always **-ing** after *before* / *after* — never the *to + verb* shape.
</Callout>

<SayIt text="I always have breakfast before going to work." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="After doing the shopping, they went home." />

## Now you try

Finish each sentence about your own life. Pick the right joiner.

1. *I usually feel tired **after** ___ .*
2. *I learnt a lot **during** ___ .*
3. *I never check my phone **while** ___ .*
4. *I always wash my hands **before** ___ -ing.*

<Callout type="tip" title="One-sentence summary">
  Look at what comes **after** the joiner. Noun → *during / before / after*. Verb-clause → *while / before / after*. A pure length of time → *for*. Same person, two actions → *before / after + -ing*.
</Callout>
