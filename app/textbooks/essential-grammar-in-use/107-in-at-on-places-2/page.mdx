Unit 106 set up the big idea: **in** = inside, **at** = a point, **on** = a surface. Today's lesson is the **everyday list** — the fixed phrases native speakers use without thinking. Some of them aren't logical. Just learn the partner.

## Part A — *in* phrases

Some of these look like clear "inside" phrases (*in bed, in a car*). Others are fixed (*in the sky, in a photo, in the middle*).

| in + place | example |
|---|---|
| **in bed** | 'Where's Kate?' '**She's in bed.**' |
| **in hospital** | David's father is ill. He's **in hospital**. |
| **in the sky** | I like to look at the stars **in the sky** at night. |
| **in the world** | What's the largest city **in the world**? |
| **in a newspaper / in a book** | I read about the accident **in the newspaper**. |
| **in a photo / in a picture** | You look sad **in this picture**. |
| **in a car / in a taxi** | Did you come here **in your car**? |
| **in the middle (of …)** | There's a big tree **in the middle of the garden**. |

<Callout type="info" title="*in hospital* = ill, not visiting">
  British English uses *in hospital* / *in prison* / *at university* without an article when you mean **as a patient / a prisoner / a student**: *He's in hospital* (he's ill). <br />
  Add **a** / **the** when you mean the building itself: *I work in a hospital* (a job there).
</Callout>

## Part B — *at* phrases

**at** points to a building or an event you're **inside**, but treated as a **point** on the map. The list to memorize:

| at + place | example |
|---|---|
| **at home** | Will you be **at home** this evening? |
| **at work / at school** | 'Where's Kate?' 'She's **at work**.' |
| **at university / at college** | Helen is studying law **at university**. |
| **at the station / at the airport** | I'll meet you **at the station**, OK? |
| **at Lisa's / at my sister's / at the doctor's / at the hairdresser's** | I saw Tom **at the doctor's**. |
| **at a concert / at a party / at a football match** | There weren't many people **at the party**. |

### *at* or *in* — both are OK for some buildings

Hotels, restaurants, museums and shops can take **at** or **in**:

- We stayed **at a nice hotel**. *or* &nbsp; We stayed **in a nice hotel**.
- We had dinner **at a small Italian restaurant**. *or* &nbsp; … **in a small Italian restaurant**.

Use **at** when you mean the **occasion / address**; use **in** when you mean **inside the building**.

## Part C — *on* phrases

**on** travels with vehicles you **board** (you walk inside, then sit) — and with **floors** of a building, and with the idea of **being on the way**.

| on + thing | example |
|---|---|
| **on a bus / on a train / on a plane / on a ship** | Did you come here **on the bus**? |
| **on the ground / first / second floor** | The office is **on the first floor**. |
| **on the way (to …) / on the way home** | I met Anna **on the way to work** / **on the way home**. |

<Callout type="warning" title="The car is the odd one out">
  Vehicles you **walk into and stand or sit in** take **on**: *on a bus, on a train, on a plane, on a ship*. <br />
  But cars and taxis take **in**: *in a car, in a taxi*. <br />
  *Did you come **on the bus** or **in your car**?*
</Callout>

## Now you try

Finish each sentence about your own life.

1. *I'm sitting **at / in / on** ___ right now.*
2. *I usually go to school **on / in** ___ .*
3. *I last had dinner **at** ___ .*
4. *Right now, my friends are **at / in / on** ___ .*

<Callout type="tip" title="Master list — three columns">
  **in:** in bed / hospital / the sky / a newspaper / a photo / a car / a taxi / the middle. <br />
  **at:** at home / work / school / university / the station / the airport / the doctor's / a party / a concert. <br />
  **on:** on a bus / train / plane / ship / the first floor / the way home. <br />
  And the trap: *in a car / in a taxi*, but *on a bus / on a train / on a bicycle*.
</Callout>
