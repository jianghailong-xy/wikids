In Lessons 1 and 2 you learned to say **who** someone is and how to **ask** about them. Now we level up: instead of just saying who someone is, we'll say what they are **doing right this second**. English calls this the **present continuous**.

## The pattern: am/is/are + -ing

You already know **am / is / are** from Lesson 1. To say what's happening **right now**, take the verb, glue **-ing** onto the end, and stick it after **am / is / are**:

> **am / is / are** + verb-**ing**

<Conjugation ending=" eating dinner." />

| Subject | + be | + verb-ing |
|---|---|---|
| **I** | **am** | working |
| **He / She / It** | **is** | working |
| **We / You / They** | **are** | working |

Tap to hear all three:

<SayIt text="I am working." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="She is reading a book." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="They are playing football." />

<Callout type="warning" title="Both halves, always">
  The two pieces — **be** + **-ing** — must travel together. Drop one and the sentence breaks. <br />
  ✅ She **is reading**. &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ She reading. &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ She is read.
</Callout>

## What does it mean? Right now.

The present continuous is your "live commentary" tense. It's for things happening **at this very moment**, while you're speaking.

<div className="not-prose my-6 rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-teal-50 p-5 text-center shadow-sm">
  <div className="flex items-center justify-between text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-slate-500">
    <span>past</span>
    <span className="text-emerald-700">now</span>
    <span>future</span>
  </div>
  <div className="my-3 h-1 w-full rounded-full bg-gradient-to-r from-slate-200 via-emerald-400 to-slate-200" />
  <p className="text-base font-semibold text-emerald-800">↑ <em>am/is/are + -ing</em> lives here</p>
</div>

- "Please be quiet — **I'm working**." → I am working *right now*.
- "Look, there's Sarah. **She's wearing** a brown coat." → on her body *right now*.
- "**It's not raining** any more." → not at this very minute.
- "Where are the kids?" "**They're playing** in the park." → out there now.

<Callout type="info" title="Compare with Lesson 1">
  - **She is** a doctor. → an always-true fact about Sarah (Lesson 1) <br />
  - **She is wearing** a coat. → happening *right now* (this lesson)
</Callout>

## Spelling: how to make the -ing form

Most verbs just take **-ing** with no fuss:

- eat → eat**ing** &nbsp;·&nbsp; play → play**ing** &nbsp;·&nbsp; read → read**ing** &nbsp;·&nbsp; watch → watch**ing**

But three little tricks are worth knowing.

### Rule 1 — verb ends in **-e**? Drop the **e**.

<Contraction
  caption="come → coming"
  segments={[
    { keep: "com" },
    { swap: "e", to: "ing" },
  ]}
/>

The same trick works for **write → writing**, **dance → dancing**, **make → making**, and **live → living**.

### Rule 2 — short verb ending in **consonant + vowel + consonant**? Double the last letter.

<Contraction
  caption="run → running"
  segments={[
    { keep: "ru" },
    { swap: "n", to: "nning" },
  ]}
/>

Same with **sit → sitting**, **swim → swimming**, **stop → stopping**, **get → getting**.

### Rule 3 — the special one: **lie → lying**

<Contraction
  caption="lie → lying"
  segments={[
    { keep: "l" },
    { swap: "ie", to: "ying" },
  ]}
/>

The **ie** turns into **y** before **-ing**. The same thing happens with **die → dying** and **tie → tying**.

## Saying "no" — the negative

To say something is **not** happening, drop **not** between the **be** and the **-ing** — exactly the same trick as Lesson 1.

- I'**m not** watching TV. <SayIt text="I'm not watching TV." iconOnly />
- She **isn't** eating. <SayIt text="She isn't eating." iconOnly />
- The sun **isn't** shining. <SayIt text="The sun isn't shining." iconOnly />
- They **aren't** walking. <SayIt text="They aren't walking." iconOnly />

<Callout type="info" title="Two ways to say 'no'">
  Just like Lesson 1, **she is not eating** can shrink two ways: <br />
  • **She isn't eating.** &nbsp; • **She's not eating.** <br />
  Both are correct — pick the one that feels easy to say.
</Callout>
