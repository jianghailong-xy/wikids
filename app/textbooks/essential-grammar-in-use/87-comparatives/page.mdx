When two things or people aren't quite equal, English makes a **comparative**: *older, heavier, more expensive*. Two recipes — pick by **counting syllables**. Short word (1 syllable) → stamp on **-er** (*old → older, slow → slower, big → bigger*). Long word (2/3/4 syllables) → put **more** in front (*careful → more careful, expensive → more expensive*). And four classics break the rules: *good → better, bad → worse, far → further*.

<Callout type="tip" title="Count the syllables">
  **1 syllable** → adjective + **-er** (old**er**, slow**er**, big**ger**). **2+ syllables** → **more** + adjective (more careful, more expensive). Words ending in **-y** are special: drop the y, add **-ier** (eas**y** → eas**ier**, heav**y** → heav**ier**).
</Callout>

## Part A — what is a comparative?

*Older / heavier / more expensive* are **comparative** forms. We use them when one thing has more of a quality than the other.

- The 93-year-old is **older** than the 92-year-old.
- A 50 kg weight is **heavier** than a 40 kg weight.
- The £120 shoes are **more expensive** than the £105 shoes.

## Part B — short words: + **-er**

| adjective | comparative |
|-----------|-------------|
| old | old**er** |
| slow | slow**er** |
| cheap | cheap**er** |
| nice | nic**er** *(just -r after -e)* |
| late | lat**er** |
| big | bi**gg**er *(short vowel + 1 consonant → double it)* |
| hot | ho**tt**er |
| thin | thi**nn**er |

### Words ending in **-y** → **-ier**

- eas**y** → eas**ier**
- heav**y** → heav**ier**
- earl**y** → earl**ier**
- happ**y** → happ**ier**
- prett**y** → prett**ier**

### Examples

- Rome is **old**, but Athens is **older**. *(not more old)*
- Is it **cheaper** to go by car or by train? *(not more cheap)*
- Helen wants a **bigger** car.
- This coat is OK, but I think the other one is **nicer**.
- Don't take the bus. It's **easier** to take a taxi. *(not more easy)*

### *far → further*

- A: How far is it to the station? A mile?
- B: No, it's **further**. About two miles.

## Part C — long words: **more** + adjective

For 2/3/4 syllable words, put **more** in front (no -er):

| adjective | comparative |
|-----------|-------------|
| careful | **more** careful |
| polite | **more** polite |
| expensive | **more** expensive |
| interesting | **more** interesting |
| serious | **more** serious |
| modern | **more** modern |

- You must be **more careful**.
- I don't like my job. I want to do something **more interesting**.
- Is it **more expensive** to go by car or by train?

<Callout type="warning" title="Never both">
  ❌ *more older / more bigger / more easier*. Only one tag at a time — either *-er* **or** *more*, never both. ✅ *older*, *bigger*, *easier*.
</Callout>

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences using a comparative.

1. *My ___ is ___ than my ___ .* (-er)
2. *I think ___ is more ___ than ___ .* (more)
3. *Today the weather is ___ than yesterday.* (better/worse)
4. *It's much ___ to ___ than to ___ .* (-er)
5. *I want a ___ ___ .* (-er + noun)
6. *Math is ___ than history.* (more …)

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — count first, write second">
  Before you write, **clap the syllables**. *Big* → 1 clap → *bigger*. *Mod-ern* → 2 claps → *more modern*. *Ex-pen-sive* → 3 claps → *more expensive*. The clap rule never fails — except for the four irregulars: *good → better, bad → worse, far → further, well → better*.
</Callout>
