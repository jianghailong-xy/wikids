In lesson 66 you saw that nouns come in **one** and **more than one**. Today we add a second split that's just as important: some nouns can be **counted** at all (*one bottle, two bottles*) — others cannot (*one water* ❌). The countable ones take *a/an* and a plural. The uncountable ones (water, money, music, salt) have **only one form** and never take *a/an*. To count an uncountable, you put it in a container: *a bottle of water, a piece of cheese, a cup of coffee*.

## Two kinds of shopping

A bag full of bottles, a wallet full of money, music in the air. Listen for the difference between *bottles* (you can count) and *water / music / money* (you can't).

<Callout type="tip" title="Two questions to ask">
  When a noun lands in your sentence, ask: **(1) Can I put a number in front?** (one bottle, two bottles → countable). **(2) If not, can I package it?** (a bottle of water, a piece of cheese → uncountable).
</Callout>

## Part A — Countable nouns

You can put **one, two, three** in front of countable nouns. They have a singular and a plural.

- I've got **a car**.
- New **cars** are very expensive.
- There aren't **many cars** in the car park.

<Callout type="warning" title="No bare singular">
  ❌ *We can't get into the house without key.* <br />
  ✅ We can't get into the house **without a key**. <br />
  Singular countable nouns **always** need *a/an, the, my* etc. — never alone.
</Callout>

## Part B — Uncountable nouns

You **can't** put a number in front. They don't have a plural — just one form.

## Part C — Package them: *a bottle of …*

You can't say *a water* — but you can put it in a **container** or **piece**:

<Callout type="info" title="Package by the right container">
  Liquids → *a bottle of, a glass of, a cup of, a carton of*. Solids → *a piece of, a bar of, a loaf of, a slice of*. Activities → *a game of, a piece of*.
</Callout>

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences about your kitchen or fridge. Use three countable nouns (with *a/an* or numbers) and three uncountable nouns (no *a/an* — package them if needed).

1. *I have a/an ___ in my fridge.*
2. *There are ___ ___ on the table.* (number + plural)
3. *I need ___ ___ .* (a + container + uncountable)
4. *I don't have much ___ today.* (uncountable)
5. *Would you like some ___ ?* (uncountable or plural)
6. *Money is ___ .* (an opinion — no *a* before money)
