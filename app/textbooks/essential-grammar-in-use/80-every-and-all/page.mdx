There are two ways to say *100%* in English. **every** picks up each item one by one — *every house, every country, every time* — and uses a **singular** noun and a **singular verb**. **all** sweeps them up as a group — *all the houses, all the students* — and uses a **plural** noun and verb. They mean the same thing, but they don't take the same shape.

<Callout type="tip" title="One word, one verb">
  *every* + **singular noun** + **singular verb** (every house *is*, every country *has*). *all* + **plural noun** + plural verb (all the houses *are*).
</Callout>

## Part A — *every + singular noun*

- Sarah has been to **every country** in Europe.
- **Every summer** we have a holiday by the sea.
- She looks different **every time** I see her.

### Singular verb after *every*

- **Every house** in the street **is** the same. *(not are)*
- **Every country has** a national flag. *(not have)*

## Part B — *every day* vs *all day*

These look similar but mean **completely different things**.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">every day = on all days (how often?)</p>
    <p className="text-base text-amber-900">It rained <strong>every day</strong> last week. <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat, Sun)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-amber-900">Ben watches TV for about two hours <strong>every evening</strong>.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-pink-200 bg-pink-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-pink-700">all day = the complete day (how long?)</p>
    <p className="text-base text-pink-900">It rained <strong>all day</strong> yesterday. <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(from morning to night, that one day)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-pink-900">On Monday, I watched TV <strong>all evening</strong>.</p>
  </div>
</div>

Same trick with **morning, night, summer, week**: *every morning* vs *all morning*; *every summer* vs *all summer*.

## Part C — *everybody / everything / everywhere*

Add **every-** to *-body / -thing / -where* and you get the 100% version of lesson 79's grid:

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-sky-200 bg-sky-50">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-sky-200 sm:grid-cols-3">
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">everybody / everyone (people)</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-sky-900">all people</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">everything (things)</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-sky-900">all things</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">everywhere (places)</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-sky-900">in/to all places</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

- **Everybody (or Everyone)** needs friends. *(= all people need friends)*
- Do you have **everything** you need? *(= all the things you need)*
- I lost my watch. I've looked **everywhere** for it. *(= in all places)*

### Singular verb after *every-*

- **Everybody has** problems. *(not Everybody have)*
- **Everyone is** ready. *(not everyone are)*
- **Everything was** OK. *(not everything were)*

## Now you try

Write **six** true sentences using *every*, *all*, or *every-/all-* + word.

1. *I drink coffee every ___ .*
2. *Yesterday I worked all ___ .*
3. *Every ___ in my class … .*
4. *Everybody in my family ___ .* (singular verb)
5. *I've been everywhere in ___ .*
6. *Everything in this room ___ .*
