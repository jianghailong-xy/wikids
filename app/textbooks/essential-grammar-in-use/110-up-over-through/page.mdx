## Part A — *to* / *from* / *into* / *out of*

- Jane is going **to France** next week.
- We walked **from the hotel to the station**.
- A lot of English words come **from Latin**.
- We jumped **into the water**.
- A man came **out of the house** and got **into a car**.
- Why are you looking **out of the window**?
- I took the old batteries **out of the radio**.

<Callout type="warning" title="*put* takes *in*, not *into*">
  ❌ *I put new batteries **into** the radio.* <br />
  ✅ *I put new batteries **in** the radio.* <br />
  Most movement verbs use *into*, but *put* prefers a plain *in*.
</Callout>

## Part B — *on* / *off* / *up* / *down*

- Don't put your feet **on the table**.
- Please take your feet **off the table**.
- I'm going to hang some pictures **on the wall**.
- Be careful! Don't fall **off your bike**.
- We got **on the bus** in Princes Street.
- We walked **up the hill** to the house.
- Be careful! Don't fall **down the stairs**.

<SayIt text="We walked up the hill to the house." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="Don't fall off your bike." />

## Part C — *over* / *under* / *through* / *round*

- The plane flew **over the mountains**.
- I jumped **over the wall** into the garden.
- Some people say it is unlucky to walk **under a ladder**.
- A bird flew into the room **through a window**.
- The old road goes **through the village**.
- The new road goes **round the village**.
- The bus stop is just **round the corner**.
- I walked **round the town** and took some photographs.

You can also say **around** (= round):

- We walked **around the town**.

## Part D — *along* / *across* / *past*

- I was walking **along the road** with my dog.
- Let's go for a walk **along the river**.
- The dog swam **across the river**.
- They walked **past me** without speaking.
- 'Excuse me, how do I get to the hospital?' 'Go **along this road**, **past the cinema**, **under the bridge**, and the hospital is on the left.'

## Now you try

Picture the route from your home to school or work. Describe it using **at least four** of these path-words:

> *along, past, across, through, round, over, up, down*

Example: *I walk **along** my street, **past** the bakery, **across** the main road, and **up** a small hill.*

<Callout type="tip" title="One-sentence summary">
  Position-prepositions place a thing; **path-prepositions draw a movement**: *to / from*, *into / out of*, *on / off*, *up / down*, *over / under*, *through / round*, *along / across*, *past*. Watch the trap: *put* takes a plain *in* — never *into*.
</Callout>
