You met **have / have got** all the way back in lesson 9 — the possession kind (*I've got a car, she has long hair*). Today we add the second life of *have*: the *do/take* kind. *Have breakfast, have a shower, have a good time, have a baby* — these don't mean *own*; they mean **eat / drink / take / experience**. And — important — they **never** swap to *have got*.

## Two lives, one verb

Sue and Joel are catching up on the phone. Listen for both flavours of *have*: the **own** kind (*I've got a cold*) and the **do/take** kind (*'m having lunch*).

<Callout type="tip" title="Two flavours, one verb">
  *I've got a cold* (= it's mine — possession). *I'm having lunch* (= I'm eating it now — action). Same verb, different jobs. The action kind never says *I've got lunch*.
</Callout>

## Part A — *have* and *have got* (= it is mine)

When *have* means **own / possess**, you can swap it freely with **have got**.

- I **have** a headache. *or* I**'ve got** a headache.
- **Do** you **have** a cold? *or* **Have** you **got** a cold?

### The past — *had* (no *got*)

For yesterday, drop the *got*. Use **had / didn't have / Did you have ?**.

- When I first met Sue, she **had** short hair.
- He **didn't have** any money because he **didn't have** a job.
- **Did** you **have** enough time to do everything you wanted?

## Part B — *have* = eat / drink / take / experience

Here comes the second life of *have*. With these expressions, *have* means **do** or **take** the thing — and **have got is wrong**.

    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-amber-900">have a cup of coffee / a glass of milk</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-amber-900">have something to eat / drink</p></div>

- 'Where's Lisa?' '**She's having** lunch.'
- I **don't usually have** breakfast.
- I **had** three cups of coffee this morning.
- '**Have** a biscuit!' 'Oh, thank you.'

- I **had** a shower this morning.
- We**'re having** a party next week. You must come.
- Enjoy your holiday. **Have a nice time**!
- **Did** you **have** a good time in Tokyo?
- Sandra **has just had** a baby.
- Can I **have a look** at your magazine?

## Part C — Compare: *have got* vs *have* (do/take)

The same noun can take both — but the meaning shifts.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">it is mine — both work</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">I <strong>have</strong> / I<strong>'ve got</strong> a new shower. It's very good.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-rose-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">do/take — only <em>have</em></p>
    <p className="text-base text-rose-900">I <strong>have</strong> a shower every morning.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-xs italic text-slate-500">(not <em>I've got a shower every morning</em>)</p>
    <p className="mt-3 text-base text-rose-900">A: Where's Paul? B: He<strong>'s having</strong> a shower. <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(= washing now)</span></p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="info" title="Two-step test">
  Ask yourself: **Is it mine?** → either *have* or *have got* works. **Am I doing/taking it?** → only *have*, and you can put it in the **-ing** form (*'m having a shower / lunch / a great time*) when it's happening right now.
</Callout>

## Now you try

Write **six** true sentences about you. Three with *have / have got* (own) and three with *have* (do/take).

1. *I'**ve got** …* (a thing of yours)
2. *I **don't have** …* (something you wish you owned)
3. *Yesterday I **had** …* (an ache or illness — past)
4. *Tomorrow I'm going to **have** …* (a meal or drink)
5. *In the evenings I sometimes **have** …* (a shower / a walk / a rest)
6. *Last weekend I **had** …* (a great time / a holiday / a party)

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — 'have got'-proof your week">
  Look at your six sentences. For each one, try swapping in *have got*. The first three should sound fine. The last three should sound wrong (*I'm having got a shower* — nope!). That mismatch is the whole lesson in one trick.
</Callout>
