Adjectives are the describing words: *nice, old, brown, hungry, beautiful*. They sit in **two slots** only: (1) **before** a noun (*a **nice** day, **brown** eyes*); (2) **after** the verb *be* — and the small family *look, feel, smell, taste, sound* (*The weather **is nice**. You **look tired**. It **smells good**.*). And the ending **never changes** — *a different place* and *different places*, never *differents*.

<Callout type="tip" title="Two slots, one shape">
  An adjective sits **before a noun** (*a modern house*) or **after be / look / feel / smell / taste / sound* (*The house is modern*). Its ending **never changes**: *a different place, different places* (not *differents*).
</Callout>

## Part A — adjective + noun

The adjective goes **in front of** the noun:

- It's a **nice day** today.
- Laura has **brown eyes**.
- There's a very **old bridge** in this village.
- Do you like **Italian food**?
- I don't speak any **foreign languages**.
- There are some **beautiful yellow flowers** in the garden.

<Callout type="warning" title="Word order, no -s">
  ❌ *They live in a house modern.* &nbsp; ✅ They live in a **modern house**. <br />
  ❌ *Have you met any people famous?* &nbsp; ✅ Have you met any **famous people**? <br />
  ❌ *I went to differents places.* &nbsp; ✅ *a **different** place / **different** places* — the adjective never adds -s.
</Callout>

## Part B — *be* + adjective

After **am / is / are / was / were**, drop the noun and put the adjective straight after:

- The weather **is nice** today.
- These flowers **are very beautiful**.
- **Are you cold**? Shall I close the window?
- I**'m hungry**. Can I have something to eat?
- The film **wasn't very good**. It **was boring**.
- Please **be quiet**. I'm reading.

## Part C — *look / feel / smell / taste / sound* + adjective

The 'sense' verbs work like *be*: an adjective comes **straight after** them — **no -ly**.

<Callout type="warning" title="Adjective, not adverb">
  After *be / look / feel / smell / taste / sound* the word that follows is an **adjective**, not an *-ly* adverb. ❌ *You look tiredly.* &nbsp; ✅ *You **look tired**.* (The adverb form *quickly / badly / nicely* belongs with **action** verbs — see lesson 86.)
</Callout>

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences using adjectives in different slots.

1. *I live in a ___ ___ .* (adjective + noun)
2. *My best friend is very ___ .* (be + adjective)
3. *Today the weather is ___ .*
4. *You look ___ .* (talk to a friend)
5. *This ___ smells / tastes ___ .*
6. *I have ___ ___ eyes.* (colour + noun)

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — adjective vs adverb (preview lesson 86)">
  Two near-twin sentences: *Sue is very **quiet**.* (adjective, after *be*) vs *Sue speaks very **quietly**.* (adverb, after the action *speak*). After *be / look / feel / smell / taste / sound*, use the adjective. After most other verbs, the adverb (-ly) shape is the one. Lesson 86 makes this swap your superpower.
</Callout>
