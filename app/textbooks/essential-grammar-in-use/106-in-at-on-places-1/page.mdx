## Part A — *in*

**in** = inside something **or** inside an area. Picture yourself **surrounded** by it.

| in + small thing | in + bigger area |
|---|---|
| in a room | in a garden |
| in a shop | in a town |
| in a car | in the city centre |
| in the water | in Brazil |

- 'Where's David?' '**In the kitchen.** / **In the garden.** / **In London.**'
- What's **in that box** / **in that bag** / **in that cupboard**?
- Rachel works **in a shop** / **in a bank** / **in a factory**.
- I went for a swim **in the river** / **in the pool** / **in the sea**.
- Milan is **in the north of Italy**. Naples is **in the south**.
- I live **in a big city**, but I'd like to live in the country.

## Part B — *at*

**at** = a **point** or a **specific position**. Think of a pin on a map.

| at + position |
|---|
| at the bus stop |
| at the door |
| at the traffic lights |
| at her desk |
| at the top (of the page) |
| at the bottom (of the page) |
| at the end (of the street) |

- There's somebody **at the bus stop** / **at the door**.
- The car is waiting **at the traffic lights**.
- Vicky is working **at her desk**.
- Write your name **at the top of the page**.
- My house is **at the end of the street**.

<Callout type="info" title="*at* vs *in*">
  *at* zooms in on a **point**: *standing at the door* (right by the door). <br />
  *in* steps **inside**: *standing in the doorway / in the kitchen* (inside the space). <br />
  Same with cities: *I'll meet you at the station* (the spot) — *I live in London* (the area).
</Callout>

## Part C — *on*

**on** = touching a **surface**. Floor, wall, ceiling, shelf, page, body of water — any surface counts.

| on + surface |
|---|
| on a shelf |
| on a plate |
| on a balcony |
| on the floor |
| on a wall |
| on a door |
| on the ceiling |
| on the grass |
| on the envelope |

- There are some books **on the shelf** and some pictures **on the wall**.
- There are a lot of apples **on those trees**.
- Don't sit **on the grass**. It's wet.
- There is a stamp **on the envelope**.

### *on* a horse / a bicycle / a motorbike

Things you **sit astride** also take **on**:

- Who is that man **on the motorbike**?
- He's **on his bicycle**.
- A cowboy **on a horse**.

## Compare: same place, different angle

The same noun can take a different preposition depending on where you stand:

| word | shape |
|---|---|
| **in** the bus | *(passenger sitting inside)* |
| **on** the bus | *(travelling on it — see Unit 107)* |
| **at** the door | *(standing right by it)* |
| **in** the doorway | *(inside the frame)* |
| **on** the door | *(a sign hangs on it)* |
| **in** the corner of the room | *(inside, the corner is part of the room)* |
| **at** the corner of the street | *(an outside meeting-point)* |

## Now you try

Finish each sentence about your own life.

1. *I live **in** ___ .*
2. *Right now I am sitting **at / in / on** ___ .*
3. *There's a picture **on** ___ .*
4. *The shops are **at / in** ___ .*

<Callout type="tip" title="One-sentence summary">
  **in** = inside something or inside an area (*in a room, in Brazil*). <br />
  **at** = a point or a position (*at the door, at the bus stop, at the end of the street*). <br />
  **on** = touching a surface (*on the wall, on the floor, on a horse*).
</Callout>
