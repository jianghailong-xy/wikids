You've now seen *in / at / on* for **time** (Unit 103) and **place** (Units 106–108). Today's prepositions are the everyday **list** — fixed phrases with five tiny words: **on, at, by, with, about**.

## Part A — *on* (the everyday list)

A small bundle of nouns that always pair with **on**:

| on + noun | example |
|---|---|
| **on holiday** | Jane isn't at work this week. She's **on holiday**. |
| **on television / on TV** | We watched the news **on TV**. |
| **on the radio** | We listened to the news **on the radio**. |
| **on the phone** | I spoke to Rachel **on the phone** last night. |
| **on fire** | The house is **on fire**! Call the fire brigade. |
| **on time** *(= not late)* | 'Was the train late?' 'No, it was **on time**.' |

<SayIt text="She's on holiday." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="The train was on time." />

## Part B — *at* (numbers / measurements)

Use **at** with a precise number — an age, a speed, a temperature, a price:

- Lisa got married **at 21**. *(or … at the age of 21)*
- A car uses more petrol **at 120 kilometres an hour** than **at 90**.
- Water boils **at 100 degrees Celsius**.

<Callout type="info" title="Speed, temperature, age — all *at*">
  English locks all measured numbers to **at**: *at 100 km/h, at zero degrees, at the age of 30, at $50 a kilo*.
</Callout>

## Part C — *by* (transport, makers, the passive)

### *by car / by bus / by plane / by bike*

- Do you like travelling **by train**?
- Jane usually goes to work **by bike**.

But for walking, English has its own phrase: **on foot**.

- You can't get there **by car**. You have to go **on foot**. *(= you have to walk)*

<Callout type="warning" title="*by* — but *in / on* with *the / my*">
  *by car / by bus / by bike* — no article, just the bare noun. <br />
  But: *in **the** car / on **the** bus / on **my** bike* (Unit 107). <br />
  Switch words when you add *the / my / his*.
</Callout>

### *a book by … / a painting by …*

**by** also names the **maker** of a book, song, or picture:

- Have you read any books **by Charles Dickens**?
- **Who** is that painting **by**? Picasso?

### *by* + the doer (passive)

After a passive verb, **by** says who did it:

- I was bitten **by a dog**.
- *Hamlet, Othello* and *Macbeth* are plays **by William Shakespeare**.
- I was arrested **by two policemen** and taken to the police station.

## Part D — *with / without*

**with** = together / having / using. **without** = not having.

| with / without | example |
|---|---|
| **with** + people | Did you stay at a hotel or **with friends**? |
| **without** + person | Wait for me. Please don't go **without me**. |
| **with / without** + thing | Do you like your coffee **with** or **without milk**? |
| **with** + tool | I cut the paper **with a pair of scissors**. |
| **with** + a feature | a man **with a beard** / a woman **with glasses** |

- Do you know that man **with the beard**?
- I'd like to have a house **with a big garden**.

<SayIt text="Coffee with milk, please." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="A man with a beard." />

## Part E — *about*

Use **about** for the **topic** of talking, thinking, hearing, or knowing:

- Some people **talk about their work** all the time.
- I don't **know** much **about cars**.
- There was **a programme about volcanoes** on TV last night. Did you see it?

The verbs that pull *about*: **talk / speak / think / hear / know / read / write / a book / a question / a programme / information** *about …*

<SayIt text="I don't know much about cars." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="A programme about volcanoes." />

## Now you try

Finish each sentence about your own life.

1. *I usually go to work / school **by** ___ .*
2. *I'd like to read a book **about** ___ .*
3. *I have a friend **with** ___ .*
4. *My favourite song is **by** ___ .*
5. *I drink my coffee **with / without** ___ .*

<Callout type="tip" title="One-sentence summary">
  **on** + holiday / TV / phone / fire / time. <br />
  **at** + a number (age, speed, temperature). <br />
  **by** + transport / a maker / the passive doer (but **on foot** for walking). <br />
  **with** + a tool / a feature / a person — **without** for the absence. <br />
  **about** for the **topic** of talking, thinking, reading, or hearing.
</Callout>
