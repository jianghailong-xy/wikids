Two ways to say *A is smaller than B*. The **than** way: *Athens is **older than** Rome*. The **not as … as** way: *Rome is **not as old as** Athens*. Same meaning, different angle. The trick: between the two *as* sits the **plain adjective** (no -er, no more) — *not as old as, not as expensive as, not as much as*. And one cousin lives next door: **the same as** for things that are equal.

<Callout type="tip" title="The shape">
  *not* + **as** + **adjective (plain form)** + **as** + the second thing. ✅ *not as old as*. ❌ *not as older as / not so older as*.
</Callout>

## Part A — *not as … as*

The adjective in the middle is **plain** — no -er, no more:

- **Rome is not as old as Athens.** *(= Athens is older)*
- **The Grand Hotel isn't as expensive as the Europa.** *(= the Europa is more expensive)*
- **I don't play tennis as often as you.** *(= you play more often)*
- **The weather is better today. It isn't as cold.** *(= as cold as it was yesterday)*

## Part B — *not as much as / not as many as*

For amounts, slip *much* (uncountable) or *many* (plural) between the two *as*:

- I **don't have as much money as you**. *(= you've got more money)*
- I **don't know as many people as you**. *(= you know more people)*
- I **don't go out as much as you**. *(= you go out more)*

## Part C — Compare *not as … as* and *than*

The two shapes carry the **same idea** from opposite sides:

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">not as … as</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">Rome is <strong>not as old as</strong> Athens.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Tennis <strong>isn't as popular as</strong> football.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">I <strong>don't go out as much as</strong> you.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">comparative + than</p>
    <p className="text-base text-violet-900">Athens is <strong>older than</strong> Rome. <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(not older as Rome)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">Football is <strong>more popular than</strong> tennis.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">You go out <strong>more than</strong> me.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="Don't mix the words">
  *as* goes with the **plain** adjective. *than* goes with the **comparative**. ❌ *older as Rome / not as older as*. ✅ *older **than** Rome*; *not **as old as** Rome*.
</Callout>

## Part D — pronouns after *as*

Like *than*, **object pronouns** are most common — but *as he is / as I do* is also fine:

- She's not as old **as him**. *or* She's not as old **as he is**.
- You don't work as hard **as me**. *or* You don't work as hard **as I do**.

## Part E — *the same as*

When two things are **equal** (same age, same colour, same time), use **the same as**:

- The weather today is **the same as** yesterday.
- My hair is **the same colour as** yours.
- I arrived at **the same time as** Tom.
- Andy is **the same age as** Laura.

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences using *not as … as* or *the same … as*.

1. *I'm not as ___ as my ___ .*
2. *Today isn't as ___ as yesterday.*
3. *I don't have as much ___ as ___ .*
4. *I don't know as many ___ as ___ .*
5. *My ___ is the same ___ as ___ .*
6. *I got up at the same time as ___ .*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — flip both ways">
  Pick three pairs from your life (you and your sister, your phone and your laptop, your old job and your new job). Write each comparison **twice** — once with *than* (*She is taller than me*), once with *not as … as* (*I am not as tall as her*). Same fact, two angles. Once you can flip on demand, both shapes feel automatic.
</Callout>
