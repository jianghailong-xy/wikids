You've spent four lessons on possessives — *my, mine, his, hers*. There's one more shape, and it's the most everyday of all: stick **'s** onto a person's name. *Kate's camera, my brother's car, the manager's office*. It's English's quickest way to say "this thing belongs to that person". For things and places, English flips the trick around and uses ***of*** instead. Today we learn when to do which.

## A family of cameras and cars

Everyone holds something — a camera, a car, an office. Listen for the **'s** on the owner's name.

<Callout type="tip" title="Shortest possessive in English">
  Just stick **'s** onto the owner's name. *Kate's camera = her camera. My brother's car = his car. The manager's office = his/her office.* No long phrase needed.
</Callout>

## Part A — *‑'s* for people

For **people** (and pets), use **owner's + thing**.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">✅ owner's + thing</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">I stayed at <strong>my sister's house</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Have you met <strong>Mr Black's wife</strong>?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Are you going to <strong>James's party</strong>?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Paul is <strong>a man's name</strong>. Paula is <strong>a woman's name</strong>.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-rose-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">❌ the long way (don't use it)</p>
    <p className="text-base text-rose-900 line-through">the house of my sister</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">the wife of Mr Black</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">the party of James</p>
  </div>
</div>

### *‑'s* without a noun after it

If the noun is obvious, you can drop it:

- Sophie's hair is longer than **Kate's**. *(= Kate's hair)*
- 'Whose umbrella is this?' 'It's **my mother's**.' *(= my mother's umbrella)*
- 'Where were you last night?' 'I was at **Paul's**.' *(= Paul's house)*

## Part B — *friend's* (one) vs *friends'* (two or more)

When the owner is **plural** and **already ends in ‑s** (*friends, students, parents*), just add an **apostrophe** at the end. No second *s*.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-700">one owner — 's</p>
    <p className="text-base text-violet-900">my <strong>friend's</strong> house <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(= one friend → his/her house)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">my <strong>mother's</strong> car <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(one mother)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-violet-900">my <strong>father's</strong> car <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(one father)</span></p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">two or more — '</p>
    <p className="text-base text-amber-900">my <strong>friends'</strong> house <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(= two or more friends → their house)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-amber-900">my <strong>parents'</strong> car <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(two parents)</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-amber-900">the <strong>students'</strong> answers <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(many students)</span></p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="info" title="Listen for the s">
  *friend's* and *friends'* sound exactly the same when you say them out loud — only the apostrophe tells you how many owners there are. Singular owner → **'s**. Plural owner already ending in *s* → **'**.
</Callout>

## Part C — *of …* for things and places

For **things** (a building, a car, a film) and **places**, English doesn't use *‑'s*. It flips it around and says **the X of Y**.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">✅ of for things / places</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">Look at the roof <strong>of that building</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">We didn't see the beginning <strong>of the film</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">What's the name <strong>of this village</strong>?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Madrid is the capital <strong>of Spain</strong>.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-rose-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">❌ don't 's a thing</p>
    <p className="text-base text-rose-900 line-through">that building's roof</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">the film's beginning</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">this village's name</p>
  </div>
</div>
