When you talk about **places where you do their main thing** (a school where you study, a hospital where you're being treated, a bed where you sleep), English drops the *the*. But when you go somewhere to **visit it as a place** — a cinema, a station, a doctor — the *the* slides back in. Today you sort the two families out.

## A day around town

Listen for **no the** with *work, school, home, bed* — and **the** with *the cinema, the bank, the doctor*.

<Callout type="tip" title="Two questions">
  *Do you go there to do its main thing?* (work to work, sleep to bed) → **no the**. *Do you go there to visit it as a place?* (a film, a stamp) → **the**.
</Callout>

## Part A — *no the* family

For these places, drop **the**. We say *(go) to + place*, *(be) at / in + place*.

    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-emerald-900">hospital / in hospital</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-emerald-900">prison / in prison</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-emerald-900">church / in/at church</p></div>

- Bye! I'm **going to work** now. *(not to the work)*
- I **finish work** at 5 o'clock every day.
- What did you learn **at school** today? *(not at the school)*
- Helen wants to **go to university** when she leaves school.
- Jack had an accident. He had to **go to hospital**.
- David usually **goes to church** on Sundays.
- I'm tired. I'm **going to bed**. *(not to the bed)*
- I'm **going home**. *(not to home — no preposition either!)* Are you **staying at home**?

## Part B — *the* family

For these, English wants **the**. We're treating each as **a place we visit**.

    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">the bank</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">the post office</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">the station</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">the airport</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">the city centre</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">the doctor / the dentist</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

- I never **go to the theatre**, but I **go to the cinema** a lot.
- 'Are you going to **the bank**?' 'No, to **the post office**.'
- The number 5 bus goes to **the airport**; the number 8 goes to **the city centre**.
- You're not well. Why don't you **go to the doctor**?
- I have to **go to the dentist** tomorrow.

## Now you try

Write **six** true sentences about your week.

1. *I go to ___ at … o'clock.* (work / school)
2. *I get home from ___ at … .*
3. *Last weekend I went to ___ .* (cinema / theatre / city centre)
4. *I usually go to ___ at … pm.* (bed)
5. *I'm staying ___ tonight.* (at home — no the!)
6. *I have to go to ___ tomorrow.* (the dentist / the doctor / the bank)

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — the matching pair">
  Write **two** sentences about *school*: one *no-the* (where you study) and one *with the* (the building you visit). Example: <br /><br />
  *Children **go to school** from the age of five. (no the — to study) <br /> The festival is **at the school** on Saturday. (the — visiting the building)*
</Callout>
