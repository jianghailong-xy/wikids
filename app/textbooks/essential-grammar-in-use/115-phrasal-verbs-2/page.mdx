In Unit 114 the phrasal verbs stood on their own. Today's family **takes an object** — and there's a tiny word-order rule you have to learn.

> **put on** your coat &nbsp; *or* &nbsp; **put** your coat **on**.

For a noun, **either order** is fine. But for a pronoun (*it / them / me / him*), there's only one allowed slot.

### The pronoun rule

A **noun** can sit on either side of *on / off*. A **pronoun** (*it / them / me / him / her*) **must sit between the verb and the particle**.

<Callout type="warning" title="put **it** on, never put on **it**">
  ✅ put **on** your coat &nbsp; / &nbsp; put your coat **on** &nbsp; / &nbsp; put **it on** <br />
  ❌ ~~put on it~~ <br />
  ✅ take **off** your shoes &nbsp; / &nbsp; take your shoes **off** &nbsp; / &nbsp; take **them off** <br />
  ❌ ~~take off them~~
</Callout>

## Part B — more separable phrasal verbs

The same rule applies to a whole family of useful pairs.

### *turn on / turn off* &nbsp; (lights, machines, taps)

- It was dark, so I **turned on the light**. *(or … turned the light on.)*
- I don't want to watch this programme. You can **turn it off**.

Same job: **switch on / switch off**.

- I **switched on** the light and **switched off** the television.

### *pick up / put down*

- Those are my keys on the floor. Can you **pick them up** for me?
- I stopped reading and **put my book down**. *(or … put down my book.)*

### *bring back / take back / give back / put back*

- You can take my umbrella, but please **bring it back**.
- I **took my new sweater back** to the shop. It was too small.
- I've got Rachel's keys. I have to **give them back** to her.
- I read the letter and then **put it back** in the envelope.

## Now you try

Finish each sentence about your own day.

1. *When I get home, I usually **take off** ___ .*
2. *When it gets dark, I **turn on** ___ .*
3. *I borrowed ___ from a friend. I have to **give it back**.*
4. *Yesterday I picked up ___ from the floor.*

<Callout type="tip" title="One-sentence summary">
  A separable phrasal verb (*put on / take off / turn on / turn off / pick up / put down / bring back / give back …*) takes an object. <br />
  **Noun** — either side: *put **on** your coat* / *put your coat **on***. <br />
  **Pronoun** (*it / them*) — only between verb and particle: *put **it** on*, never *put on it*.
</Callout>
