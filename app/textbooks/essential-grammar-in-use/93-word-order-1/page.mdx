Two simple word-order rules that fix dozens of beginner errors. **(1)** The **verb and its object stay together** — never slip another word between them: ✅ *Sue bought some new shoes yesterday.* ❌ *Sue bought yesterday some new shoes.* **(2)** When you have both **place** (*where?*) and **time** (*when?*), **place comes first**: ✅ *We went to a party last night.* ❌ *We went last night to a party.*

<Callout type="tip" title="Two locked rules">
  **(1)** verb + object are friends — nothing slides between them. **(2)** place (*where?*) before time (*when? / how long? / how often?*).
</Callout>

## Part A — verb + object together

The verb (*bought*) and its object (*some new shoes*) stand **side by side**. We say:

- Sue **bought some new shoes** yesterday. *(not Sue bought yesterday some new shoes)*

| | verb | object | (then anything else) |
|---|---|---|---|
| He | **speaks** | English | very well. *(not very well English)* |
| I | **like** | Italian food | very much. *(not I like very much …)* |
| Did you | **watch** | TV | all evening? *(not watch all evening TV)* |
| Paul often | **wears** | a black hat. | *(not Paul wears often …)* |
| We | **invited** | a lot of people | to the party. |
| I | **opened** | the door | slowly. |
| Why do you always | **make** | the same mistake? |  |
| I'm going to | **borrow** | some money | from a friend. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Don't split the pair">
  ❌ *He speaks very well English.* &nbsp; ✅ He **speaks English** very well. <br />
  ❌ *I drink every day three cups of coffee.* &nbsp; ✅ I **drink three cups of coffee** every day.
</Callout>

## Part B — place before time

When the same sentence has *where?* (place) and *when? / how long? / how often?* (time), the **place** comes **first**:

- We went **to a party last night**. *(not We went last night to a party)*

| | verb | place (where?) | time (when? how long? how often?) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lisa | walks | **to work** | every day. |
| Will you be |  | **at home** | this evening? |
| I usually | go | **to bed** | early. |
| We | arrived | **at the airport** | at 7 o'clock. |
| They've | lived | **in the same house** | for 20 years. |
| Joe's father has | been | **in hospital** | since June. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Time goes last">
  ❌ *Lisa walks every day to work.* &nbsp; ✅ Lisa **walks to work every day**. <br />
  ❌ *I'll be this evening at home.* &nbsp; ✅ I'll **be at home this evening**.
</Callout>

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences that use both rules.

1. *I ___ ___ very much.* (verb + object + adverb)
2. *We ___ to ___ last weekend.* (place + time)
3. *I drink ___ every morning.*
4. *They've lived in ___ for ___ .*
5. *I usually go to ___ early.*
6. *I want to ___ ___ fluently.*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — keep the pair, then place, then time">
  Three quick checks before you say a sentence:<br />
  ① **Verb-object pair** — anything between them? Move it out.<br />
  ② **Place** word — push it right after the object.<br />
  ③ **Time** word — push it to the end.<br />
  *I (yesterday) bought (in town) a coat.* → *I **bought a coat in town yesterday**.* Same words, perfect order.
</Callout>
