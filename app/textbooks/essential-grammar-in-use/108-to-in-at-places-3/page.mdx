Three more place-prepositions, but a brand-new split. The right one depends on **whether you're moving or stopped**.

> 🚌 We're going **to London** on Sunday. *(movement → to)* <br />
> 🏛️ Piccadilly Circus **is in London**. *(position → in)*

Movement verbs (*go, come, return, walk, drive*) take **to**. Stopped verbs (*be, stay, live, do something*) take **in / at**.

## Part A — *to* vs *in / at*

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-sky-200 bg-sky-50">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-sky-200 sm:grid-cols-2">
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">to — movement</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-sm text-sky-900"><strong>go / come / return / walk (etc.) to …</strong></p>
      <ul className="mt-3 space-y-1 text-sm text-slate-700">
        <li>• We're <strong>going to London</strong> on Sunday.</li>
        <li>• I want to <strong>go to Italy</strong> next year.</li>
        <li>• We <strong>walked</strong> from my house <strong>to the centre of town</strong>.</li>
        <li>• What time do you <strong>go to bed</strong>?</li>
        <li>• The bus is <strong>going to the airport</strong>.</li>
        <li>• Karen didn't <strong>go to work</strong> yesterday.</li>
        <li>• I <strong>went to a party</strong> last night.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">in / at — position</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-sm text-sky-900"><strong>be / stay / do something (etc.) in / at …</strong></p>
      <ul className="mt-3 space-y-1 text-sm text-slate-700">
        <li>• Piccadilly Circus <strong>is in London</strong>.</li>
        <li>• My brother <strong>lives in Italy</strong>.</li>
        <li>• The main shops <strong>are in the centre of town</strong>.</li>
        <li>• I like <strong>reading in bed</strong>.</li>
        <li>• The bus <strong>is at the airport</strong>.</li>
        <li>• Sarah <strong>wasn't at work</strong> yesterday.</li>
        <li>• I <strong>met</strong> a lot of people <strong>at the party</strong>.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="tip" title="Move vs stay">
  If the verb tells you somebody **goes / comes / drives / walks / returns** there → use **to**. <br />
  If the verb tells you somebody **is / lives / stays / waits / works** there → use **in / at** (Unit 106–107).
</Callout>

## Part B — *home* — the silent slot

**home** is special. With movement verbs, **never** add *to*. With stopped verbs, you say *at home*.

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-rose-50">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-rose-200 sm:grid-cols-2">
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">go / come / walk … home</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-sm italic text-rose-700">(without <strong>to</strong>)</p>
      <ul className="mt-3 space-y-1 text-sm text-slate-700">
        <li>• I'm tired. I'm <strong>going home</strong>.</li>
        <li>• Did you <strong>walk home</strong>?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">be / stay / do … at home</p>
      <ul className="mt-3 space-y-1 text-sm text-slate-700">
        <li>• I'm <strong>staying at home</strong> tonight.</li>
        <li>• Dan doesn't work in an office. He <strong>works at home</strong>.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warning" title="Never *to home*">
  ❌ *I'm going **to home**.* &nbsp; → ✅ *I'm going **home**.* <br />
  *home* drops the *to*. It's the only place-word that does this in everyday English.
</Callout>

## Part C — *arrive* and *get*

Two everyday verbs for "reaching a place" — and they each pick a different preposition.

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-emerald-200 sm:grid-cols-2">
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">arrive in</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-xs italic text-emerald-700">a country or town</p>
      <ul className="mt-3 space-y-1 text-sm text-slate-700">
        <li>• They <strong>arrived in this country</strong> last week.</li>
        <li>• <strong>arrive in Italy / arrive in Paris</strong></li>
      </ul>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-xs italic text-rose-600">not arrived <s>to</s> this country</p>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">arrive at</p>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-xs italic text-emerald-700">other places (a building, an event)</p>
      <ul className="mt-3 space-y-1 text-sm text-slate-700">
        <li>• What time did you <strong>arrive at the hotel</strong>?</li>
        <li>• <strong>arrive at the station / arrive at work</strong></li>
      </ul>
      <p className="mt-2 text-center text-xs italic text-rose-600">not arrive <s>to</s> the hotel</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

**get to** is the everyday alternative — used for **any** place:

- What time did you **get to the hotel**?
- What time did you **get to Paris**?

And the silent-slot rule from Part B applies again — **get home** and **arrive home** take **no preposition**:

- I was tired when I **got home**. *(or I **arrived home**.)*

<Callout type="info" title="The five-shape map">
  *arrive in* — country / town. <br />
  *arrive at* — building / event / specific place. <br />
  *get to* — any place. <br />
  *get home / arrive home* — no preposition. <br />
  *be in / at* — when you've stopped.
</Callout>

## Now you try

Finish each sentence about your own life. Pick the right shape — or skip the preposition for *home*.

1. *At 3 o'clock this morning I was **in / at** ___ .*
2. *Yesterday I went **to** ___ .*
3. *At 11 o'clock yesterday morning I was **at / in** ___ .*
4. *One day I'd like to go **to** ___ .*
5. *I don't like going **to** ___ .*
6. *At 9 o'clock yesterday evening I was **at / in** ___ .*

<Callout type="tip" title="One-sentence summary">
  **Movement → to** *(go to London, walk to the station, arrive at the hotel, get to Paris)*. <br />
  **Position → in / at** *(live in Italy, stay at home, wait at the bus stop)*. <br />
  *home* is the silent slot — never *to home*. <br />
  *arrive **in*** a country/town, *arrive **at*** a building/event, but *get **to*** any place.
</Callout>
