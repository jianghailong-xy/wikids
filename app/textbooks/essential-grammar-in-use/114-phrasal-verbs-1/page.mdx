A **phrasal verb** is a verb (*go / look / be …*) **+ a tiny direction word** (*in / out / up / down …*). Together they make a new meaning that often **isn't** what each word means alone.

> 🚪 I waited outside the shop. I didn't **go in**. <br />
> 🚲 Be careful! Don't **fall off**.

In Unit 114 the phrasal verbs **stand on their own** — no object after them. Just verb + direction.

## Part D — *away* / *off* &nbsp; vs &nbsp; *back*

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-amber-200 sm:grid-cols-2">
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">away / off</p>
      <ul className="mt-2 space-y-1 text-sm text-slate-700">
        <li>• The thief <strong>ran away</strong>. <em>(or … ran off)</em></li>
        <li>• Emma got into the car and <strong>drove away</strong>. <em>(or … drove off)</em></li>
        <li>• Tom has <strong>gone away</strong> for a few days. <em>(be / go away = in/to another place)</em></li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div className="bg-white p-4">
      <p className="text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">back</p>
      <ul className="mt-2 space-y-1 text-sm text-slate-700">
        <li>• Go away and don't <strong>come back</strong>!</li>
        <li>• We went out for dinner and then <strong>went back</strong> to our hotel.</li>
        <li>• Tom is away. He'll <strong>be back</strong> on Monday.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

Finish each sentence with one phrasal verb from this lesson.

1. *I usually **get up** at ___ .*

3. *When somebody calls my name, I **turn** ___ .*

<Callout type="tip" title="One-sentence summary">
  A phrasal verb = verb + a tiny direction word (*in / out / up / down / on / off / away / back / over / round*). The pair often has a new meaning (*get up = leave bed*, *fall off = drop from something you're on*, *go away = leave*). In Unit 114 the pair stands on its own, with no object behind it.
