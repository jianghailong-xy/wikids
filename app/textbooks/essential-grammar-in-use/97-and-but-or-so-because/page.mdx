Five tiny **conjunctions** — **and, but, or, so, because** — let you glue two short sentences into one longer one. Each glue word tells the reader something different about how the two halves are related:

- **and** — *both true* (one thing plus another)
- **but** — *contrast* (this, but the opposite is also true)
- **or** — *a choice* (this thing, or that thing?)
- **so** — *the result* (because of A, B happened)
- **because** — *the reason* (B happened because of A)

Same five words; five totally different jobs.

## How conjunctions work

A conjunction sits **between two sentences** and turns them into one:

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50">
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-amber-200 sm:grid-cols-3">
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">sentence A</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-amber-900">The car stopped.</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">glue word</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-amber-900">and</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">sentence B</p><p className="mt-1 text-sm text-amber-900">The driver got out.</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

→ *The car stopped **and** the driver got out.*

## #1 — *and* / *but* / *or*

These three live in the **same slot** between two sentences:

| Sentence A | + | Sentence B |
|---|---|---|
| We stayed at home | **and** | (we) watched television. |
| My sister is married | **and** | (she) lives in London. |
| He doesn't like her, | **and** | she doesn't like him. |
| I bought a sandwich, | **but** | I didn't eat it. |
| It's a nice house, | **but** | it doesn't have a garden. |
| Do you want to go out, | **or** | are you too tired? |

<Callout type="info" title="One little shortcut">
  When sentence B has the **same subject** as sentence A, you don't need to repeat it: *We stayed at home and (we) watched TV.* — *My sister is married and (she) lives in London.*
</Callout>

### Lists with *and*

In a list, the items are separated by **commas (,)** and **and** comes before the **last** item:

- I got home, had something to eat, sat down in an armchair **and** fell asleep.
- Karen is at work, Sue has gone shopping **and** Chris is playing football.

<SayIt text="I got home, had something to eat, sat down and fell asleep." />

## #2 — *so* (the result)

**so** introduces what **happened because of** sentence A. Sentence A is the cause; sentence B is the result.

| Sentence A *(cause)* | + | Sentence B *(result)* |
|---|---|---|
| It was very hot, | **so** | I opened the window. |
| Joe does a lot of sport, | **so** | he's very fit. |
| They don't like travelling, | **so** | they haven't been to many places. |

<SayIt text="It was very hot, so I opened the window." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="Joe does a lot of sport, so he's very fit." />

## #3 — *because* (the reason)

**because** flips the order: sentence A is the **result**, sentence B is the **reason**.

| Sentence A *(result)* | + | Sentence B *(reason)* |
|---|---|---|
| I opened the window | **because** | it was very hot. |
| Joe can't come to the party | **because** | he's going away. |
| Lisa is hungry | **because** | she didn't have breakfast. |

**Because** can also stand at the beginning — then a comma separates the two halves:

- **Because it was very hot,** I opened the window.
- **Because she didn't have breakfast,** Lisa is hungry.

<Callout type="tip" title="*so* vs *because* — same idea, opposite order">
  *It was hot, **so** I opened the window.* &nbsp;**=**&nbsp; *I opened the window **because** it was hot.* <br />
  Same two facts; the glue word decides which half comes first.
</Callout>

## #4 — more than one conjunction in a sentence

You can use **two** conjunctions in the same sentence to link three ideas:

- It was late **and** I was tired, **so** I went to bed.
- I always enjoy visiting London, **but** I wouldn't like to live there **because** it's too big.
- I bought a sandwich **and** an apple, **but** I only ate the apple.

## Now you try — six glue lines

Say a sentence out loud for each prompt about your real day.

1. *I ___ **and** ___ .* — two things you did one after the other.
2. *I wanted to ___ , **but** ___ .* — something that didn't quite work out.
3. *Shall we ___ , **or** ___ ?* — give a friend a choice.
4. *I was ___ , **so** I ___ .* — a feeling and the action it caused.
5. *I ___ **because** ___ .* — give a reason.
6. *It was ___ **and** I was ___ , **so** I ___ .* — a sentence with two conjunctions.

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — chain three ideas">
  Pick three things from today: a fact, a contrast, and a reason. <br />
  Glue them with **and / but / because**: <br />
  *I had coffee this morning **and** went to work, **but** I forgot my umbrella **because** I was in a hurry.* <br />
  One sentence, three conjunctions, and you've used the whole family.
</Callout>
