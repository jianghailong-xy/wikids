In English you can almost never say a singular thing on its own. **Bank** is not a sentence, and *Rachel works in bank* is broken. You need a tiny word in front: **a** or **an**. They both mean *one* — *one bank, one apple, one taxi*. The choice between **a** and **an** is decided by **the very next sound**: a consonant sound takes *a*, a vowel sound takes *an*. Today you also learn the second job of *a/an*: telling people **what something is** — including jobs.

## Three quick captions

A camera, a taxi, a beautiful day. Three different sentences — all kicked off by **a**.

<Callout type="tip" title="Read the next sound, not the next letter">
  ***a* hat** but ***an* umbrella**, ***an* hour** (the *h* is silent → vowel sound!), but ***a* university** (the *u* sounds like *yu* → consonant sound!). It's about the **sound**, not the spelling.
</Callout>

## Part A — *a/an = one*

Use **a** or **an** in front of a singular thing or person.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">✅ a / an + singular noun</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">Rachel works in <strong>a bank</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Can I ask <strong>a question</strong>?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">I don't have <strong>a job</strong> at the moment.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">There's <strong>a woman</strong> at the bus stop.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-rose-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">❌ no a/an</p>
    <p className="text-base text-rose-900 line-through">Rachel works in bank.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">Can I ask question?</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">There's woman at the bus stop.</p>

</div>

## Part B — *an* before a vowel sound

Use **an** in front of a noun that begins with **a, e, i, o, u** sound:

<div className="not-prose my-6 overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border-2 border-violet-200 bg-violet-50">
  <div className="bg-violet-200 px-4 py-2 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-900">an + a / e / i / o / u sound</div>
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-violet-200 sm:grid-cols-2 lg:grid-cols-5">
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">an apple</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">an egg</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">an idea</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">an office</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">an umbrella</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

- Do you want **an apple** or **a banana**?
- I'm going to buy **a hat** and **an umbrella**.
- There was **an interesting programme** on TV last night.

### The "trick the spelling" exceptions

Sound, not letter, decides. Two famous traps:

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-amber-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">silent h → vowel sound → an</p>
    <p className="text-base font-semibold text-amber-900"><strong>an</strong> hour <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(sounds like "our")</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base font-semibold text-amber-900"><strong>an</strong> honest answer</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-sky-200 bg-sky-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-sky-700">u → "yu" → consonant sound → a</p>
    <p className="text-base font-semibold text-sky-900"><strong>a</strong> university <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(sounds like "yuniversity")</span></p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base font-semibold text-sky-900"><strong>a</strong> European country</p>
  </div>
</div>

### *another = an + other*

It's just *an* glued to *other*, written as one word.

- Can I have **another cup of coffee**?
- That's **another problem** for tomorrow.

## Part C — *a/an* says *what something is*

The second main job of *a/an*: telling someone **what someone or something is**. Always use *a/an* with a job, role, or category.

    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">a/an = "is one of these"</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">The sun is <strong>a star</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Football is <strong>a game</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Dallas is <strong>a city in Texas</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">A mouse is <strong>an animal</strong>. It's <strong>a small animal</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">Joe is <strong>a very nice person</strong>.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-pink-200 bg-pink-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-pink-700">a/an for jobs</p>
    <p className="text-base text-pink-900">A: What's your job? B: I'm <strong>a dentist</strong>. <span className="text-xs italic text-slate-500">(not I'm dentist)</span></p>

    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-pink-900">Picasso was <strong>a famous painter</strong>.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-pink-900">Are you <strong>a student</strong>?</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences about your life and the world around you. Use *a/an* in each one.

1. *I'm ___ .* (your job or status)
2. *My best friend is ___ .* (their job)
3. *In my street there's ___ .*
4. *Today I bought ___ .*
5. *___ is ___ .* (a thing + its category, e.g., *A whale is a mammal*)
6. *I want to be ___ .* (a future job)

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — sound, not letter">
