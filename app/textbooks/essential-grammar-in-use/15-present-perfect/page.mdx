In Lesson 10 we travelled into the **past** with **was** and **were**. Today we learn a tense that does something clever: it talks about the past **and** the present at the same time. It's called the **present perfect**, and the trick is to ask one question — *"and what about now?"*

## The picture in three frames

Here's the idea in pictures. Something happened *before*. We can see the result *now*. The sentence in the middle ties the two together — that's the **present perfect**.

A second example, the same shape:

- **They are at home.** → **They are going out.** → **They have gone out.** *(= they're not at home now)*

<Callout type="info" title="The big idea in one line">
  Present perfect = an action **in the past** + a result we can still see **now**. *I've lost my keys* tells you both: I lost them **then**, and I don't have them **now**.
</Callout>

## The recipe: have / has + past participle

Every present perfect sentence is built from two pieces glued together:

<div className="not-prose my-6 rounded-2xl border-2 border-indigo-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-indigo-50 to-purple-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
  <div className="flex flex-wrap items-center justify-center gap-3 text-2xl font-bold sm:text-3xl">
    <span className="rounded-lg bg-white px-3 py-1 text-indigo-700">have / has</span>
    <span className="text-indigo-500">+</span>
    <span className="rounded-lg bg-indigo-600 px-3 py-1 text-white">past participle</span>
  </div>
  <p className="mt-3 text-center text-sm text-slate-600">
    helper word ↑ &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ↑ the verb in its &ldquo;done&rdquo; form
  </p>
</div>

The helper word changes with the subject — exactly like in Lesson 9:

| Subject | helper | + past participle | example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I / we / you / they** | **have** ('ve) | cleaned / lost / gone | I **have** cleaned. &nbsp; I**'ve** cleaned. |
| **he / she / it** | **has** ('s) | cleaned / lost / gone | She **has** cleaned. &nbsp; She**'s** cleaned. |

Tap to hear the short forms — they're what you'll hear in real conversation:

<SayIt text="I've cleaned my shoes." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="She's gone home." /> &nbsp; <SayIt text="We've finished." />

<Callout type="warning" title="'s = is OR has — context decides">
  In Lesson 1 you met **she's = she is**. Now **she's = she has** is also possible! <br />
  ✅ **She's tired.** *(= she is tired)* <br />
  ✅ **She's gone home.** *(= she has gone — there's a past participle after it)* <br />
  Look at the next word: if it's a past participle (**gone, lost, finished**…), the **'s** means **has**.
</Callout>

## The "past participle" — the verb in its done form

Every English verb has a special form for this tense. We call it the **past participle**. There are two families.

### Regular verbs: just add -ed

If you already know the past simple of these verbs, you already know the participle — they're **the same**: just **-ed**.

| base | past simple | past participle |
|---|---|---|
| clean | cleaned | **cleaned** |
| finish | finished | **finished** |
| start | started | **started** |
| decide | decided | **decided** |
| invite | invited | **invited** |

- I **have cleaned** my shoes.
- We **have finished** the report.
- She **has started** her new job.

### Irregular verbs: you have to learn each one

Some verbs refuse to take **-ed**. Sometimes the past simple and the participle look the same, sometimes they don't. Here are the ones from this lesson — meet them now and they'll feel like old friends later.

| base | past simple | past participle |
|---|---|---|
| buy | bought | **bought** |
| have | had | **had** |
| lose | lost | **lost** |
| tell | told | **told** |
| take | took | **taken** |
| break | broke | **broken** |
| see | saw | **seen** |
| fall | fell | **fallen** |
| forget | forgot | **forgotten** |
| go | went | **gone** |

<Callout type="tip" title="Same or different?">
  In the **buy / had / lost / told** group, past simple = past participle — that's the easy half. <br />
  In the **broken / seen / fallen / forgotten / gone** group, the participle is its **own** word. The trick: many participles end in **-en** or **-n**.
</Callout>

## Saying "no" — haven't / hasn't

To make the present perfect negative, stick **n't** on the helper word. Same trick you've used since Lesson 1.

      ],

A few real uses — notice how each negative is tied to a result *now*:

- It's Rachel's birthday tomorrow and I **haven't bought** her a present. *(= no present now)*
- 'Where are my glasses?' 'I don't know. I **haven't seen** them.' *(= no glasses found now)*
- I know that woman, but I **'ve forgotten** her name. *(= can't say it now)*
- Sam isn't here. He **hasn't arrived** yet. *(= still not here)*

## Two have's, one verb — don't get tangled

Lesson 9 taught you **have** for *owning* things: *I have a sister*. The same little word **have** is now also a helper for the present perfect. Same spelling, two completely different jobs.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-amber-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-amber-700">have = own / possess</p>
    <p className="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800">I <strong>have</strong> a car.</p>
    <p className="text-sm text-slate-600">No second verb after — just a thing.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-gradient-to-br from-emerald-50 to-teal-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">have = helper for present perfect</p>
    <p className="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800">I <strong>have bought</strong> a car.</p>
    <p className="text-sm text-slate-600">A past participle right after — an action with a result now.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="info" title="The next-word test">
  Look at the word **right after** *have* / *has*. If it's a noun (*a car, a sister*), it's the &ldquo;own&rdquo; meaning. If it's a past participle (*bought, gone, lost*), it's the present perfect.
</Callout>
