There's one more big *the*-rule, and it's the flip side of everything you've just learned. When you talk about something **in general** — *music, exams, cold weather, history, life* — you **drop the**. Add **the** back when you mean **a specific set you and the listener share**: *the music in this café, the weather today, the history of your country*. Same word, two completely different meanings.

<Callout type="tip" title="In general or this specific one?">
  Talking about **the type of thing** in general → no *the*. Talking about **this specific batch we both see** → *the*.
</Callout>

## Part A — No *the* with general ideas

Drop **the** when you mean *that thing in general*:

- I like **music**, especially **classical music**. *(not the music … the classical music)*
- We don't eat **meat** very often. *(not the meat)*
- **Life** is not possible without **water**. *(not The life … the water)*
- I hate **exams**. *(not the exams)*
- Is there a shop near here that sells **newspapers**?

### No *the* with games and sports

- My favourite sports are **football** and **skiing**. *(not the football … the skiing)*

### No *the* with languages and school subjects

- Do you think **English** is difficult? *(not the English)*
- Tom's brother is studying **physics** and **chemistry**.

## Part B — *flowers* vs *the flowers*?

The same word changes meaning when you add *the*.

  *I love flowers. The flowers in your garden are beautiful.*<br />
