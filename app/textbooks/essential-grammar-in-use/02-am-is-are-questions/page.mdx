## The big trick: just flip!

A statement and a question use the **same words** — only the **order** changes. Watch the subject and **am / is / are** swap places:

<WordFlip
  subject="You"
  verb="are"
  rest="a student"
  caption="You are a student. → Are you a student?"
/>

The real rule is super short:

<Callout type="info" title="The flip rule">
  **Statement:** subject + **am/is/are** + ... &nbsp;&nbsp; → &nbsp;&nbsp; **Question:** **am/is/are** + subject + ... ?
</Callout>

Look how every pronoun does the same flip:

| Statement | Question |
|---|---|
| **I am** late. | **Am I** late? |
| **He is** at home. | **Is he** at home? |
| **She is** a doctor. | **Is she** a doctor? |
| **It is** cold. | **Is it** cold? |
| **We are** ready. | **Are we** ready? |
| **You are** tired. | **Are you** tired? |
| **They are** new. | **Are they** new? |

Tap to hear a few:

## One word of warning: keep the rest in order

When the subject is more than one word (like **your mother** or **your shoes**), only the **verb** moves to the front. Everything else stays put.

<Callout type="warning" title="Don't break the sentence">
  ✅ **Is your mother** at home? &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ Is at home your mother? <br />
  ✅ **Are your shoes** new? &nbsp;&nbsp; ❌ Are new your shoes?
</Callout>

Think of it like this: grab **am / is / are** and lift it to the front. **Don't touch anything else.**

## Asking for information: Wh-questions

Sometimes "yes" or "no" isn't enough. We need details — *where? what? who? how? why?* Just put the **wh-word** before the flip:

<Callout type="info" title="The pattern">
  **Wh-word** + **am/is/are** + **subject** + ... ?
</Callout>

- **Where is** your mother? <SayIt text="Where is your mother?" iconOnly />
- **Where are** you from? <SayIt text="Where are you from?" iconOnly />
- **What colour is** your car? <SayIt text="What colour is your car?" iconOnly />
- **How old is** Joe? <SayIt text="How old is Joe?" iconOnly />
- **How are** your parents? <SayIt text="How are your parents?" iconOnly />
- **How much are** these shoes? <SayIt text="How much are these shoes?" iconOnly />
- **Why is** this hotel so expensive? <SayIt text="Why is this hotel so expensive?" iconOnly />

<Callout type="warning" title="Only with 'is'">
  We only shrink **wh-word + is** (one person/thing). With **are**, we keep them apart: ✅ *Where are you from?* &nbsp; ❌ *Where're you from?*
</Callout>

## Saying "yes" and "no" the short way

When someone asks a yes/no question, the polite answer is **not** just "yes" or "no" — we add the matching **am / is / are** too. It sounds friendlier and more complete.

| Question | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Are **you** tired? | **Yes, I am.** | **No, I'm not.** |
| Is **he** English? | **Yes, he is.** | **No, he isn't.** |
| Is **she** at home? | **Yes, she is.** | **No, she isn't.** |
| Is **it** cold? | **Yes, it is.** | **No, it isn't.** |
| Are **we** late? | **Yes, we are.** | **No, we aren't.** |
| Are **they** your keys? | **Yes, they are.** | **No, they aren't.** |

<Callout type="tip" title="Mirror, mirror">
  The verb in the answer **mirrors** the subject of the answer — not the question. *"Are **you** tired?"* → *"Yes, **I am**."* (you turns into I!)
</Callout>

Listen:

<SayIt text="Are you hungry? No, I'm not, but I'm thirsty." />

<Callout type="info" title="Two ways to say 'no'">
  Both are correct — pick whichever feels easier:<br />
  • **No, he isn't.** / **No, she isn't.** / **No, it isn't.** <br />
  • **No, he's not.** / **No, she's not.** / **No, it's not.**
</Callout>
