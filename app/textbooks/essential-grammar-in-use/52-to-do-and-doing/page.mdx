Lesson 51 gave you the **map**. Today we zoom in on the trickiest corner of it: when one verb leads into another. *I want* + ___? *I enjoy* + ___? Some verbs always demand **to + verb** (*I want to dance*). Others always demand **-ing** (*I enjoy dancing*). A third group accepts either — your choice. Today's lesson is mostly about **memorising which verb pulls which form** — and there's no shortcut, but the lists below cover almost everything you'll meet.

### Real sentences

- What do you **want to do** this evening?
- It's not very late. We don't **need to go** home yet.
- Tina has **decided to sell** her car.
- You **forgot to switch** off the light when you went out.
- My brother is **learning to drive**.
- I **tried to read** my book, but I was too tired.

## Part B — verbs + *-ing*

These verbs always want the **-ing** form after them.

  <div className="bg-violet-200 px-4 py-2 text-center text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-violet-900">verb + -ing</div>
  <div className="grid gap-px bg-violet-200 sm:grid-cols-2 lg:grid-cols-5">
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">enjoy</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">stop</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">finish</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">mind</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-violet-900">suggest</p></div>
  </div>
</div>

### Real sentences

- I **enjoy dancing**. *(not enjoy to dance)*
- I don't **mind getting** up early.
- Has it **stopped raining**?
- Sonia **suggested going** to the cinema.

<Callout type="warning" title="The most-broken rule in English">
  ❌ I enjoy <span className="line-through">to dance</span>. &nbsp;✅ I **enjoy dancing**.<br />
  ❌ He suggested <span className="line-through">to go</span>. &nbsp;✅ He **suggested going**.<br /><br />
  *Enjoy* and *suggest* don't even tolerate *to + verb* once. Lock these into memory: *enjoy + -ing, suggest + -ing*.
</Callout>

## Part C — verbs that take *either*

A handful of verbs are happy with **both**. Same meaning. Pick whichever feels nicer.

    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">hate</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">prefer</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">start</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">begin</p></div>
    <div className="bg-white p-3 text-center"><p className="font-semibold text-sky-900">continue</p></div>
  </div>

### Same meaning, two shapes

- Do you **like getting** up early? &nbsp;*or*&nbsp; Do you **like to get** up early?
- I **prefer travelling** by car. &nbsp;*or*&nbsp; I **prefer to travel** by car.
- Anna **loves dancing**. &nbsp;*or*&nbsp; Anna **loves to dance**.
- I **hate being** late. &nbsp;*or*&nbsp; I **hate to be** late.
- It **started raining**. &nbsp;*or*&nbsp; It **started to rain**.

## Part D — *would like to* (and friends)

But watch out: when *like / love / hate / prefer* sit after **would** (*'d like, 'd love, 'd hate, 'd prefer*), they ALWAYS want **to + verb**. No -ing version.

<div className="not-prose my-6 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-emerald-700">would + like/love/hate/prefer + to + verb</p>
    <p className="text-base text-emerald-900">Amy <strong>would like to meet</strong> you.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">I'<strong>d love to go</strong> to Australia.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">I'<strong>d prefer to stand</strong>, thank you.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-emerald-900">I live in a small village. I'<strong>d hate to live</strong> in a big city.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-2xl border-2 border-rose-200 bg-rose-50 p-5 shadow-sm">
    <p className="mb-2 text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider text-rose-700">❌ never -ing after would</p>
    <p className="text-base text-rose-900 line-through">I'd love going to Australia.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">I'd prefer standing.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-base text-rose-900 line-through">I'd hate living in a big city.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="info" title="Quick rule">
  Plain *like / love / hate / prefer* + (either form). <br />
  *Would* + *like / love / hate / prefer* + **to + verb only**.
</Callout>

## Now you try: about yourself

Finish each sentence with a true *to + verb* or *-ing* — whichever the trigger demands.
