One word, one meaning — *the right amount, no more no less*. But **enough** is fussy about where it stands. With a noun it goes **in front** (*enough money, enough time*). With an adjective or adverb it goes **after** (*tall enough, good enough*). Plus three follow-up shapes: *enough for somebody/something*, *enough to do something*, and *enough for somebody to do something* — note the **to + verb**, never *for + verb*.

<Callout type="tip" title="Two slots, one word">
  **enough + noun** (*enough money, enough time*). **adjective + enough** (*tall enough, big enough*). Never *money enough* or *enough tall*.
</Callout>

## Part A — *enough + noun*

In front of a noun, *enough* comes **first**:

- A: Is there **enough milk** in your coffee? B: Yes, thank you.
- We wanted to play football, but we didn't have **enough players**.
- Why don't you buy a car? You've got **enough money**. *(not money enough)*

### *enough* on its own (no noun)

You can also drop the noun once it's clear:

- I've got some money, but not **enough** to buy a car. *(= I need more money to buy a car)*
- A: Would you like some more to eat? B: No, thanks. I've had **enough**.
- You're always at home. You don't go out **enough**.

## Part B — *adjective + enough*

After an adjective (or adverb), *enough* comes **after**:

- A: Shall we sit outside? B: No, it isn't **warm enough**. *(not enough warm)*
- Can you hear the radio? Is it **loud enough** for you?
- Don't buy that coat. It's nice, but it isn't **long enough**. *(= it's too short)*

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch the slot">
  **enough money** ✅ &nbsp; *money enough* ❌ <br />
  **tall enough** ✅ &nbsp; *enough tall* ❌
</Callout>

### Compare the two slots

| enough + **noun** | **adjective** + enough |
|---|---|
| enough money | tall enough |
| enough time | good enough |
| enough people | old enough |

<Callout type="warning" title="to + verb, never for + verb">
  ❌ *enough money for buy a car.* &nbsp; ✅ *enough money **to buy** a car.* &nbsp; *for* takes a noun (*for a car*); *to* takes a verb (*to buy*).
</Callout>

## Now you try

Write **six** sentences with *enough*.

1. *I haven't got enough ___ .*
2. *I'm tall / strong / old / clever enough to ___ .*
3. *This room isn't big enough for ___ .*
4. *I don't have enough money to ___ .*
5. *Is your ___ ___ enough?*
6. *There aren't enough ___ for everybody to ___ .*

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — slot test">
  Read your gap sentence and ask: *what word comes next?* If it's a **noun** (money, time, people) → *enough* goes **before**. If it's an **adjective or adverb** (tall, big, well) → *enough* goes **after**. Then ask: *what comes after enough?* Thing → *for*. Action → *to + verb*. Three quick questions, never wrong.
</Callout>
