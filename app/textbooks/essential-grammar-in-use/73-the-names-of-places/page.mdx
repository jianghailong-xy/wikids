Place names break English's *the* rules in their own special way. *France* is fine on its own, but *the United States* always wants the. *Egypt* takes none, but *the Nile* always does. The trick is sorting place names into two camps: those that **never** take *the* (most countries, cities, streets, single mountains), and those that **always** do (republics, plural names, oceans/seas/rivers, *X of Y* names).

## Part B — Plural names of countries / islands / mountains → *the*

If the name **ends in -s** (a group), it takes *the*.

- **the Netherlands**, **the Philippines**, **the Canary Islands**, **the Alps**

## Part C — Oceans / seas / rivers / canals → *the*

- **the Atlantic (Ocean)**, **the Mediterranean (Sea)**, **the Black Sea**
- **the Amazon**, **the (River) Nile**, **the Suez Canal**

## Part E — *the …* of *…* names

If the name has **of** in it, use **the**.

- **the Museum of Modern Art**, **the University of California**, **the Great Wall of China**, **the Tower of London**

We also say **the north / the south / the east / the west (of …)**:

- I've been to **the north of Italy**, but not to **the south**.

## Now you try

Write **six** true sentences about places.

1. *I live in ___ .* (your country — usually no the)
2. *I'd like to visit ___ .* (a country / region with the)
3. *The longest river in ___ is ___ .*
4. *I've been to ___ .* (a famous building or museum with the)
5. *I went to ___ University.* (no the)
6. *I went to ___ .* (a hotel or theatre — with the)

<Callout type="tip" title="Star challenge — three quick rules">
  Memorise these three: *(1) plural country/island name → the (the Netherlands, the Alps); (2) ocean/river/canal → the (the Atlantic, the Nile, the Suez Canal); (3) name with* of *→ the (the University of California, the Tower of London).* If a name fails all three tests, default to **no the**.
</Callout>
