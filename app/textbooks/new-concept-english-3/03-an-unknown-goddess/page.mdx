## The Text 课文

Some time ago, an interesting discovery **was made** by archaeologists on the Aegean island of Kea. An American team explored a temple which stands in an ancient city on the promontory of Ayia Irini. The city at one time **must have been** prosperous, for it enjoyed a high level of civilization. Houses — often three storeys high — **were built** of stone. They had large rooms with beautifully decorated walls. The city **was even equipped** with a drainage system, for a great many clay pipes **were found** beneath the narrow streets.

The temple which the archaeologists explored **was used** as a place of worship from the fifteenth century B.C. until Roman times. In the most sacred room of the temple, clay fragments of fifteen statues **were found**. Each of these represented a goddess and had, at one time, **been painted**. The body of one statue **was found** among remains dating from the fifteenth century B.C. Its missing head happened to be among remains of the fifth century B.C. This head **must have been found** in Classical times and carefully preserved. It was very old and precious even then. When the archaeologists reconstructed the fragments, they were amazed to find that the goddess **turned out to be** a very modern-looking woman. She stood three feet high and her hands rested on her hips. She was wearing a full-length skirt which swept the ground. Despite her great age, she was very graceful indeed, but, so far, the archaeologists **have been unable** to discover her identity.

## 课文注释 Notes on the text

<Callout type="info" title="6 个值得停下来嚼一嚼的点">
  1. **must have + V-ed** (l.4, l.13) — 全课的题眼。对**过去事实**的**肯定推测**："（过去）一定……"。课文里两次出现：*must have been prosperous*（城市当年一定很繁荣）/ *must have been found*（脑袋一定是在古罗马时代就被发现了）。常配 *for / because / since* 给出推断依据。否定推测用 *can't have + V-ed*。
  2. **at one time** (l.4, l.11) — "曾经，一度"。相当于 *once*，但更书面。注意 l.11 *had, at one time, been painted* — 两个逗号把 *at one time* 当**插入语**插到 *had been painted* 中间，节奏更稳。
  3. **be used as ...** (l.9-10) — "被用作……"。是科技/历史文体的固定结构。**别和这两个长得像的搞混**：*used to + V*（过去常常做）/ *be used to + V-ing*（习惯于做）—— 三个 used 含义完全不同。
  4. **remains dating from the fifteenth century B.C.** (l.12) — *dating from ...* 是现在分词作定语，修饰 *remains*。整体相当于 *remains which date from ...*。**date from**（追溯到）是历史/考古写作必备短语；同义的 *date back to* 也可换用。
  5. **happen to + V** (l.12) — "碰巧、恰好"，**不是"发生"**。同型还有 *It happened that ... / If you happen to ... / Whatever happened to ...?*。课文里 *Its missing head happened to be ...* —— 头颅竟巧出现在更晚的遗迹里，正是这种"巧合"。
  6. **turn out to be ...** (l.15) — "结果是、原来是"。揭示意外的真相，比 *prove to be* 更口语。配合 *were amazed to find that ... turned out to be ...* 是地道的"意外揭晓"句型。
</Callout>

## 3. 关键句型 Past simple, past passive & *must have + V-ed*

<Callout type="tip" title="一分钟回顾">
  本课讲了一个**过去发生**的考古故事，但叙述方式比"我做了什么"复杂得多。值得偷学的有三层：

  - **A · 一般过去时 (Past Simple)**：讲述已经完成的动作。要点是不规则动词的过去式——*choose → chose / rise → rose / raise → raised / lay (put down) → laid / lie (recline) → lay / lie (tell a lie) → lied*。**lay/lie 是最容易混的一对**。

  - **B · 过去时被动 (Past Simple Passive)**：*was/were + 过去分词*。本课**几乎每一句都是被动**——*was made / were built / was used / were found / had been painted*。为什么？因为故事的主角是**文物**，不是考古学家。把文物当主语，被动语态把人隐去，文风显得客观。

  - **C · 过去推测 (must have + V-ed)**：对**过去事实**的肯定推测，"过去一定……"。
    *The city must have been prosperous.* （从证据推断的过去状态）
    *This head must have been found in Classical times.* （被动推测）
    否定推测用 *can't / couldn't have + V-ed*；可能推测用 *may / might have + V-ed*。
</Callout>

## 4. 难点：words often misused and confused 易混词

<Callout type="tip" title="3 组课文里直接出现的易混词">
  ### A. happen — "发生" vs. "碰巧"

  同一个词，两条用法，意思完全不同：

  - **happen = take place 发生**：*Tell me what happened. / What happened next?*
  - **happen to + V = by chance 碰巧**：*I happened to find the ticket in my pocket.* / *Its missing head happened to be among remains of the fifth century B.C.* (l.12) / *If you happen to see him, ...*
  - **It happens that ...** = It is by chance that ...：*It happened that I found the ticket in my pocket.*

  ### B. *storey* vs. *story*

  | 拼写 | 含义 | 例子 |
  |---|---|---|
  | **storey** /ˈstɔːri/ | 楼层（英式） | *Houses — often three **storeys** high — were built of stone.* (l.5) |
  | **story** /ˈstɔːri/ | 故事 / 美式"楼层" | *Read me a bedtime **story**.* / *(US)* *a three-**story** house* |

  发音完全一样，只在英式写作里区分拼写。复数：*storeys / stories*。

  ### C. *worship* vs. *warship*

  | 拼写 | 含义 | 例子 |
  |---|---|---|
  | **worship** /ˈwɜːʃɪp/ | 崇拜，礼拜 | *The temple ... was used as a place of **worship**.* (l.9-10) |
  | **warship** /ˈwɔːʃɪp/ | 军舰 | *The Arethusa used to be a **warship**.* |

  拼写只差一个字母，但发音、含义都不一样。听写最容易踩雷的一对。
</Callout>

The body of one statue lay among remains from the fifteenth century B.C., but its head turned up among fifth-century B.C. remains. ## 10. 作文 Composition (约 150 词扩写)

<Callout type="info" title="任务">
  Write an **imaginary account** of how the archaeologists explored the sacred room of the ancient temple. Expand the following skeleton into a paragraph of about **150 words**.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="提示骨架（每个省略号都得自己填）">
  - After walking round the ancient city, the archaeologists **...**
  - This temple **...**
  - On entering the sacred room, the archaeologists **...**
  - After this, workmen began digging and soon discovered **...**
  - They also found **...**
  - The archaeologists carefully **...**
  - They were astonished to find that **...**
  - The goddess was **...**
  - She **...**
  - Although the archaeologists **...** they were unable to find out her name.
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip" title="写作脚手架">
  - **时态主线**：一般过去时叙事；过去完成时 (*had been buried / had once been painted*) 表更早发生的事件；**被动语态**到处可用——文物是受动者。
  - **替代 see/find 的动词库**：*noticed, spotted, came across, uncovered, unearthed, exposed, brought to light*。
  - **画面动词**：*brushed away the dust, sifted the soil, fitted piece to piece, pieced together, swept aside, marvelled at*。
  - **结构上的小心机**：用 *On entering ... / After walking ...* 这种**分词短语开头**让段落有节奏感（参考 Lesson 1 的 *Armed with a torch, ...*）。
  - **结尾的张力**：最后那句 *they were unable to find out her name* 是悬念。可以加一句心理反应——*and yet she seemed to gaze back at them, silent and undisturbed*。
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip" title="参考范文（自己写完再看）">
  After walking round the ancient city, the archaeologists came at last to a small stone temple set high on the promontory. This temple, they decided, was the most important building of all. On entering the sacred room, the archaeologists noticed an uneven patch of earth in the centre of the floor. After this, workmen began digging and soon discovered the headless body of a clay statue. They also found, in a layer dating from a much later century, the missing head itself. The archaeologists carefully brushed away the dust and laid every fragment out on a long table. They were astonished to find that, once reassembled, the statue stood barely three feet high — yet looked startlingly modern. The goddess was painted, graceful, with her hands resting on her hips. She wore a long, sweeping skirt that brushed the ground. Although the archaeologists examined every inscription and pottery shard nearby, they were unable to find out her name. *(157 words)*
</Callout>

## 11. 书信写作 Letter writing

<Callout type="info" title="任务">
  Write **six phrases** which could be used to **end letters to friends**.
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip" title="6 个最常用的私信结尾句">
  | # | 结尾句 | 用在什么时候 |
  |---|---|---|
  | 1 | *I look forward to hearing from you soon.* | 等对方回信，最稳妥 |
  | 2 | *Do write back when you have the time.* | 不急的随意约稿 |
  | 3 | *Please give my love / regards to your family.* | 顺便问候对方家人 |
  | 4 | *Take care of yourself.* | 关心、温柔收尾 |
  | 5 | *Hope to see you again soon.* | 期待再见 |
  | 6 | *Once again, thank you for everything.* | 表达感谢后收笔 |
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip" title="结束语 (sign-off) 的强度排序">
  从正式到亲密：

  - **Yours faithfully** — 最正式，只在抬头是 *Dear Sir / Madam*（不知道收信人名字）时用
  - **Yours sincerely** — 正式 / 商务，知道对方名字时用
  - **Yours ever / Yours truly** — 半正式，朋友之间皆可
  - **Best wishes / All the best** — 朋友间最常用的中性收尾
  - **Love / Lots of love / xx** — 家人、密友、伴侣

  规则：**只大写第一个词**（*Yours sincerely*，不是 *Yours Sincerely*），后接逗号，再换行签名。
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="3 个最容易踩的雷">
  1. **Yours faithfully ≠ Yours sincerely**：抬头如果写了对方名字（*Dear John,*），结尾就要 *Yours sincerely*；抬头若是 *Dear Sir,* 则用 *Yours faithfully*。**对应错了会显得没读过基础礼仪**。
  2. **不要把"再见"翻译成 *Goodbye* 放在信末** —— 信件结尾不用 *Goodbye*，用 *sign-off + 名字*。
  3. **P.S. 写在签名下方**，左对齐，不再缩进。一般只用一条；超过两条说明你信里没讲清楚。
</Callout>

## 12. 拓展练习 Going further

<Callout type="tip" title="自学者建议">
  - **影子跟读 (shadowing)**：用第 1 节的三个 SayIt 按钮，跟读 3-5 遍。重点感受 *must have been prosperous* 里 *must have been* 的连读（听起来像 "musta been"）。
  - **复述 (retell)**：合上课文，用自己的话讲一遍这个考古故事（约 2 分钟）。卡壳的地方就是没掌握的句型。
  - **造句迁移**：用今天的 5 个高价值结构造你自己的句子——
    1. *S + must have + V-ed*（对过去的肯定推测）
    2. *S + must have been + V-ed*（对过去的被动推测）
    3. *S + happen to + V*（碰巧）
    4. *S + turn out to be ...*（结果是、原来是）
    5. *So far, S + have/has been + adj./V-ed*（迄今为止……）
  - **延伸思考**：课文里反复出现的"被动语态"+"层层时态嵌套"是**学术英语 (academic English)** 的典型风格。读论文、看 BBC 历史/科技报道时，几乎都是这种笔调。这一课你练的不只是语法点，而是一种**文体感**。
</Callout>
