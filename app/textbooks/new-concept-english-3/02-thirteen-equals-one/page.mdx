## The Text 课文

Our vicar is always raising money for one cause or another, but he has never managed to get enough money to have the church clock repaired. The big clock which used to strike the hours day and night was damaged many years ago and has been silent ever since.

One night, however, our vicar woke up with a start: the clock was striking the hours! Looking at his watch, he saw that it was one o'clock, but the bell struck thirteen times before it stopped. Armed with a torch, the vicar went up into the clock tower to see what was going on. In the torchlight, he caught sight of a figure whom he immediately recognized as Bill Wilkins, our local grocer.

'Whatever are you doing up here Bill?' asked the vicar in surprise.

'I'm trying to repair the bell,' answered Bill. 'I've been coming up here night after night for weeks now. You see, I was hoping to give you a surprise.'

'You certainly **did** give me a surprise!' said the vicar. 'You've probably woken up everyone in the village as well. Still, I'm glad the bell is working again.'

'That's the trouble, vicar,' answered Bill. 'It's working all right, but I'm afraid that at one o'clock it will strike thirteen times and there's nothing I can do about it.'

'We'll get used to that, Bill,' said the vicar. 'Thirteen is not as good as one, but it's better than nothing. Now let's go downstairs and have a cup of tea.'

## 课文注释 Notes on the text

<Callout type="info" title="6 个值得停下来嚼一嚼的点">
  1. **woke up with a start** (l.5) — start 这里是**名词**，意为"惊吓、猛地一动"。*with a start* 整体作状语，"猛地一惊"。冒号后那句 *the clock was striking the hours!* 正是对 start 的**具体说明**——为什么惊？因为本不该响的钟突然响了。
  2. **recognize sb. as sb.** (l.9) — "认出某人是某人"。注意 **as 后接名词或名词短语**（不是 that 从句）。同型：*identify A as B / mistake A for B*。
  3. **Whatever are you doing up here?** (l.10) — 疑问句里的 *whatever / whoever / whyever* 用来**加强语气**，相当于 *what on earth*，带惊讶、责备或好奇。**仍是疑问句词序**（are you doing，不是 you are doing），不要误当成宾语从句。
  4. **Armed with a torch** (l.7) — **过去分词短语作伴随状语**，逻辑主语是后面的 *the vicar*。比 *He held a torch and went up...* 紧凑得多。同类开头：*Dressed in black, ... / Surrounded by friends, ...*。
  5. **You certainly *did* give me a surprise!** (l.13) — 肯定句里用助动词 **do / does / did** 表示**强调**（朗读时要重读 did），意思是"确实、的确"。后面的实义动词**保持原形**（give，不能用 gave）。
  6. **get used to + 名词 / V-ing** (l.17) — "习惯于……"。这里的 **to 是介词**，不是不定式标志，所以后接 -ing 或名词。课文里 *get used to **that*** = 习惯于那（13 下钟声）。一定要和 *used to + V*（过去常常）区分清楚。
</Callout>

## 3. 关键句型 Present continuous vs. *always* + V-ing

<Callout type="tip" title="一分钟回顾">
  - **What is happening?** 用**现在进行时** *be + V-ing*，讲此刻正在发生的事。课文里到处都是：
    *'I'm trying to repair the bell.'* (l.11)
    *'It's working all right.'* (l.15)
    *'I'm glad the bell is working again.'* (l.14)

  - **What always happens?** 用**一般现在时**，讲反复发生、长期成立的事。
    *Bees make honey. / The sun rises in the east.*

  - **特殊用法：be always + V-ing** 用现在进行时配 *always / forever / constantly*，表示某人**老是、总是**做某事——常带一丝**抱怨、夸张或宠溺**的味道：
    *Our vicar is always raising money.* (l.1) —— 没完没了地筹款（带点苦笑）
    *He is always getting into trouble.* —— 老是惹麻烦
    *She is always writing letters.* —— 总在写信

  这是本课最值得偷学的高级用法：用进行时表"习惯"，比 *He always gets into trouble.* 多了一层情绪。
</Callout>

## 4. 难点：phrases with *in* 介词短语

<Callout type="tip" title="一句话讲透">
  英语里大量"动作/状态副词"是用 **in + 名词**的短语来表达的。这类短语不能逐字直译——*in surprise* 不是"在惊讶里"，而是"惊讶地"。课文里 *asked the vicar in surprise* (l.10) 就是典型用法。

  把下面这一组当作"现成的副词"来记，写作里直接拿来用：

  | 短语 | 含义 |
  |---|---|
  | **in surprise** | 惊讶地 |
  | **in a hurry** | 匆忙地，赶时间地 |
  | **in the end** | 最后，终于 |
  | **in sight** | 看得见，在视线内 |
  | **in common** | 共同的（have sth. in common）|
  | **in tears** | 含着泪，在哭 |
  | **in ink / in pencil** | 用墨水/铅笔（写） |
  | **in bed** | 在床上（卧床） |
  | **in danger** | 处于危险中 |
  | **in love** | 在恋爱中 |
  | **in debt** | 负债 |
  | **in difficulty** | 处于困境 |
  | **in all** | 总共 |
</Callout>

## 9. 摘要写作 Summary writing (≤ 80 词)

<Callout type="info" title="任务">
  In not more than **80 words**, describe what happened **from the moment the vicar woke up**. Do not include anything that is not in the passage.

  按下面 10 个提示点串成一段：

  1. What woke the vicar up?
  2. What was the time?
  3. How many times did the clock strike?
  4. Where did the vicar go?
  5. What did he take with him?
  6. Whom did he see in the clock tower?
  7. What did Bill Wilkins say he was trying to do?
  8. Had Bill Wilkins succeeded in repairing the clock or not?
  9. Was the vicar pleased or angry?
  10. What did he offer the grocer?
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip" title="参考范文（自己写完再看）">
  The vicar was woken at one o'clock by the church bell, which struck thirteen times. Armed with a torch, he climbed into the clock tower and caught sight of Bill Wilkins, the local grocer. Bill said he had been trying to repair the bell for weeks as a surprise. The clock was working, but it would strike thirteen at one o'clock — nothing could be done. The vicar, pleased rather than angry, offered him a cup of tea. *(80 words)*
</Callout>

## 10. 作文 Composition (约 200 词扩写)

<Callout type="info" title="任务">
  Write a composition of about **200 words** using the ideas given below. **Title: A sticky business.**

  - **Introduction:** A small village — the church clock suddenly stopped — no one could explain why.
  - **Development:** The vicar climbed into the clock tower — found that the clock had been invaded by bees — full of honey and wax.
  - **Conclusion:** A bee keeper was called — removed the queen bee — the other bees followed — the clock was cleaned — working again.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="写作脚手架">
  - **时态主线**：一般过去时叙事；用**过去完成时**（*had been invaded / had filled*）表更早发生的事；偶尔用**过去进行时**渲染画面（*was buzzing*）。
  - **承接钟与蜂蜜的隐喻**：sticky 既指字面"黏糊糊的（蜂蜜）"，又指比喻"棘手的事"——这是题目的双关。结尾若能呼应这层意思更佳。
  - **画面动词库**：*swarmed, buzzed, clung to, dripped, oozed, poured out, vibrated, sealed up, smoked out*。
  - **避免单调**："the vicar / the bee keeper / the clock / the bees"——用代词、同位语适度替换，避免重复。
  - **结尾的张力**：可以加一句轻微幽默——*and the vicar, scraping honey from his hands, said he had never had such a sweet repair bill*。
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip" title="参考范文（自己写完再看）">
  In a small village, the church clock had always struck the hours faithfully — until one summer morning, when it stopped without warning. The villagers were puzzled, and no one could explain why. The vicar, anxious to set things right, fetched a ladder and climbed into the clock tower himself.

  What he found astonished him. The clock had been invaded by a swarm of bees, which had quietly settled inside the mechanism over many weeks. The gears were sealed up with wax, and golden honey oozed from every crevice. The hands themselves were stuck fast.

  The vicar climbed down and sent at once for the local bee keeper. The old man came up with smoke and a wooden box, and after a careful search he found and removed the queen bee. As soon as the queen was lifted out, the other bees followed her in a thick, humming cloud. The tower fell silent. Two of the parishioners then spent the afternoon scraping out the wax and washing the gears clean.

  By evening, the clock was ticking again — and the vicar joked that he had never had such a sweet repair bill. *(196 words)*
</Callout>

## 11. 书信写作 Letter writing

<Callout type="info" title="任务">
  Write **six phrases** which could be used to **begin letters to friends**.
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip" title="6 个最常用的私信开头（按场合排序）">
  | # | 开场 | 用在什么时候 |
  |---|---|---|
  | 1 | *Thank you for your last letter.* | 对方先来信，你回信 |
  | 2 | *It was lovely to hear from you again.* | 久违来信，表示开心 |
  | 3 | *I have just received your letter and hasten to reply.* | 刚收到，正在回信（稍正式） |
  | 4 | *I'm sorry I haven't written for so long.* | 自己拖了很久才写 |
  | 5 | *I hope this letter finds you well.* | 主动写信问候，不针对来信 |
  | 6 | *I have some exciting news to share with you.* | 主动写信报喜/通知大事 |
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="3 个用法上的小心机">
  1. **称呼后**用**逗号**（*Dear John,*），不是冒号。冒号偏商务/美式正式。
  2. 这些开场白都是**完整句子**，第一句之后另起一行进入正文段，**不要堆在同一段**。
  3. 不要把 *Yours sincerely* 类的结束语放在开头——那是结尾用的。一封私人信件**开头**一般不放套语缩写。
</Callout>

## 12. 拓展练习 Going further

<Callout type="tip" title="自学者建议">
  - **影子跟读 (shadowing)**：用第 1 节的三个 SayIt 按钮，跟读 3-5 遍。重点感受 *Our vicar is always raising money* 里 *always raising* 的拖长感，和 *Thirteen is not as good as one, but it's better than nothing* 的 stop-and-go 节奏。
  - **复述 (retell)**：合上课文，用自己的话讲一遍这个故事（约 2 分钟）。卡壳的地方就是没掌握的句型。
  - **造句迁移**：用今天的 5 个高价值结构造你自己的句子——
    1. *be always + V-ing*（带情绪色彩地描述某人的习惯）
    2. *have sth. done*（请人做某事的被动）
    3. *... has been + adj. ever since*（从过去到现在持续的状态）
    4. *Armed/Dressed/Equipped with ..., S + V ...*（过去分词作伴随状语）
    5. *not as ... as ..., but ... than nothing*（聊胜于无的对比句）
  - **延伸阅读**：思考一下题目 *Thirteen equals one* 的幽默——它其实是在讲"约定俗成 / convention"：钟敲几下代表几点，并不是物理定律，只是大家**约定**好的。如果全村都习惯了 13 = 1，那就真的等于。这是一种很英国式的幽默。
</Callout>
